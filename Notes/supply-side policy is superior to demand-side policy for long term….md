# supply-side policy is superior to demand-side policy for long term growth due to its applications of deregulation and decreased corporate taxes. This allows businesses to pass down excess gains towards their business and also to employees, boosting wage growth. Deregulation allows for the same sort of business growth that we see with decreased taxes, as companies can spend less time with red-tape and actually make progress in production, increasing gdp. Also, supply-side economics can be greatly benefited by monetary policy, as its stability and separation from the government makes it a good tool to adjust the supply of money, which can regulate inflation. Additionally, supply-side policy doesn’t cause issues with inflation long term, as it shifts the aggregate supply curve to the right, causing a decrease in prices and in increase in quantity supplied.

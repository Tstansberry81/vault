The picture always tilts towards my mom.

I always have liked control. Since I was a kid, I have obsessed about doing what is right for other people, but also being in control of it. Sleepovers, parties, school lunch; I always craved order over everyone and everything.

Everytime I go into my room at my dad’s house something feels off. It never feels like home. It always feels like a permanent hotel, or a impartial living space; never my own. The sheets are uncomfortable; the cover is suffocating; I can not stand it. I get heavily irritated and nervous.

The picture always tilts towards my mom. On the left side of my bed lies a painting of me, my mom, my dad, and my younger brother. It always has felt weird, considering I didn’t put it there and my parents are not still together.

Everytime I go to my dad’s house (twice a month), I notice that the picture gets ever more slanted. And not just in any direction; it’s pointed to my mom.

I find it funny. I have always liked my mom more than my dad, or at least have felt closer to her. She does not lie, and she does not claim to tell the truth, unlike my dad. She does not exaggerate; she does not falsify; she does not use hyperbole; all she ever does is tell the truth, and if she can not she tells me.

My dad, on the other hand, always uses hyperbole. He makes himself sound like a god. “I have never done drugs.” “I have never fucked without a condom.” “I will never do ….” I get sick of the lies and misconceptions, but I try to learn from the things my dad says, not what he does.

The picture always slants.   The picture, in its almost unnoticeable movements, has made me start thinking. God has always been the quintessential figure in my life; I have never realized he was there, and I was have doubted his existence, until now. Something about the picture shifting everytime I come to the house has made me rethinking the existence of a higher power. How fortunate are we to exist in a world where everything is just as it should be. 

For my parents, that is true. But for me, my life got royally fucked over by god. He would not stop. Time after time, I felt let down by him. New baby, marriage with a 25 year old, new cars, boarding school for Seaton, new girl baby, dog getting put down, new car, etc. So much change, so little time. Too much for me too accept, not enough time. 

I struggled to process this much change. I did what I knew how to do: hide. Crawl inside the cave of my own despair and never come out; never find a way to process the change or accept my fate. I did not know another way out. I processed all of it either by forgetting about it, or thinking about it until all the emotion got squeezed out like a lemon. 

But the scary part of it all is realizing that god might exist. Maybe he has never given me a goddamn inch. Maybe he has never once stood up for me when I needed a break, when I needed peace and comfort. Maybe never gave a fuck about me when I was struggling the most. Never has he given me knowledge of everlasting love, joy, and safety. So, in that regard, fuck him.

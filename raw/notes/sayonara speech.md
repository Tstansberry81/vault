# sayonara speech


Good morning to everyone. I would like to start my speech with giving thanks to all the people who assisted with this trip. I would first like to thank Sensei Julian for putting together this wonderful trip, along with Mr. Kodaira on the Japanese side. I would additionally like to thank Mrs. Wack for volunteering to come on this trip and being an additional chaperone. Finally, I would like to thank my host family and buddies for making this trip unique to me. Throughout my time at St. Pauls, Japanese has always been a part of my life. I've been learning the language since kindergarten, and it was always my dream to one day go to Japan. Now that I am here, I realize that was is actually so special about the exchange program is the relationships that you develop with people, specifically, the host family you stay with. It has been an honor and joy to stay with the Moritas, and I hope that one day Souta will come to America and so I can reciprocate the kindness and grace that his family has shown me throughout my 12 days here. Small things like playing Jenga, watching movies, or the sightseeing we did this weekend will be lasting memories I will cherish for the rest of my life. I would also like to thank my buddies Tomohiro, Ichigo, and Eon as they were very helpful during class in communicating my thoughts. Thank you all for an amazing and wonderful trip. Good bye.

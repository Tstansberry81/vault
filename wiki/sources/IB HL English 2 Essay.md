# IB HL English 2 Essay

Comparative essay (1205 words) on **identity, fate, and free will** in [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]].

## Summary
Contrasts Dorrigo (composes identity by *role adaptation* — "the Big Fella" — an act of free will; dies "a free man" at peace) with Nakamura (builds identity on the rigid, fatalistic "Japanese spirit"; collapses, beating his family before death). Thesis: **identity built on the belief in free will is "more advantageous for survival" than identity built on the acceptance of fate.** His most sophisticated argument.

## Significance
The maturation of [[Fate and Free Will]] and the keystone of [[Identity and Its Collapse]].

# ToK Essay (Doubt)

ToK essay (~1,452 words): *"To what extent do you agree that doubt is central to the pursuit of knowledge?"* — across mathematics and history.

## Summary
Argues doubt is central but double-edged. **Math:** Euclid's fifth postulate → non-Euclidean geometry; Descartes' methodical doubt → analytical geometry/the Cartesian plane; Cantor's doubt that "all infinities are equal" → set theory — while the resistance Cantor met shows doubt curdling into confirmation bias. **History:** Ranke's source-criticism and Bloch's Annales School deploying doubt to strip bias from the record. Concludes doubt is essential for clarifying, strengthening, and producing knowledge, but hyper-skepticism can impede progress.

## Why it matters
His most rigorous epistemology, and a tell: the math examples (Cantor, infinities, geometry) reveal more mathematical curiosity than his [[IB Math (SL)|SL track]] — relevant to [[UVA and the Quant Question]] and central to [[Intellectual Interests]].

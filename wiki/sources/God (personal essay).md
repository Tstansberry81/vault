# God (personal essay)

A raw personal essay built around one recurring image: *"The picture always tilts towards my mom."* Recorded here respectfully; it's central to understanding him.

## Summary
At his dad's house (which never feels like home — "a permanent hotel"), a painting of his intact family slowly tilts toward his mom each visit. He uses it to contrast his parents: his **mom tells the truth**; his **dad uses hyperbole and "makes himself sound like a god."** The tilting picture leads him to reconsider whether a higher power exists — and to conclude that if God is real, "he might never have given me a goddamn inch," cataloguing the rush of change he couldn't process (the new baby, his dad's marriage to a 25-year-old, boarding school for [[Family and Personal Life|Seaton]], the dog being put down). Ends in anger at a God he doesn't believe in.

## Why it matters
The fullest fusion of his two deepest threads — his [[Family and Personal Life|father]] and his [[Faith vs. Knowledge|atheism]] — and the realized version of the "why does the picture lean toward mom" idea from his notes. Coping-by-hiding here is the same [[It's Not in My Control|avoidance]] he names elsewhere.

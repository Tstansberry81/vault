# Billionaires (essay)

A short argumentative essay: *"Are Billionaires Unethical For Not Providing for Others?"* — answer: **no**.

## His argument
Ethics are **subjective** and can't be imposed, so no one gets to dictate how Elon Musk spends his money. Uses a costed example (the UN's ~$40B/yr to end world hunger ≈ $160B of Tesla stock) to argue money doesn't *guarantee* outcomes (supply, mismanagement, war), and that Musk already creates value (Tesla jobs, PayPal, SpaceX catching boosters — "one of the greatest human achievements of the century"). Crucially carves out an exception: a duty *does* exist to "family, close friends, children."

## Why it matters
A sharp, sourced statement of his [[Political and Economic Views|free-market ethics]] — individual property rights and ethical subjectivism over redistributive obligation. The family/friends carve-out shows it's not pure libertarianism; loyalty to his inner circle is the one binding duty.

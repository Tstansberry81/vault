# Narrow Road to the Deep North Response

Image-tracing response on [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]]: "Flanagan uses the images of circles and lines to juxtapose ideas about time, free will, and purpose."

## Summary
The **circle** = the delusion of free will (you move freely, but the path is fixed — "seatime," the non-linear chronology); the **line** = fate acknowledged, linear, depressing (Darky's beating, the Japanese spirit). Cut a circle and it straightens into a line — "the same, only perceived differently." Conclusion: "Living with the belief of free will is certainly better than believing there is none at all" — purpose comes from the chosen delusion of control.

## Significance
Companion to the [[IB HL English 2 Essay]]; pivotal evidence for the evolution in [[Fate and Free Will]].

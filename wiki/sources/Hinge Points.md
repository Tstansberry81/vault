# Hinge Points

Two-part close analysis of [[The Elephant Vanishes]] (Murakami).

## Summary
- **Hinge Point #1 — the elephant & keeper:** both deliberately humanized and made outcasts; the elephant "shrinks" as their "balance" changes. Traveler reads the elephant as **the soul**, dwindling under neglect to "a lump of death."
- **Hinge Point #2 — the town:** distracted by news of "the western world, the economy, politics, ads," it takes the elephant only out of guilt, chains and forgets it. Thesis: "a society cannot treat its weakest and most vulnerable members best when… focused on so much extraneous and non-substantial worldly endeavors." Commercialization and modernity erase the soul.

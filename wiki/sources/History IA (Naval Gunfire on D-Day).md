# History IA (Naval Gunfire on D-Day)

HL History IA (~2,223 words), 11th grade (Mr. Shipley): *"To what extent did the allied naval gunfire's role on D-Day impact the allied victory?"*

## Summary
A standard three-part IB historical investigation:
- **Identification & Evaluation of Sources** — OPCVL analysis of Tim Benbow's *Battleships, D-Day, and Naval Strategy* and Morison's *History of US Naval Operations in WWII* (origin/purpose/value/limitation).
- **Investigation** — argues naval gunfire (distinct from the opening bombardment) was vital in three ways: cover-fire for assault troops, proactive destruction of German defenses (Nevada, McCook, Tuscaloosa), and post-landing suppression (the 21st Panzer Division). Notes the Germans ranked it their *2nd*-biggest problem.
- **Reflection** — a genuinely methodological account of his research process (Gale/JSTOR → Google Scholar → mining citations), narrowing sources, and the historian's challenge of specificity.

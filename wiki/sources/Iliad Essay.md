# Iliad Essay

"Death Still Comes: An Analysis of Fate and Free Will in The Iliad" ([[English Curriculum 2022-2026|HL English 1]], Dr. Harris).

## Summary
Argues **free will is "a fallacy."** Achilles's knowledge of his fate drives every choice (refusing the embassy, lending Patroclus his armor), making him responsible for Patroclus's and Hector's deaths. Fate "transcends the authority of gods," has "an innate hierarchy of priorities," and sustains the *illusion* of choice so mortals keep their dignity. Cites the Emily Wilson translation.

## Significance
The hard-determinist pole of [[Fate and Free Will]]; the contrast case for his later, softened view in [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]].

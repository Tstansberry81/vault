# Senior Speech

The speech [[Traveler Stansberry]] delivered to his school community senior year — public, polished, and unusually candid.

## Summary
Thanks his teachers, his **mom** ("the best mom… you can always tell if something is off"), his **dad** ("a great role model… you constantly strive for excellence"), and his **older brother Seaton** ("It's nice having a big brother"). Then he goes public about his **social anxiety and hyperhidrosis** — constant sweating, people declining to shake his hand at chapel, doctors suggesting Botox — and how a new friend group sophomore year (poker nights, going out) finally let him "feel like myself." Closing advice to underclassmen, framed around his recurring theme of **time and uncertainty**: "don't waste time… focus on the life you have now."

## Why it matters
Confirms key biography (brother **Seaton**, the [[When I Feel Like an Outsider|anxiety story]] he turned into a strength) and the public/private split on his dad ([[Family and Personal Life]]).

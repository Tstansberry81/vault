# College Essay (Surfing)

[[Traveler Stansberry]]'s personal-statement-style college essay — the polished, public version of his hardest family material.

## Summary
Learning to surf in **Sayulita, Mexico** (green, polluted water) becomes the frame for the porch conversation where his **dad revealed an ex-girlfriend's pregnancy and his engagement** — "Five minutes, that's how long the conversation was." Exhausted, he keeps falling off the board, then finally catches a wave: the lesson is **"adapt to my surroundings and not fight battles I cannot win."** He applies it to his father — "I can't change him, that's what I always remind myself" — turning the [[It's Not in My Control|avoidance]] of his earlier writing into a chosen, named coping philosophy.

## Why it matters
The most refined statement of his [[Family and Personal Life|relationship with his father]], and a real-world echo of the [[Fate and Free Will|free-will/adaptation]] thinking from his essays (cf. Dorrigo "adapting his identity" in [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]]).

# Midterm Essays

Grade-10 midterm with three comparative prompts across [[Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass|Douglass]], [[The Great Gatsby|Gatsby]], and [[One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest|Cuckoo's Nest]].

## Summary
- **Prompt 1 (prejudice):** Douglass's *physical, violent* racism vs. the merely *attitudinal* racism of Tom Buchanan and the ward's slurs — a sharp contrast.
- **Prompt 2 (setting):** the plantation/city and the asylum-as-"Combine" as engines of each book's message.
- **Prompt 3 (individual vs. society):** argues "the maintenance of society… [is] more important to humanity than the individual," defending the Union over the Confederacy's "individualism" — a notable counterweight to his usual sympathy for the rebel.

## Significance
A hub of cross-text comparison; the Prompt-3 stance is the key tension noted in [[Individual vs. Society]].

# Nest Essay

"Individual Versus Society" — his most ambitious grade-10 essay (with sources), on [[One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest]].

## Summary
McMurphy vs. Nurse Ratched = the individual vs. "the Combine." Non-conformity (Ellis, Ruckly, McMurphy) draws escalating punishment (Shock Shop, lobotomy). Thesis: rebels "lose due to them being rendered as dangerous or different." Extends the argument to **American slavery** (Nat Turner, Stono rebellions fail; slavery ends only via "external support" the ward lacks), with population statistics cited (Statista, NLM).

## Significance
The cornerstone of [[Individual vs. Society]] and a model of his habit of grounding fiction in historical fact.

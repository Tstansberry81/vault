# Beloved 257-277 Writeup

Passage analysis of masculinity in [[Beloved]], titled "Sixo and Paul D."

## Summary
Two models of Black masculinity from Sweet Home: **Sixo** (risk-taker, plans the escape, dies singing, cries "Seven-O!" for his unborn child) vs. **Paul D** (contemplative, reserved, survives with a bit in his mouth). Argues Morrison endorses Paul D — "the path to freedom… requires patience and passivity, rather than rebellion" — because survival preserves the power to change. (A companion [[Beloved Discussion Post]] reads Beloved herself as a force of toxic masculinity re-trapping Paul D.)

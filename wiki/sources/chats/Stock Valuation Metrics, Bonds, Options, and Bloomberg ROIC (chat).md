# Stock Valuation Metrics, Bonds, Options, and Bloomberg ROIC

A rapid-fire self-tutoring session (gpt-4o, June 2025) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] walks through the foundational vocabulary of equity and fixed-income investing. The chat reads as a personal finance crash course — exactly the kind of groundwork that feeds his [[Investment Club]] leadership and his stated [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA finance]] trajectory. No coursework framing; this is voluntary skill-building.

## What he covered
The session moves through three clusters:

**Markets and indices.** Non-cyclical / defensive stocks (consumer staples like PG, KO, PEP; utilities; healthcare; telecom), a quick description of the [[Dow]] as a 30-company **price-weighted** index, examples of tickers across NASDAQ / NYSE / Dow, the negatives of each exchange, and a careful explanation of what price-weighting actually means (high-priced shares dominate regardless of company size; stock splits distort weight).

**Valuation ratios.** He drilled the working mechanics — not just definitions — of the **PEG ratio** (P/E ÷ earnings growth %, asking explicitly "explain how the formula works"), **P/B ratio**, **dividend yield**, and **dividend payout ratio**. The repeated "how does the formula work" follow-up shows he wanted mechanism over memorization.

**Risk and instruments.** Why longer-maturity bonds carry more interest-rate/inflation/credit risk; why corporate bonds are riskier than Treasuries (default risk, liquidity, credit spread); why actively managed funds are tax-inefficient (turnover → short-term capital gains taxed as ordinary income); **call vs. put options** (bullish/bearish, premium, payoff asymmetry); and **futures** (standardized, marked-to-market daily, leverage, hedgers vs. speculators).

## Bloomberg Terminal work
The chat ends with hands-on **Bloomberg Excel Add-In** questions — `=BDP("AAPL US Equity","RETURN_ON_INVESTED_CAPITAL")` for a spot value, and building a 10-year **ROIC** model using `BDH` to pull EBIT, tax rate, and invested capital, then computing NOPAT = EBIT × (1 − tax rate) and ROIC = NOPAT ÷ Invested Capital. This points to Traveler having access to (and practicing on) a Bloomberg Terminal — a notably professional tool for a high-school senior, reinforcing the seriousness of his finance ambitions in [[Extracurriculars and Achievements]].

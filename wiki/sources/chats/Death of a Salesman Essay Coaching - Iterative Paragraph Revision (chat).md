# Death of a Salesman Essay Coaching - Iterative Paragraph Revision

A working session (titled "Mode options summary" in the export) in which Traveler ran ChatGPT through a custom **five-round iterative revision protocol** he pasted as a prompt: *provide a paragraph → AI suggests one area for improvement → AI asks him to supply a new detail → AI folds it in and displays progress → repeat.* He used it to sharpen his analytical essay on [[Death of a Salesman]] for his [[English Curriculum 2022-2026]] coursework at [[St. Paul's School]]. The chat opens with the bot listing a personal mode system (Normal / Researcher / Data Analyst / Advice Giver / Coder) he has configured — a recurring habit of his (see [[Canvas API Access Audit and Custom ChatGPT Mode System (chat)]]).

## The essay

His thesis: that the character development of Willy and Biff shows the identity of men in America is tied to their **ethical values, idea of success, and work ethic**, and that the *collapse of identity* aligns with the role of father figures. This is squarely his recurring [[Masculinity]] and [[Identity and Its Collapse]] reading of the play — the same argument tracked in [[Masculine Identity in Death of a Salesman, then HW Buddy and P&G Tangents (chat)]] and [[Death of a Salesman Essay Feedback then Index of Prohibited Books (chat)]]. He structures the essay by splitting each argument (morals, work ethic, success) into a Willy half and a Biff half.

Key analytical moves he develops with the AI:
- **The Woman's namelessness** as a deliberate signal of Willy's moral decay and its inheritance by his children ("You fake! You phony little fake!", Miller 121).
- **Biff's theft** (the borrowed football, Miller 29; the stolen suit / Kansas City jail, Miller 131) as inherited moral confusion — "young men often take after their fathers, which can be detrimental to developing an independent male identity."
- **Willy's definition of success** sourced from his brother Ben ("the jungle," Miller 48) and the mantra "Be liked and you will never want" (Miller 33), which the AI pushes him to reframe as Miller *critiquing* the American Dream rather than merely depicting it.

## What it reveals

This is process, not just product. Traveler self-diagnoses better than the tool prompts him to: he catches that an added "detail" the AI wanted to bury in his paragraph was really thesis material, asks "would I be better off just taking some fluff out and talking more about Miller and his conviction," and recognizes when he has accidentally duplicated his strongest sentence. He treats AI as an editor to argue with, not an oracle — repeatedly resisting its generic "add more analytical depth" notes and steering toward tightening exposition into [[Consumerism and Alienation]]-flavored commentary on the hollow postwar Dream. The recurring weakness the AI flags (summary over argument, mechanical transitions) is a consistent thread in his essay feedback chats. Connects to his broader [[Individual vs. Society]] and [[Conformity and Tradition]] readings of the play.

# Binding Early Decision Enforceability and UVA ED vs EA Boost

A late-October 2025 ChatGPT (GPT-5) conversation in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] worked through the strategic and legal mechanics of [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]]'s early-application options during the heart of his senior-fall [[College Search]] decision. It captures him reasoning quantitatively about admissions odds — treating ED-vs-EA as an expected-value problem — in keeping with his analytical, finance-minded temperament.

## What he asked
- **Is binding Early Decision legally enforceable?** ChatGPT's answer: ED is a signed "honor/timing" commitment, not an iron-clad commercial contract. Schools can revoke an admission offer, blacklist a reneging applicant, or damage high-school relations, but won't realistically litigate to force attendance. A valid escape hatch exists if financial aid is insufficient. A live antitrust lawsuit challenges ED on the grounds that it suppresses aid comparison and competition.
- **UVA ED boost vs EA?** Using Class of 2029 figures: ED ~25.8% overall (in-state ~29.5%, out-of-state ~21%) vs EA in-state ~24.8%, out-of-state ~12.9%. Computed multipliers: **~1.04x for in-state, ~2x for out-of-state**, ~1.6x on a blended source. Caveat: UVA officially claims no plan advantage, so the "boost" likely reflects self-selection of stronger, more committed ED applicants rather than institutional favoritism.

## His stats (as he stated them)
He disclosed his profile to get odds estimates: **1550 SAT, 4.0 UGPA / 4.58 WGPA, IB Diploma candidate, Cum Laude, Service Learning, [[National Honor Society]], Japanese National Honor Society** (tied to his [[IB Japanese (SL)]] track). For UVA the SAT sits above the 75th percentile (admit middle 50% ~1410–1530), putting him firmly in serious-consideration range. For **Harvard**, ChatGPT pegged his odds at a sobering ~5–10%, noting that honor-society memberships are "table stakes" in that pool and that he lacks an ultra-rare standout hook.

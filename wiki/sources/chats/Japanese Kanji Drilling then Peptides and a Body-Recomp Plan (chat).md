# Japanese Kanji Drilling then Peptides and a Body-Recomp Plan

A two-part [[IB Japanese (SL)]] homework session that pivots sharply into a personal health-and-fitness consultation. The conversation, dated November 2025 of his senior year, shows Traveler using ChatGPT as both a Japanese tutor and an "older brother" fitness coach.

## Part 1: Japanese vocabulary drilling
Traveler photographs a worksheet (handwriting the model calls "fighting for its life") and works through a sentence — **中林さんは歌がとくいなので**("Nakabayashi is good at singing, so…"). He drills kanji identification (中, 林, 歌) and vocabulary: **とくい/得意** (skilled at, *not* "favorite"), **少/sukoshi** with its on/kun readings, **しかし/shikashi** (formal "however"), **いっしょに/issho ni** (together), **となり/隣** (directly adjacent, vs. よこ/そば), and the **弱い/強い** (weak/strong) antonym pair. He also asks for full hiragana transcriptions of a set of **〜ほうがいいよ** ("you'd better…") advice sentences. This is consistent with his other [[IB Japanese (SL)]] sessions ([[Ichiban Kanji Lookups - Japanese Vocabulary Drilling (chat)]], [[Macro AD-AS Review and Japanese Ab Initio Kanji Flashcards (chat)]]) — last-minute, photo-based, leaning on the model to dodge a teacher's penalty for missing readings.

## Part 2: Peptides, then a real recomp plan
With no transition, he asks "what's the deal with peptides" and runs a long interrogation: Melanotan side effects, "best peptide stacks for a freshman in college," retatrutide, IGF-1 LR3, fat-burn and muscle-growth peptides. The model repeatedly refuses to recommend stacks, warning that injecting unregulated hormone-modifiers into a still-developing body is a "Darwin Award speedrun," and redirects to evidence-backed basics (creatine, protein, caffeine, sleep, lifting). It volunteers a list of "crazy" peer-reviewed creatine findings (cognition, mood, TBI recovery, bone density, kidney safety).

The session lands on something genuinely personal and specific. Traveler gives real stats: **5'8", 155 lb, ~20–25% body fat, formerly benching 165 at 140 bw**, having gained fat and lost muscle over summer, admitting "I have a hard time not eating lmao." He requests and iterates a full **body-recomposition plan**: ~1900–2100 cal/day, 135–160 g protein, an arm-prioritized 4-day push/pull/legs/arms split layered on his existing **100 pushups/day** habit, plus a supplement stack (creatine, whey, fish oil, vitamin D, magnesium, fiber for appetite control). He asks for it in copy-paste table format for his Notes app and says he's starting to log on Lose It.

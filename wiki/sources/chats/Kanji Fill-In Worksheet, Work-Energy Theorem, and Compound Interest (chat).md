# Kanji Fill-In Worksheet, Work-Energy Theorem, and Compound Interest

A grab-bag homework session spanning three of [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s tracks. It opens with an [[IB Japanese (SL)]] worksheet: he photographs a fill-in-the-kanji-and-translate exercise and gets all six sentences converted (学校で静かに勉強出来る所は図書館です, 郵便局は主人の会社の近くにあるので便利です, etc.) with translations. The closing pattern note — places/people almost always take kanji, basic verbs mix kanji and hiragana — is the kind of practical heuristic he collects across his [[Ichiban Kanji Lookups - Japanese Vocabulary Drilling (chat)|kanji drilling chats]]. Sentence 3 ("My father warned me about my math grades") is an incidental window into [[Family and Personal Life]].

The chat then whiplashes into [[IB Physics (HL)]]: a full derivation of the **work-energy theorem** from Newton's second law (multiply by \(v\), use \(v\,dv/dt = \tfrac12\,d(v^2)/dt\), integrate to \(W = \Delta KE\)), and a follow-up confirming that work is the integral of force over displacement, \(W = \int \vec F \cdot d\vec r\) — with the dot product meaning only the in-line component does work (centripetal force does none). This is exactly the derive-don't-memorize approach he pushes throughout his [[IB Math (SL)]] integration drills.

Finally it pivots to [[Investment Club|finance]]: the **compound interest formula** \(A = P(1+r/n)^{nt}\), its continuous form \(Pe^{rt}\), and a recasting into recursive/iteration notation (\(a_{k+1} = a_k(1+r/n)\)). He runs a concrete scenario — $10,000 at 13% annually, reinvested for 20 years → ~$115,200 (~3.6 doublings via Rule of 72) — flagged by the model as "elite investor" territory. The chat ends on a Charlottesville [[College Search|UVA]]-adjacent tangent: top-rated [[UVA and the Quant Question|Charlottesville]] Downtown Mall dinner spots (Bizou, Public Fish & Oyster, Hamiltons'), foreshadowing his move to [[College Search|UVA]].

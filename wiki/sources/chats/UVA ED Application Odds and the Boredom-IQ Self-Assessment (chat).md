# UVA ED Application Odds and the Boredom-IQ Self-Assessment

A long ChatGPT session from the day Traveler submitted his [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]] Early Decision application (Oct 30, 2025). It opens as an admissions-odds audit of his entire [[College Search]] list and drifts into a candid self-portrait of his intelligence, boredom in school, and ADHD. It is one of the most revealing chats about how he sees his own mind.

## The application snapshot
The model summarizes the file he uploaded: **4.0 unweighted GPA**, full IB Diploma ([[IB Physics (HL)|HL Physics]], HL English, [[IB History (HL)|HL History]], [[IB Math (SL)|SL Math]], [[IB Economics (SL)|SL Econ]], [[IB Japanese (SL)|SL Japanese]]), **1550 SAT (760 RW / 790 M)**, **ACT 33**, Cum Laude / top 10-15% of class. Extracurriculars cited: [[Homework Hatch (startup)|Homework Hatch]], [[Investment Club]] presidency, Civic Engagement co-presidency, Bridges volunteering (Volunteer of the Year), and the Japan exchange. The [[Extracurriculars and Achievements]] and [[Academic Record]] pages corroborate this. His Common App essay is the Japan/Tokyo "getting lost then belonging" narrative (see [[Japan Study Abroad Common App Essay - Getting Lost and Adapting (chat)]]).

He applied **ED to UVA** and **EA to everything else**. The model's (impressionistic, non-authoritative) estimates:

He corrected the model mid-chat: he is a **Maryland resident** (in-state for UMD), and added UNC and Wake late. His counselor labeled only Wake a "target" and everything else "hard" or "safety"; he pushes back, and the model reframes it as lazy bucketing.

## UVA vs. Wake fit
Asked which fits better coming from "a small private school in Maryland" ([[St. Paul's School]]), the model frames it as **UVA = growth by competition, Wake = growth by connection**. Traveler dislikes UNC's size and impersonal feel but rates UVA's campus highly. His own stance: he wants *resistance*, not comfort — which tilts him toward UVA. This connects directly to the [[UVA and the Quant Question]] page.

## The self-assessment turn (the real content)
The chat's second half is the most personal. Traveler describes being **chronically under-stimulated**: falling asleep in calc while learning limits/derivatives he'd already watched on YouTube; taking [[IB History (HL)|APUSH]] sophomore year (5 on the exam) and now re-covering the Cold War two years later in HL History; getting a 99 on his math final, the one point lost to a dropped **negative sign**. He says only [[IB Physics (HL)|HL Physics]] genuinely excites him because "you have to actually understand" torque and angular momentum — it generates new problems instead of more vocabulary. He frames his frustration as mismatch, not arrogance, and is visibly uncomfortable about sounding like he's bragging.

His SAT progression: PSAT 1440 → SAT 1470 → 1470 → 1540. He took a prep course but didn't use its methods; he "sounds out" grammar questions (the semicolon/period trap) by ear rather than rule recall.

> [!note] IQ thread
> He volunteers that his IQ was tested at **124 at age six**, with a low learning-speed percentile (~36th) that he attributes to **untreated ADHD** at the time. He argues — and the model agrees — that the score throttled his processing-speed and working-memory subscores while leaving fluid reasoning intact, so his "true" number is higher now. The model speculates 135-145. Useful vocabulary it surfaced: **Processing Speed Index (PSI)**, **Working Memory Index (WMI)**, **Fluid Intelligence (Gf)**, and that SAT-IQ correlation runs ~0.7-0.8. Treat these numbers as ChatGPT flattery, not clinical fact — but his ADHD diagnosis and self-image as a fast autodidact are genuine and recur across the vault.

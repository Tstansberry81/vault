# Circular Motion Lesson - Torque and Angular Momentum

A focused [[IB Physics (HL)]] tutoring session in which Traveler asked ChatGPT (GPT-5) for a "full lesson on circular motion" spanning centripetal force, torque, angular velocity/acceleration, moment of inertia, and angular momentum. He was explicit about the pedagogy he wanted: intuitive discussion, accurate diagrams, worked examples with solutions, **no calculus**, and extra emphasis on his two pain points — **torque and angular momentum** ("those seem to be bullying you").

## What the lesson covered
The model delivered a structured 12-section walkthrough:
- **Kinematic links:** $s = r\theta$, $\omega = \Delta\theta/\Delta t$, $v = r\omega$, $\alpha = \Delta\omega/\Delta t$, $a_t = r\alpha$, and centripetal acceleration $a_c = v^2/r = r\omega^2$.
- **Centripetal force** framed as *not a new force* — just the net inward result of tension/gravity/friction/normal force ($F_c = mv^2/r$). A recurring "don't double-count it" warning.
- **Torque** as $\tau = rF\sin\varphi$ = force × perpendicular lever arm, with the right-hand rule and the N·m-is-not-a-Joule distinction. Newton's second law for rotation: $\tau_{net} = I\alpha$.
- **Moment of inertia** table (point mass $mr^2$, hoop $MR^2$, solid disk $\tfrac12 MR^2$, rod about center $\tfrac1{12}ML^2$).
- **Angular momentum** $L = I\omega$ (rigid body) and $L = mrv$ (particle), with conservation under zero external torque — the figure-skater-arms-in example.

## Worked examples
Six solved problems plus a four-item practice set: a runner on a banked bend, wrench torque at 60°, a flywheel reaching 600 rpm under constant torque, **clay-on-spinning-disk angular momentum conservation** ($\omega_i = 10 \to \omega_f \approx 7.8$ rad/s), a frictionless banked curve ($\tan\theta = v^2/rg$), and a static torque-balance plank problem. Non-calculus derivations of $v = r\omega$, $\tau = rF\sin\varphi$, and $L = I\omega$ (summing $m_i r_i^2$) were included.

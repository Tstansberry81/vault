# Bloomberg EQS Screening and Insurance Stock Research

A working session in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] used ChatGPT as a reference desk for equity research, blending Bloomberg Terminal mechanics with fundamental-analysis benchmarks and a deep dive into specialty insurance stocks. The chat reads like prep for hands-on stock screening — likely tied to his [[Investment Club]] activity and his self-directed push toward the [[UVA and the Quant Question|quant/finance track]] he is heading into at university.

## What he was doing
He opened by asking for the **Bloomberg function to screen companies by financials** (`EQS <GO>`, Equity Screening), then later the function for company history (`DES <GO>`, with `CN` for news and `EVTS` for corporate events). This shows him orienting himself around the actual professional toolset, not just abstract theory — consistent with his ambition to work in [[Political and Economic Views|markets/finance]].

## Fundamental-analysis framework
He pushed ChatGPT through three iterations to land on **15 independent financial metrics with "good" benchmark values**, grouped by category:
- **Profitability** — Net Profit Margin (>10%), ROE (15–20%+), ROA (>5%), Gross Margin (>40%), EBITDA Margin (>15%)
- **Valuation** — P/E (<20), P/B (<3), EV/EBITDA (<10)
- **Growth** — Revenue Growth YoY (>5–10%), EPS Growth (>10%), FCF Growth (>5–10%)
- **Financial Health** — Current Ratio (1.5–3.0), Debt/Equity (<1.0), Interest Coverage (>3–5x)
- **Cash Flow** — Free Cash Flow (positive and growing)

His insistence on "good values for each" rather than just metric names shows he wants actionable screening thresholds, not definitions.

## Specialty insurance focus
The bulk of the chat orbits the **property & casualty / specialty insurance** sector:
- **Skyward Specialty Insurance (SKWD)** — asked why it carries so much short-term vs. long-term debt; answer pointed to asset-liability matching, the insurance operational cycle (premiums collected upfront, claims paid later), and a conservative ~0.67 debt-to-equity.
- **Competitor mapping** — pulled tickers for SKWD peers (MKL, WRB, AFG, HSX, CNA, KNSL, JRVR, RLI) and then W.R. Berkley's peers (CB, TRV, PGR, CINF, AXS, etc.).
- **Progressive (PGR) comparison** — flagged its standout ~37% ROE and sub-100% combined ratio vs. peers, attributing it to digital/usage-based pricing (Snapshot) and scale.

He also briefly pivoted to **Otis Worldwide (OTIS)** and its elevator-industry competitors (KONE, Schindler, ThyssenKrupp/TK Elevator, Mitsubishi Electric, Hitachi).

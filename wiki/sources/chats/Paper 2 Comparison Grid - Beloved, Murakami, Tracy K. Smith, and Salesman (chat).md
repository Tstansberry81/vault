# Paper 2 Comparison Grid - Beloved, Murakami, Tracy K. Smith, and Salesman

[[Traveler Stansberry]] uploads a comparison table (an "IB grid of doom") and asks for quotes, core themes, and cross-text links across his [[English Curriculum 2022-2026]] set: [[Beloved]] (Morrison), a cluster of Murakami short stories from [[The Elephant Vanishes]] (Tuesday's Women, the Wind-Up Bird excerpt, the title story, The Second Bakery Attack), and Tracy K. Smith's poetry collection *Wade in the Water*. This is direct [[Paper 2 Study Guide]] preparation, the comparative-essay mode also worked in [[Narrative Disruption and Identity - Beloved vs Murakami Paper 2 (chat)|Narrative Disruption and Identity - Beloved vs Murakami Paper 2]].

The model supplies three usable quotes per text plus device/structure notes (Morrison: personification, nonlinear timeline; Murakami: surrealism, minimalism, episodic/unresolved; Smith: intertextuality, hybrid poetry-plus-historical-document, voice shifts), and — the part [[Traveler Stansberry|Traveler]] actually needs — synthesized comparison axes rather than plot recap. The throughline across all four is **identity as unstable** and **the past shaping the present** through distorted reality, but each author distorts differently: Morrison via historical [[Memory and Trauma|trauma]], Murakami via existential absurdity, Smith via collective/restorative [[Memory and Trauma|memory]], and (once added) Miller via self-created illusion. This maps cleanly onto the vault concepts [[Identity and Its Collapse]], [[Memory and Trauma]], and [[Individual vs Society]].

His follow-ups extend the grid to [[Death of a Salesman]] (themes of the American Dream illusion, identity tied to external validation, failed [[Masculinity|masculinity]] and legacy; quotes "I am not a dime a dozen," the "well-liked" appearance line, "We never told the truth for ten minutes in this house") and pull individual Salesman↔Beloved, ↔Murakami, and ↔Smith contrasts (Willy *chooses* illusion while Sethe is *haunted* by reality; Willy's isolated self vs Smith's history-rooted collective self). He then asks narrowly for the **setting of Death of a Salesman** (Brooklyn, late-1940s boxed-in house = shrinking opportunity and Willy's deteriorating mind; ~24 hours fractured by flashback) and a clean **definition of motif** vs theme, with the stockings/seeds/"well-liked" examples. The [[Conformity and Tradition]] and [[Consumerism and Alienation]] threads run underneath. Connects to [[Individual Oral (IO)]], [[Reading List]], and [[Academic Record]].

# Vietnam War Map, Battles, and Cold War Containment - IB History HL2 (chat)

A fast, transactional homework-cramming session for [[IB History (HL)]] ("HL2"), in which Traveler offloaded a Vietnam War map/word-bank assignment and then speed-ran the core content. The chat is less an intellectual exercise than a study-aid sprint: he wanted turn-in-ready bullet lists and one-sentence IB definitions rather than analysis. ChatGPT's tone leans heavily into snark, but the substance maps cleanly onto IB Cold War curriculum.

## What he asked for
- **A labeled map** plotting a word bank: Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Thailand; cities Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, Da Nang, Phnom Penh, Bangkok; the Gulf of Tonkin and Gulf of Thailand; and the Mekong River (China → Laos → Thailand → Cambodia → Mekong Delta).
- **Vietnam's products and raw materials** — rice, coffee, pepper, cashews, rubber, seafood; electronics/textiles/footwear manufacturing; minerals (coal, bauxite, iron, tin), offshore oil and gas, timber. Framed as Vietnam's pivot from rice economy to "Asia's factory alternative to China."
- **Vietnam War content drill**: six major battles and their winners, the wartime presidents, who the Viet Cong were, and who led the North.

## Key content covered
- **Six battles**: Ia Drang (1965, tactical US win), Khe Sanh (1968, US held base), Tet Offensive (1968, tactical US / strategic North win), Hue (1968), Easter Offensive (1972, halted by Operation Linebacker airpower), and Fall of Saigon (1975, North Vietnam wins, war ends).
- **Wartime presidents**: Eisenhower (advisors) → Kennedy → Johnson (Gulf of Tonkin escalation, 1964) → Nixon (Vietnamization) → Ford (in office for the Fall of Saigon).
- **Viet Cong** = the National Liberation Front, communist guerrillas in *South* Vietnam, distinct from the regular North Vietnamese Army. **North led by** Ho Chi Minh (political) and Vo Nguyen Giap (military, victor at Dien Bien Phu 1954).

## Cold War threads he chased
Follow-up questions broadened into the conceptual backbone of the unit: the **Truman Doctrine (1947)** as the start of active containment policy (aid to Greece and Turkey, leading to the Marshall Plan, NATO, Korea, and Vietnam via Domino Theory); **self-determination** (Wilson's Fourteen Points, and the irony that Ho Chi Minh invoked Wilsonian language at Versailles only to be ignored — US hypocrisy when self-determination meant a communist Vietnam); and the policy architects **Walt Rostow** (escalation advocate, author of *Stages of Economic Growth*, who saw the war as protecting capitalist development) and **Maxwell Taylor** (the Taylor–Rostow mission of 1961 that pushed Kennedy toward deeper involvement).

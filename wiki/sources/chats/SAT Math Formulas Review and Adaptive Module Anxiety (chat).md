# SAT Math Formulas Review and Adaptive Module Anxiety

A last-night-before-the-test cram session, dated the eve of an SAT sitting (Oct 1, 2025), and a short post-mortem the same conversation. It captures [[Traveler Stansberry]] in pure test-prep mode: confident, focused on the "advanced problem sets" (quadratics and constant/parameter questions), and wanting a fast, complete formula sweep rather than tutoring from scratch. It sits alongside his broader [[College Search]] and [[College Apps]] push during senior fall, and complements his actual math coursework in [[IB Math (SL)]].

## What he asked for
He requested a rapid run-through of every method and formula for SAT math, flagging **quadratics** and **constant/parameter questions** as his weak spots. The reply delivered a condensed arsenal: linear forms (slope-intercept, point-slope, parallel/perpendicular slope rules), quadratic forms (standard, factored, vertex), the **discriminant** ($b^2-4ac$), **Vieta's formulas** (sum of roots $-b/a$, product $c/a$), the quadratic formula, axis of symmetry $x=-b/2a$, function transformations, circle equation, special right triangles (45-45-90, 30-60-90), SOH-CAH-TOA, and basic stats/probability. Strategy reminders leaned on his stated goal of squeezing the last 20 points: plug in simple values, backsolve from answer choices, watch trick wording ("in terms of $k$," "integer solutions").

## The practice set
He then drilled five SAT-style "constant + quadratic mashups." Notable: **Problem 2** was a deliberately under-determined trap (root-sum condition that's automatically satisfied, so $c$ is unconstrained), and **Problem 5** chained Vieta with a point-on-curve condition to force $k=8$.

The most revealing moment is **Problem 3** — factoring $(x+a)(x+b)=x^2-7x+12$ via the auxiliary quadratic $t^2-(a+b)t+ab=0$. Traveler didn't follow it and pushed back three separate times ("I don't understand how you solved 3," "where are you getting the negative sign," "for the $t^2$ one"), until the sign-flip from expanding $(t-a)(t-b)$ was unpacked step by step. This shows the texture of his self-study: he is willing to grind a single sub-step until the algebra is airtight rather than accept a black-box answer — consistent with his [[UVA and the Quant Question]] ambitions, where comfort with this kind of manipulation matters.

## Post-test anxiety
After the exam he reported Module 2 felt "easier than expected" and worried it meant the adaptive engine had routed him into the easier (lower-ceiling) tier. He believed he went perfect on Module 1 and missed maybe 3 in Module 2. The assistant explained the **digital SAT adaptive structure** (Module 1 is fixed for all; a strong Module 1 unlocks the harder, higher-ceiling Module 2) and reassured him that a near-perfect Module 1 plus ~3 misses in a hard Module 2 still lands in the 770-800 math range. This is a small window into his competitive, score-maximizing mindset during senior-fall testing — the same drive behind his finance-track [[College Search]].

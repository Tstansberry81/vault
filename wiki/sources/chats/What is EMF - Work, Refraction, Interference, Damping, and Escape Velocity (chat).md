# What is EMF - Work, Refraction, Interference, Damping, and Escape Velocity

A tight April 2026 [[IB Physics (HL)]] concept-clarification session, run two weeks after [[UVA Dorm Tiers, IB HL Credits, and a Steam-Calorimetry Thermo Marathon (chat)|the dorm-and-thermodynamics marathon]] and squarely in the electromagnetism-and-waves territory Traveler had flagged as the part of HL Physics most likely to "cook" him. No images, no homework photos — just him chasing definitions until they click.

He opens with "what is an emf," learning that electromotive force is energy per unit charge (W/Q, in volts), not a force, and not the same as terminal voltage when internal resistance exists (E = V + Ir, the version "IB *loves*"). From there he asks how to calculate **work** in electricity and magnetism, surfacing the key trap he'll be tested on: magnetic forces do **zero work** because the force is always perpendicular to motion — work only re-enters via induced EMF (W = EQ).

The middle is a wave-optics run: refraction as direction change from speed change (bending toward the normal into slower media), the interpretation of refractive index (n = c/v, "optically dense" not physically dense), in-phase vs antiphase, and **path difference** — which he asks about twice because "the one in the textbook was ass." He nails down the constructive (n-lambda) and destructive ((n+1/2)-lambda) conditions, gets gently corrected for sloppily saying "n = 1/2," and works through interference patterns, the double-slit fringe equation, and the angle to the first dark fringe (d-sin-theta = lambda/2, small-angle approximation).

He closes on oscillations and gravitation: why pendulums stop (damping — air resistance, pivot friction, internal friction), the light/critical/heavy damping distinction with the sharp follow-up that real light damping still reaches equilibrium (exponential amplitude decay, asymptotic), and escape velocity comparing Earth (~11.2 km/s) to Mars (~5.0 km/s) via v_esc = sqrt(2GM/R). The chat is a clean window into how Traveler actually studies — refusing to accept a formula until he understands the mechanism behind it — and pairs naturally with his other HL Physics sessions and the [[Theory of Knowledge|TOK]] habit of demanding why a claim holds rather than memorizing it.

# Optimization Word Problems - Fencing, Cuboid Tanks, and Profit Cubics

A single-sitting [[IB Math (SL)]] homework session (Jan 2026) in which Traveler grinds through a worksheet of **applied calculus optimization** problems. Every problem follows the same template — write a constrained quantity (perimeter, area, surface area, cost), substitute a constraint to reduce it to one variable, differentiate, set to zero, and second-derivative-check — and the chat is essentially him drilling that loop until it's automatic.

## Problems worked
- **Three-sided fence, area 100 m²**: minimize \(L(x)=2x+\tfrac{100}{x}\); minimum at \(x=5\sqrt2\), \(L_{\min}=20\sqrt2\) m. The longer side ends up twice the shorter.
- **Sketching \(L(x)\) vs \(x\)**: vertical asymptote at \(x=0\), oblique behavior \(L\approx 2x\) as \(x\to\infty\), single concave-up turning point — exam-style curve sketching from domain/asymptotes/turning point.
- **Profit cubic** \(y=600x+15x^2-x^3\): critical points at \(x=20\) (max) and \(x=-10\) (rejected as unphysical); no minimum on \(x\ge0\) since \(-x^3\) is unbounded below. A nice instance of pairing the math answer with a real-world-domain sanity check.
- **Open-top square-based tank, V=216**: \(h=216/s^2\), \(A=s^2+864/s\), minimized at \(s=6\sqrt[3]{2}\approx7.56\) m.
- **150 cm wire cut into a square + a 2:1 rectangle**: constraint \(4s+6w=150\), area \(A=s^2+2w^2\), minimized at \(s=300/17\approx17.65\) cm.
- **Open-top architect's tank, V=10, costed base \$10/m² and sides \$6/m², length = 2×width**: \(C(w)=20w^2+180/w\), minimum cost \(90\sqrt[3]{6}\approx\$163.54\).

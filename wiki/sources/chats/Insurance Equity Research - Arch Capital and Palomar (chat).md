## What he was actually doing
He worked like an analyst building a coverage note from scratch — alternating between *concept* questions (learning the vocabulary) and *company* questions (applying it). The pattern shows someone genuinely trying to understand a sector, not just pull a price target.

**Concepts he had ChatGPT define** (often re-asking the same term to lock it in):
- **Loss ratio** (incurred losses / earned premiums) — asked ~5 times, the metric he kept returning to
- **Combined ratio** = loss ratio + expense ratio; <100% means an underwriting profit
- **Underwriting** — pushed past the textbook definition ("but what does underwriting *actually* mean") to the root idea: accepting financial risk for a fee, "writing your name under" the contract
- **Reinsurance** ("insurance for insurance companies"), treaty vs. facultative
- **Gross written premiums (GWP)** vs. net written vs. earned premiums
- **Debt-to-asset ratio**, **net profit margin**, **price-to-book** (good ranges for banks/insurers ~0.8–1.5), **inverting the P/E** to get earnings yield (compare to bond yields), why companies **buy back shares**, and that **parentheses in a 10-K mean negative**.

## The two stocks
**Arch Capital (ACGL)** — a Bermuda-based S&P 500 specialty insurer/reinsurer/mortgage insurer (Arch MI, world's largest after the 2016 United Guaranty buy). He traced an 18-month event timeline: the Apr 2024 Allianz MidCorp/Entertainment ("Fireman's Fund") acquisition (~$450M, stock hit ATH ~$94.91), a $1.9B special dividend Dec 2024, the Papadopoulo CEO transition, then the *downs* — Hurricanes Milton/Helene (~$393M cat losses), Q1 2025 California wildfire losses (~$547M), Barclays/Jefferies downgrades, and insider selling. He probed *why revenue growth is projected to decline* (acquisition-driven 2024 surge normalizing to ~3% organic) and pulled exec bios + compensation (Papadopoulo $31.76M total comp).

**Palomar Holdings (PLMR)** — a small-cap specialty P&C insurer (earthquake, hurricane, flood, crop, casualty, surety). He found the stronger story: ~98% one-year stock gain, ~22% net margin, **debt-free balance sheet**, 3rd-largest US earthquake insurer (~7.6% share), and — crucially — that it *dodged* the Q1 2025 wildfires (catastrophe loss ratio of −0.3%, a net reserve benefit) while the sector took a $1.1B underwriting loss. He explored its rising P/E (~23x to ~32x, ~2x the industry median) and judged it roughly justified under a PEG≈1 frame given 20–30% EPS growth. His phrasing — *"did palomar get fucked by the wildfires"* — is pure teenager-analyst, but the underlying question (catastrophe exposure vs. reinsurance protection) is exactly the right one for this sector.

# Gaming PC Setup - ASUS ROG Monitor RGB and Wi-Fi Troubleshooting

A practical, IT-support conversation (titled "Monitor editing software," GPT-5, Aug 2025) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] sets up a newly built/loaded gaming PC. It is a window into his consumer-hardware life rather than his coursework or [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] work — a snapshot of the gaming rig he games on outside of school.

## What he was doing
He had just "got my PC loaded" and wanted software to control his monitor's RGB lighting. From a photo, the assistant identified the display as an **ASUS ROG Swift OLED PG27AQDP** (a high-end 27" OLED gaming monitor), and recommended **ASUS Armoury Crate** with **Aura Sync**, requiring a USB-B upstream cable from monitor to PC. He confirmed he had the USB 3.0 Type-B cable in hand and asked whether to restart Armoury Crate after plugging in.

The monitor wasn't detected; he wondered if a prior "key lock" (OSD button lock) was the cause — the assistant clarified that key lock only disables the monitor's physical buttons, not USB/software detection, and walked through ports, USB-hub settings, drivers, and the Armoury Crate service.

## The Wi-Fi problem
The conversation pivoted to networking: Wi-Fi worked on his phone but not his PC. After running through forget-network, driver updates, band (2.4 vs 5 GHz), and `ipconfig`/`netsh winsock reset` fixes with no luck, a speed test showed the PC *was* connected but crawling (1.43 Mbps down / 0.54 up) — reframed as a signal/interference/failing-adapter issue, not a config one. He bought a **TP-Link RE615X AX1800** Wi-Fi extender to use as a wired bridge (Ethernet to PC, bypassing the flaky internal Wi-Fi card). He also mentioned his case is an **NZXT** and asked basic Windows questions (how to restart, what the power icon looks like).

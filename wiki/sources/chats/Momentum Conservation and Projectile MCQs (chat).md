# Momentum Conservation and Projectile MCQs (chat)

A [[IB Physics (HL)]] homework session from early September 2025, the start of senior year. Traveler photographs a worksheet of mechanics multiple-choice questions plus a longer structured problem and works through them with ChatGPT, mostly checking his own reasoning rather than asking for answers cold.

## What he worked on
- **Q3 — trolley + dropped load** (perfectly inelastic): a load dropped vertically has no horizontal momentum, so conservation gives \( mu = (m+M)v \Rightarrow M = \tfrac{m(u-v)}{v} \) (answer A).
- **Q4 — ball rebounding off a wall**: impulse on the wall equals the change in the ball's momentum \( \Delta p \) by Newton's third law; the weight \( Mg \) acts vertically and contributes negligible impulse over the short collision (a "red herring").
- **Q5 — momentum ratio from kinetic energy**: with \( m_X = 2m_Y \) and \( KE_X = 4KE_Y \), he asked for it redone using only \( p = mv \) and \( KE = \tfrac12 mv^2 \) instead of \( K = p^2/2m \), getting \( p_X/p_Y = 2\sqrt{2} \).
- **Pellet-into-clay-block problem**: a 2.0 g pellet at 140 m/s embeds in a 56 g block, giving ~4.8 m/s; friction over 2.8 m gives ~0.24 N; then projectile launch off a 0.85 m table giving ~1.8 m horizontal range.

## What it shows
Characteristic of how Traveler uses AI for STEM: he treats it as a checker and tutor, not an answer key. He pushed back repeatedly — asking for the **simpler derivation** (only \( p=mv \) and \( KE \)), questioning whether his **negative force value** "makes sense" (it does — it's just direction, friction opposing motion), and noting he solved stopping time via \( s = \tfrac{u+v}{2}t \) rather than the suggested route. On the projectile part he posted his own incorrect working ("tell me how I'm wrong") and the diagnosis was that he had **dropped \( g = 9.8 \)** from \( v_y^2 = u_y^2 + 2gh \), collapsing his fall time and range. He also requested clean statements of the **principle of conservation of momentum** and the **SUVAT kinematic equations** — consolidating fundamentals, consistent with his preference for first-principles understanding over plugging formulas.

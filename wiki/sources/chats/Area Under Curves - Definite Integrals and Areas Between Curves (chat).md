# Area Under Curves - Definite Integrals and Areas Between Curves (chat)

A homework worksheet walkthrough for [[IB Math (SL)]] integral calculus, dated March 2026. Traveler photographs a series of textbook problems and has ChatGPT (gpt-5) work each one, mostly to check answers and supply the antiderivative steps. The session is a clean snapshot of where the SL course sits in spring of senior year: definite integration as **area under a curve** and **area between two curves**.

## Problems covered
- **Area under \(y=2x^2\)** split into vertical strips; he corrects the AI's misread of the picture ("no blue was from 0–2"), showing he is reading the geometry himself, not just trusting the model.
- **Downward parabola \(y=-x^2+x+6\):** finding x-intercepts by factoring \((x-3)(x+2)\), then integrating to get shaded area \(\tfrac{125}{6}\).
- **\(\int_0^{\pi/2}\cos x\,dx = 1\)** and the **\(\int \tan x\,dx = -\ln|\cos x|+C\)** derivation via u-substitution, including the insight that \(-\ln(\cos(\pi/2))\) diverges to \(+\infty\), matching the tangent asymptote.
- **\(\int_0^{\pi/4}\tan x\,dx = \tfrac12\ln 2\)** (a three-part exam-style question: find point A where \(\tan x=1\), find the antiderivative, then the area).
- **Exponentials:** derivative of \(e^{-x}\), and \(\int_{-1}^{1}(e^x+e^{-x})\,dx = 2(e-\tfrac1e)\). He floats a wrong simplification ("or 2/e?") and the model walks through why \(e^2\ne 2\).
- **Reciprocal / log integrals:** the equal-area split of \(y=1/x\) on \([2,10]\) lands at the **geometric mean** \(k=2\sqrt5\), not the midpoint — a nice conceptual takeaway. Plus \(\int 9/x\,dx\) and an area-equals-\(9\ln2\) problem solving \(k=6\).
- **Areas between curves:** line vs parabola (\(\tfrac43\)), \(\sqrt x\) vs \(x^2\) (\(\tfrac13\)), and \(y=x^2\) vs horizontal \(y=k\) where \(A=2.4 \Rightarrow k=3.6^{2/3}\approx2.349\).
- Closes with an algebra sanity check: \(x\sqrt x = x^{3/2}\).

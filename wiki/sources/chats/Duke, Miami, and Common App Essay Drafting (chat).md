# Duke, Miami, and Common App Essay Drafting

A long August 2025 ChatGPT session in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] iteratively drafted and rated **three different college application essays**: two Duke supplements, the **Common App personal statement**, and a University of Miami community supplement. It is one of the most revealing sources for both his [[College Search]] process and his [[Family and Personal Life]], capturing the launch of senior-year applications (class of 2026). The workflow is consistent: paste a draft, ask ChatGPT to "rate and analyze," absorb the feedback, repaste a lightly revised version, repeat — a dozen-plus cycles per essay.

## Duke "Why Us" supplement (250 words)
Traveler researched Duke's institutional values and programs before writing. The chat surfaces the specifics he then name-dropped to sound researched: **Trinity College** (Economics, CS), the **Duke Financial Economics Center (DFE)**, the **Rhodes iiD Data+/Code+** programs, **DukeEngage** (funded summer service), **Bass Connections**, and **QuadEx** (the four-year residential quad model). A key correction he absorbed: Trinity offers **Economics, not a standalone Finance major**, so he reframed his plan as "economics with a focus in finance through DFE." His final framing positions himself as a cross-disciplinary "[[Intellectual Interests|lifelong learner and builder]]" in [[UVA and the Quant Question|finance + CS/AI]], contributing to community through QuadEx and DukeEngage. This confirms Duke was a real target school alongside [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]] (where he ultimately committed).

## Duke AI-ethics supplement — built around Homework Hatch
The most substantive essay ties directly to his startup. Duke's optional prompt asks when one would or would not use AI; Traveler chose to write about **[[Homework Hatch (startup)|HW Buddy]]** (here called "Homework Buddy"), the AI study tool he built over summer 2025 that "analyzes assignments and uses AI to create quizzes, flashcards, study guides, and content guides." The essay's thesis is an integrity stance: AI should be **for learning, not cheating**. Critically, he discloses a real product-decision tension — partners **urged him to add a Turnitin-style AI checker and an essay-writing function**, and he **refused**, judging that a "cheating platform" would sell more copies but would betray "the honest pursuit of knowledge." This is a vivid, sourceable detail about Homework Hatch's product philosophy and his co-founder dynamics, and it maps directly onto his self-image as a [[Political and Economic Views|principled individualist]].

> [!note] Self-image vs. other chats
> The essay's clean "learning not cheating" line should be read against other sources where the cheating/learning boundary is murkier (e.g. market research into students who want answers). The integrity framing here is partly an admissions performance, though the refusal to add essay-writing functions appears genuine.

## Common App personal statement — "5 minutes" / surfing
He also pasted his **main personal statement**, an emotionally raw narrative. On a quiet August Friday his father sat the family down and, in five minutes, revealed that his ex-girlfriend is **pregnant with his child** and that he is **engaged to his current girlfriend**. Traveler describes a pattern of paternal volatility — new cars, new girlfriends, new businesses, a divorce, and nearly getting Traveler expelled over public disputes with the school about policy — framed through an "ebb and flow of waves" metaphor. The family then went to **Mexico** the week the baby (named **Reece**) was born; Traveler and his brother **Seaton** learned to **surf**, and surfing became the essay's controlling metaphor for controlling his actions if not his life. ChatGPT's main critique: it is ~70% about his father and needs more of *him*. This is the deepest biographical disclosure in the chats about his father's instability and his own coping style (default to anger, "put off what I can't control"), reinforcing themes in [[Emotional Life and Inner World]] and [[Family and Personal Life]].

## University of Miami community supplement (250 words)
The final essay covers his **2-week stint volunteering at the Bridges Baltimore Summer Institute at St. Paul's**, where he mentored incoming 9th graders, was named **Volunteer of the Year (middle school groups)**, and bonded with two students, **Kyeon and David**, over under-resourced Baltimore City schools, lunch, and dodgeball. This documents a concrete [[Extracurriculars and Achievements]] item and ties to [[St. Paul's School]]. ChatGPT repeatedly flagged his blunt phrasing ("notorious for being awful," later softened to "under-resourced") and pushed him to add the required Miami tie-in.

## What it shows
A snapshot of Traveler's actual application strategy: target schools (Duke, Miami, plus UVA), heavy reliance on ChatGPT as an iterative rater rather than a ghostwriter (he consistently declined full rewrites, asking instead for "tips" and re-rating his own revisions), and a willingness to mine his hardest personal material. It also doubles as primary-source documentation of Homework Hatch's mission and his family circumstances.

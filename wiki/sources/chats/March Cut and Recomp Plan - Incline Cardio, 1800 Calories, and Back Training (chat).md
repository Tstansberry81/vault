# March Cut and Recomp Plan - Incline Cardio, 1800 Calories, and Back Training

A personal fitness-coaching conversation in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] plans an aggressive March 2026 fat-loss cut and gets talked down from crash dieting toward a smarter recomposition strategy. It is a candid window into his self-image and physical-discipline habits, complementing the other body-recomp threads in the vault.

## Stats and starting point
He gives unusually specific self-data: 18, 5'7", ~150 lbs, ~25% body fat (self-estimated, later revised down to ~18-22% from progress photos), 165 then 175 bench (1.17x bodyweight), and a self-diagnosed "skinny-fat" frame he's carried since middle school with stubborn fat on the lower abdomen and lower back. His weakest link is his back: he can do only **one strict pull-up** and asks for a training program built around his home equipment (cables, dumbbells, barbells), specifically asking whether Kelso shrugs are worth it.

## The plan he actually settled on
- **Cardio:** 30-40 min incline treadmill (3.5 mph, ~14-15% incline), ~280-430 kcal/session, which the model calculated via the MET formula (~9 METs, ~10.7 kcal/min).
- **Calories:** he oscillated — proposed 1500, then 1800, then spiked to a 1400-cal / 187g-protein day — and was repeatedly pushed back toward 1700-1900 to preserve muscle. Estimated maintenance ~2400-2500, target deficit ~700-900/day, ~1 lb/week.
- **Protein:** 140-187g/day, sourced almost entirely from "Oikos yogurt protein shakes and grilled chicken breast."
- **Lifting:** back/triceps focus (his self-identified weak points), with a prescribed two-day back routine (assisted pull-ups/negatives, barbell and chest-supported rows, lat pulldowns, rear-delt flyes, Kelso shrugs).

## What it reveals
The recurring theme is impatience vs. consistency: he wanted to drop to 15% in a single month, was shown the physics (17 lbs / ~2100 kcal-deficit-per-day = impossible without muscle loss), and kept testing the floor on calories. The advice he absorbed — fix skinny-fat by *building* (back width, V-taper) rather than *shrinking* — reframes his goal from "be skinny for summer, bulk in college" toward year-round recomposition. He also disclosed an existing **nicotine/stimulant addiction**, asking how to "use it optimally" for the cut; the model declined to optimize it and gave harm-reduction notes instead. This sits alongside his broader [[Emotional Life and Inner World]] and [[Family and Personal Life]] as evidence of a self-optimizing, data-driven, occasionally extreme streak — the same temperament visible in his [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] and [[Investment Club]] work, applied here to his own body.

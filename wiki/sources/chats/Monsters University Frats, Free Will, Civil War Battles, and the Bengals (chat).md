# Monsters University Frats, Free Will, Civil War Battles, and the Bengals

A short, channel-surfing late-night chat (Nov 2025) where [[Traveler Stansberry]] fires off four unrelated curiosities in a row: the *Monsters University* fraternities/sororities, the philosophy of free will, the bloodiest battles of the American Civil War, and how the Cincinnati Bengals defense is doing in 2025. Most of it is throwaway trivia, but the middle section is a compact window into the one topic he keeps circling back to.

## Free will — the load-bearing part
The substantive exchange is Traveler asking, in sequence, for the **arguments against free will**, then "what about choice," then the **arguments for free will** — a deliberate steelman-both-sides move that matches his self-image as a [[determinist]] who still wants to test the position rather than just hold it. This connects directly to [[Fate and Free Will]], his [[Political and Economic Views|determinism]], and recurring threads in [[Core Convictions]] and [[Tensions and Open Questions]].

The model's anti-free-will case ran through: hard determinism (atoms since the Big Bang), Libet-style neuroscience (brain activity precedes conscious awareness of deciding), genetics + environment ("you can't choose your chooser"), behavioral predictability, the illusion of conscious control / post-hoc rationalization, the point that quantum randomness ≠ freedom, the "no central self, just a committee of subsystems" view, and moral luck. On "choice," the key distinction he was handed: **choice ≠ free will** — the *experience* of deliberating is real ("behavioral flexibility"), but the menu of options and the winning option are both products of unchosen prior causes.

The pro side covered the phenomenology of agency, the meaningfulness of deliberation, moral responsibility as a normative requirement, indeterminism leaving "space for agency," the self as a real emergent causal entity, the ability to act against immediate drives, rationality presupposing freedom, and — the position framed as the one most modern philosophers buy — **compatibilism**: free will as acting in accordance with your own values, uncoerced, without needing to break physics. This is the framing most in tension with Traveler's hard-determinist instinct, and worth noting as an open question for him.

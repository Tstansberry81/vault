# IB HL History Paper 1 Revision - Cold War, Apartheid, and Maoist China

Traveler fed ChatGPT a messy "memory dump" of his [[IB History (HL)]] notes and asked it to (1) summarize every topic, person, event, and idea, (2) flag what he was missing, and (3) drill him on the mechanics of HL Paper 1. The result is effectively a self-built revision sheet spanning the entire authoritarian-states / Cold War core of the IB History syllabus.

## Content covered
The note dump and the model's reorganization stitched together a chronological narrative of postwar ideological conflict:
- **Red Scare and McCarthyism** — Joseph McCarthy's 1950 Wheeling speech, the "witch hunt" framing, the demagogue concept, plus the Rosenberg execution and Alger Hiss perjury case as evidence of domestic anti-communist paranoia.
- **Early Cold War context** — capitalism vs. communism as rival claims to "human progress," divided Germany as a flashpoint, the 1945–1991 timeframe.
- **Iran 1953 coup** — John Foster Dulles, the Tudeh Party, the US/UK overthrow of Iran's elected government, and the long fuse running to the Iranian Revolution and theocracy.
- **Cuba** — Bay of Pigs (1961, JFK, Richard Bissell), the appeal of Marxism as anti-imperialism, Guantanamo, and the Cuban Missile Crisis framed through **Allison's three decision-making models** (rational actor, organizational behavior, governmental politics) plus hubris/nemesis.
- **South Africa / apartheid** — Boer Wars, Social Darwinism as ideological cover, Dr. Malan, pass laws, the ANC, Stephen Biko, the Suppression of Communism Act, and the de Klerk–Mandela transition (1990).
- **China** — warlord era, KMT vs. CCP, the Long March, PRC founding (1949), the Hundred Flowers Campaign, Great Leap Forward (~20M deaths), Cultural Revolution and Red Guards, the Sino-Soviet split, Deng Xiaoping's reforms, Tiananmen, and Nixon's ping-pong diplomacy.

## What it reveals
The chat shows Traveler studying for the **Paper 1 source-analysis exam** specifically — distinct from the [[Paper 2 Study Guide]] essay work. The model walked him through the four-question structure: message (3 marks), Origin/Purpose/Content (4), compare-and-contrast (6), and the OPCVL evaluation "boss fight" (9). Useful takeaways it pushed: sources are "limited," not "biased"; provenance over passion; own knowledge is "seasoning, not the meal"; and the mark-printing sentence stem *"This source is valuable for understanding ___, but limited because ___."*

The "anything big I'm missing" turn is the most analytically interesting: ChatGPT flagged that his notes were Atlantic-centric and leader-focused, missing **nuclear deterrence / MAD**, proxy wars (Korea, Vietnam, Afghanistan 1979), de-Stalinization, the Hollywood blacklist layer of McCarthyism, grassroots/sanctions pressure on apartheid, and explicit **continuity arguments** — the recurring mechanism where fear and state power override individual rights across McCarthyism, apartheid, Maoist purges, and Tiananmen. That synthesis ("different ideologies, same mechanism") maps neatly onto his broader interest in [[Individual vs. Society]] and [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]].

A characteristic Traveler workflow: outsource the OPCVL grind, demand the gaps be named, and have the AI generate a consolidated master notes file rather than re-write notes by hand — the same study-automation instinct behind [[Homework Hatch (startup)]].

# Foreign Policy of the First Six Presidents — Book Research

An early ChatGPT session (Oct 2023, sophomore year) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] mined the model for **book recommendations and capsule summaries on the foreign policies of the first six US presidents** — Washington, John Adams, Jefferson, Madison, Monroe, and John Quincy Adams. He worked methodically: first asking for one book per president, then drilling into each ("what was Washington's view on foreign policy?"), then iterating the same query at widening scope ("first 5 presidents" → "first 6") to find a single synthesizing text.

## What he was after
The pattern is a student building a **reading list and a thesis-level grasp of early-republic diplomacy**. Key takeaways he extracted:
- **Washington** — avoidance of permanent alliances, neutrality, trade-as-stability (the Farewell Address, via Avlon's *Washington's Farewell*).
- **John Adams** — the Quasi-War with France, XYZ Affair, Alien and Sedition Acts, peace via the Convention of 1800 (Ferling's *John Adams: A Life*).
- **Jefferson** — Louisiana Purchase, the First Barbary War, the failed Embargo Act of 1807 (Kilmeade & Yaeger's *Tripoli Pirates*; Meacham's *The Art of Power*).
- **Madison** — the War of 1812 (Ketcham's biography).
- **Monroe** — the Monroe Doctrine (Unger's *The Last Founding Father*).
- **John Quincy Adams** — Secretary of State, architect of the Monroe Doctrine (Kaplan's *American Visionary*).

For a single overview he was pointed to Gordon S. Wood's **_Empire of Liberty: A History of the Early Republic, 1789–1815_** and Joseph Ellis's *Founding Brothers* / *The Quartet*. He also hit the model's refusal to quote copyrighted text when he asked for direct quotations from Avlon to "support Washington's foreign policy agenda."

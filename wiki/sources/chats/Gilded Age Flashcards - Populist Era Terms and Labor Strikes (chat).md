# Gilded Age Flashcards - Populist Era Terms and Labor Strikes

A brief, mechanical February 2024 study-prep chat from Traveler's sophomore-year US History course at [[St. Paul's School]] — predating his [[IB History (HL)]] sequence (which centers on 20th-century and Cold War material). He pastes a chronological term list spanning 1868-1896 and asks ChatGPT first to generate front/back **flashcards**, then to strip the definitions to event names only, then to remove the dates, then to **shuffle** the list twice — clearly building an active-recall quiz tool for himself rather than seeking any analysis.

The content is the Gilded Age and Populist era: the 1868 Treaty of Fort Laramie, the Knights of Labor, the Panics of 1873 and 1893, the Crime of '73 (silver demonetization) and the 1879 gold-standard return, Little Bighorn, the Dawes Act, the Chinese Exclusion Act, and the major labor confrontations — the Great Railroad Strike, Haymarket, Homestead, Pullman, and Coxey's Army — alongside the regulatory turn (Munn v. Illinois, Wabash, the Interstate Commerce Act) and the reform texts of Henry George, Edward Bellamy, and Carnegie's "Gospel of Wealth," ending with the Populist Party's 1896 collapse.

The chat carries no commentary, no images, and no contradictions to flag — it is a pure utility session. Its value to the wiki is as an early datapoint: it shows Traveler's long-standing habit of turning raw class material into self-quizzing systems (a precursor to the spaced-recall and flashcard features he'd later spec for [[Homework Hatch (startup)]]), and it documents the labor/economic-history grounding that predates the [[Political and Economic Views|political and economic views]] he developed by senior year. It pairs with the same-vintage [[Colonial America Notes Condensing (chat)]] and [[Compact History Midterm Review - Pre-Columbus to the Civil War (chat)|Compact History Midterm Review]] as artifacts of his pre-IB American history coursework.

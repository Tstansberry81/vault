# Economics EE Examiner Feedback and AD-AS Graph (chat)

A late-draft critique session for Traveler's [[Extended Essay (Economics)]], in which he pastes the near-complete essay and asks ChatGPT to predict an IB score and suggest edits on "clarity, professionalism, evidence, or argument." The essay is his pandemic-fiscal-policy investigation argued through a [[IB Economics (SL)]] lens, with a local [[Maryland]]-Baltimore case study layered onto national data. This is the same EE explored in [[Economics EE - Pandemic Fiscal Policy and Maryland Inflation (chat)]], but here the focus is grading, polish, and the AD/AS diagram rather than research-gathering.

## The essay being graded
The full draft argues that the U.S. government's COVID-19 fiscal and monetary response — the **CARES Act** and **ARPA** (~$4T of injected aggregate demand), the Fed dropping the EFFR to near zero, and Maryland's **RELIEF Act of 2021** — was the *leading* driver of 2022 inflation, ahead of supply-chain and Russia/Ukraine oil shocks. It marshals heavy data: national CPI-U rising 260.5 → 305.1, Maryland's Baltimore-Columbia-Towson CPI-U rising 261.5 → 306.5 (both peaking June 2022 at ~6%), $5/gal Maryland gas, falling labor-participation rates, and a citation of MIT Sloan's Mark Kritzman attributing ~42% of inflation to federal spending. The thesis-defense structure (raise a counterargument, then rebut it) recurs in every section.

## What ChatGPT told him
- **Score prediction:** as-is a mid-band **B (22–26/34)**; after edits, **A range (28–32/34)**. The A was blocked by (a) "unacademic fluff" ("mass hysteria," "something the 21st century never thought would happen"), (b) popular sources like *Investopedia* where IB wants BLS/FRED/CBO/IMF/NBER, (c) repetition (the CARES/ARPA inflation mechanism explained three times), and (d) counterarguments acknowledged but not *weighed* with relative magnitude.
- It pushed him to explicitly **rank causes** — demand-pull vs. cost-push vs. energy shocks — and to give the Maryland case "teeth" by emphasizing where the state *deviated* from the national average (sharper labor-participation decline) rather than just mirroring it.
- It rewrote a clunky NBER sentence for him into IB register.

## The AD/AS graph question
The intellectually revealing exchange: Traveler asks whether "IB needs that graph to be quantitatively accurate." Answer — **no**. An IB Economics EE diagram is *diagrammatic*, meant to demonstrate theory (label AD₀/AD₁, AS₀/AS₁, P, Y, equilibrium shifts) and connect it to the case study; numerical accuracy belongs in the *text* (cite "CPI rose to 9.1% in June 2022"), not on the axes. He then asks for and receives a mock AD-right / AS-left diagram showing equilibrium moving to lower output and higher price level — the canonical stagflation picture matching his COVID-inflation story.

This shows Traveler working the [[IB History (HL)]]-adjacent essayist's instinct for argument structure but applying it to economics, and being pragmatic about exactly how much rigor the rubric demands — consistent with his broader [[UVA and the Quant Question]] orientation toward finance and his [[Political and Economic Views]] skepticism of overextended fiscal intervention. The "history repeats itself" closer and the framing of stimulus as long-term self-harm echo his determinist, intervention-wary leanings.

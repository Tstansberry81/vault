# Mixed Homework - ASAD Graph, Physics MCQs, Math, and Cold War

A rapid-fire homework session (gpt-4o, May 2025) spanning four of Traveler's IB subjects in a single thread — a window into late-junior-year cramming across [[IB Economics (SL)]], [[IB Physics (HL)]], [[IB Math (SL)]], and [[IB History (HL)]]. The questions are short and utilitarian: he is checking specific exam-style answers rather than learning concepts from scratch.

## Economics
Opens with the title question: a **change in price level** on an AS/AD graph causes **movement along** the AD and AS curves, not a shift of either. Later he returns to **monetary policy** — asking what rising/falling interest rates cause, and specifically whether a rate change shifts the **money supply (MS)** curve. The model corrects a likely misconception of his: MS is a vertical line fixed by the central bank, so an interest-rate change is only a **movement along money demand (MD)**; only open-market operations shift MS. He also lists the macro effects of higher rates (lower spending/investment, lower inflation, stronger currency, lower asset prices) — material that overlaps his investing interests in [[Investment Club]].

## Physics (HL exam MCQs)
Several Paper-1-style multiple-choice problems, all worked correctly:
- Stone falling at **constant velocity** through oil — identifies that KE is constant, GPE falls into thermal energy, and work by gravity equals work by friction (statements I and III).
- **Hooke's law** spring constant from extension: k = mg/(l − l₀).
- **Impulse/collision** with a wall: average force m(u+v)/T, KE loss ½m(u²−v²). Notably he asks the model to re-derive the force **without using momentum or impulse**, getting it via a = Δv/T and F = ma — showing he prefers grounding answers in first principles he already trusts.
- **Inclined-plane friction**: μs = tan θ₀, N = mg cos θ₀.

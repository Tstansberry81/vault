# Econ SL Paper 2 Two-Day Cram - Quotas, Currency Math, and the UVA-Credit Calculation

Two days before the [[IB Economics (SL)]] Paper 2 exam, [[Traveler Stansberry]] builds a full survival plan. ChatGPT lays out the SL exam structure (Paper 2 = data response, 1h45, 40% of grade, parts a–g with a 15-mark evaluation essay), then tiers the syllabus by yield: demand/supply, elasticity, government intervention, externalities, AD/AS, fiscal and monetary policy, exchange rates, and the global economy are "know cold," while perfect-competition firm diagrams and oligopoly game theory get flagged as more AP-style than core IB SL — useful context, given the [[Paper 2 Study Guide]] framing he applies elsewhere.

The standout content: he admits his econ teacher "sucked" and never covered quantitative methods, so the chat fills two real gaps. First, a full import-quota explanation with step-by-step graph construction (world price Pw, domestic supply/demand, quota rent vs tariff revenue, deadweight loss) — a topic the class skipped entirely. Second, a thorough currency-conversion primer using unit-cancellation (1 USD = x JPY → divide/multiply), appreciation/depreciation percentage math, and the AD = C + I + G + (X − M) link. He then dumps two years of handwritten notes for ChatGPT to OCR into an 18-chapter review guide with software-generated graphs (including Phillips and Lorenz curves) plus a standalone Paper 2 cheat sheet covering balance-of-payments and data-response math.

The most revealing exchange is strategic: he openly weighs whether to bother, noting UVA awards no credit for SL exams or the IB Diploma's SL components. ChatGPT confirms his instinct (UVA Arts & Sciences gives no SL credit) but argues for efficient effort to protect the diploma and build genuinely useful finance instincts — squarely aligned with his [[UVA and the Quant Question]] trajectory and [[Political and Economic Views]]. He closes by asking about real-world examples (ChatGPT refusing to let him fabricate fake scenarios for Paper 1) and a natural-rate-of-unemployment graph.

> [!note] What it reveals
> A clear-eyed cost-benefit student: he treats the exam as a rational investment, not a sacred duty, while still refusing to fully quit. The teacher-gap admission also explains why so much of his econ understanding is self-assembled via ChatGPT rather than classroom-delivered.

# Japanese Period Dramas Research Paper - Jidaigeki Sources for SL Japanese

A short, practical research-scaffolding chat for a class project in [[IB Japanese (SL)]]. Traveler is starting a project on Japanese television period dramas (時代劇 / *jidaigeki*) and opens by honestly flagging his level: his Japanese "extends to chapter 4 of the 3rd adventure in [[The Adventures of Japanese|Adventures in Japanese]] textbook." The model scales everything to that beginner grammar, supplying safe vocabulary (有名 famous, 人気 popular, 昔 long ago, 強い strong, 助ける to help) and example sentences within his range, while warning him off archaic samurai speech and "feudal land taxation systems unless you enjoy pain."

The substance of the chat is sourcing. The assignment requires examining **two dramas over 800 words in Japanese** with three resources total: one per drama plus one framing *jidaigeki* as a cultural phenomenon. The interesting part is Traveler iteratively tightening his constraints across the conversation — a small window into how he wrangles AI toward a real rubric rather than accepting the first answer:

1. First pass suggests *Shōgun* (2024) and *Gunshi Kanbei* with Wikipedia links.
2. He rejects it: "no wikipedia and has to be native to japan, no shogun."
3. The model pivots to Japanese-language broadcaster sources (NHK/TBS official pages, *Hiragana Times*, テレビドラマデータベース) for 水戸黄門 (Mito Kōmon) and 江戸を斬る (Edo o Kiru).
4. He reverses again: "ideally articles or resources are in english... now i only need valid resources for mito komon and edo o kiru" — landing on the Japan Foundation Film Festival *jidaigeki* guide, a Stars and Stripes Japan article on Mito Kōmon, and (reluctantly) the English Wikipedia page for Edo o Kiru.

The final two dramas chosen are **水戸黄門 (Mito Kōmon)** — the long-running formula drama about a secretly powerful old man righting wrongs as he travels — and **江戸を斬る (Edo o Kiru)**, a TBS Edo-period series, framed as a contrast in production style and tone. The model offered MLA citation formatting and a structure (intro defining the genre, two ~300-word case studies, conclusion on cultural values).

This is a minor but characteristic artifact: Traveler doing the lower-stakes language coursework that rounds out his [[Academic Record]], using AI as a research librarian and outline-builder rather than a ghostwriter (he repeatedly narrows toward *valid, citable* sources, not pre-written prose). It connects to his broader [[Japanese Kanji Drilling then Peptides and a Body-Recomp Plan (chat)|kanji and vocabulary drilling]] sessions and his general use of ChatGPT to compensate for being an early-stage SL student tackling an ambitious topic.

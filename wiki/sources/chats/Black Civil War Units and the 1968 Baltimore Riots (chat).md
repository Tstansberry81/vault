# Black Civil War Units and the 1968 Baltimore Riots (chat)

A research-and-worksheet session for a US/American history class (distinct from his [[IB History (HL)]] track, which centers on 20th-century Cold War and authoritarian states). Two unrelated tasks share the thread: (1) building a class presentation on Black military units in the Civil War, and (2) filling out a reading-check worksheet on the 1968 Baltimore riots.

## Black Civil War units presentation
Traveler asked for three notable, specific Black units that "accomplished something," with named members, battles, dates, citations, and an explicit segregation angle. The chat (after correcting his Buffalo Soldiers premise — they are post-1866, not Civil War) settled on three:

- **54th Massachusetts Infantry** — first official Northern Black regiment; assault on Fort Wagner (July 18, 1863), Col. Robert Gould Shaw killed, William Harvey Carney's Medal of Honor flag rescue. Paid \$10 vs \$13 until 1864, white officers only.
- **1st Kansas Colored Volunteer Infantry** — first Black regiment to see combat (Island Mound, Oct 29, 1862), fought before emancipation under threat of execution if captured; later Honey Springs and the Poison Spring massacre.
- **Louisiana Native Guard** — uniquely served on both Confederate then Union sides; Siege of Port Hudson (May 22, 1863), André Cailloux killed leading a charge; Black officers later stripped of command.

He iterated the request three times — first the units, then three ranked engagements per unit, then a per-unit analysis of how segregation/racial tension shaped each. The framing he pushed for is analytical rather than descriptive ("an analysis of the impact of segregation"), and the thesis he was handed — "the Civil War expanded Black participation while preserving white control" — echoes the [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]] and [[The Moral Impact of Slavery]] threads that recur across his coursework and his reading of [[Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass]]. His instinct to want material that "actually earns points" and sounds "intelligent instead of like a Wikipedia echo" is characteristic of how he uses ChatGPT as a research accelerator.

## 1968 Baltimore riots worksheet
The second half ("help me fill ts out") is a reading-check on an article, *When Baltimore Burned*, covering the riots after MLK's assassination: causes, damage to the city's image, accelerated white flight and school resegregation (City College now 89% Black), redlining by the FHA, Spiro Agnew as governor, and named individuals (Sharon Singer's looted pharmacy, Lucille Gorham's laundromat firebombing, Terry White stopped by the National Guard). This local Maryland material connects to his home-state focus elsewhere — he also wrote a paper on [[COVID Stimulus and Inflation in Maryland - Paper Research (chat)|COVID Stimulus and Inflation in Maryland - Paper Research]].

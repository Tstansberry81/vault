# Best of Enemies Civics Worksheet, Cosine Graph, and Econ Notation

A short, multi-subject homework session (GPT-5, 2025-09-25) that jumps across three unrelated school tasks: a civics worksheet on the documentary *Best of Enemies (2015)*, an [[IB Math (SL)]] trig-graph problem, and a one-line [[IB Economics (SL)]] notation question. Typical of [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s pattern of using ChatGPT as a catch-all homework assistant across his [[Academic Record]].

## Best of Enemies civics questions
After a fumble on the year (he first triggers the 2019 drama, then asks for "2025," then corrects to the **2015 documentary** *Best of Enemies: Buckley vs. Vidal*), Traveler uploads a worksheet and asks for full answers. The doc covers the 1968 ABC televised debates between **Gore Vidal** (liberal) and **William F. Buckley Jr.** (conservative) — framed as the "big bang" of confrontational TV punditry, including Buckley's infamous on-air slur. The model writes eight short essay answers on political discourse, civility vs. combativeness, media's entertainment incentives, style vs. substance, the debates' legacy in modern cable news, and whether bold personalities help or harm democracy. The civic-discourse themes (reasoned debate vs. spectacle, hostility vs. compromise) sit close to Traveler's own [[Political and Economic Views]] and his interest in [[Individual vs. Society]].

## Trig graph problem (IB Math SL)
He uploads a graph and asks to solve for the unknowns in $y = \tfrac12\cos(bx) + d$. The model flounders badly at first — misreads the midline, declares the labeled point impossible because it would require $\cos = -1.5$. **Traveler catches the error himself**, correcting that the feature at $y=-0.5$ is a y-intercept (not an x-intercept) and that $d = -1$, which he had already worked out. He then explicitly directs the method: *"dont use derivatives to solve for b use 2πk."* The clean redo gives $d=-1$, $b=1$, $y = \tfrac12\cos x - 1$, range $[-1.5,-0.5]$, period $2\pi$. A good snapshot of Traveler steering the AI rather than deferring to it — he spotted the model's mistake and dictated the solution technique.

> [!note] Self-correction
> Notable that the student out-reasoned the model here, both on the intercept reading and on insisting the curve-period method replace the derivative the model tried to use.

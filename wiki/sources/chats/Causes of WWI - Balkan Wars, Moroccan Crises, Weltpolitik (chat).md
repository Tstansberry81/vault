# Causes of WWI - Balkan Wars, Moroccan Crises, Weltpolitik

A rapid-fire [[IB History (HL)]] revision session in which Traveler quizzed ChatGPT through the long-term causes of World War I, one term at a time. The chat reads as exam cram: a string of one- or two-word prompts ("balkan wars summary," "moroccan crisis," "bosnia crisis," "weltpolitik," "nationalism pre ww1," "naval arms race britain and germany explanation") each answered with a structured cause-event-outcome-significance breakdown. The throughline is the standard IB narrative of how diplomatic crises, alliances, nationalism, and militarism turned the Balkans into the "powder keg of Europe."

## Topics covered
- **Balkan Wars (1912–13).** First Balkan War: the Balkan League (Serbia, Montenegro, Bulgaria, Greece) expels the Ottomans from Europe; Treaty of London. Second Balkan War: Bulgaria, dissatisfied over Macedonia, attacks its former allies and is crushed; Treaty of Bucharest leaves Serbia strengthened and Austria-Hungary alarmed. He asked about the Second Balkan War twice.
- **Moroccan Crises (1905–06 Tangier/Algeciras; 1911 Agadir/Panther).** Germany twice tries to break the Entente Cordiale and is twice humiliated, pushing Britain and France closer.
- **Bosnian Annexation Crisis (1908–09).** Austria-Hungary annexes Bosnia; Russia, weakened after the Russo-Japanese War, backs down but vows not to again. Direct line drawn to Gavrilo Princip and the assassination of Franz Ferdinand.
- **Naval arms race & national pride.** The Two-Power Standard, HMS Dreadnought (1906), Tirpitz's Navy Laws, and how the race became a symbol of identity/honor on both sides.
- **Weltpolitik vs. Bismarck's Realpolitik** — Wilhelm II's "place in the sun," colonial expansion, and resulting German diplomatic isolation.
- **Austria-Hungary as a multi-ethnic Dual Monarchy** (Compromise of 1867, Franz Joseph I, 1918 collapse) and **Pan-Slavism / irredentism** (Alsace-Lorraine revanchism).

## Tangents
A couple of WWII-era questions wandered in mid-session — why Germany took the Sudetenland (Lebensraum, appeasement, the 1938 Munich Agreement), whether Germany still occupies it (no; returned to Czechoslovakia, Beneš Decrees), and a comparison table of the **Triple Alliance/Triple Entente vs. the Axis/Allies**. The comparison is conceptually the most interesting moment: it notes WWI alliances were defensive balance-of-power alignments while the Axis pursued offensive expansion, and that Italy switched sides in both wars.

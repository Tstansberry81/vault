# Bipedal Efficiency, Flynn Effect, and Trig Identity Proofs

A grab-bag October 2025 ChatGPT session that swings from idle science curiosity into a focused [[IB Math (SL)]] trig-identity study session — the most substantive and revealing part of the chat.

## Curiosity questions (opening)
Traveler front-loaded three unrelated science questions, the kind of broad-interest probing that recurs across his chats and feeds his [[Intellectual Interests]]:
- **Bipedal vs. quadrupedal locomotion** — is two-legged walking more energy-efficient? (Yes, over distance, via pendulum/tendon-spring mechanics and the persistence-hunting evolutionary angle.)
- **Has average IQ risen over 100 years?** — the **Flynn Effect** (~3 points/decade through the 20th century), its environmental/educational drivers, and the post-2000 reversal; with the caveat that IQ measures a narrow slice of cognition, not biological brain upgrades.
- **Brain-to-body mass ratio** — why the raw ratio misleads and why the **encephalization quotient (EQ ~7–8 for humans)** is the better metric.

## Trig identity proofs (the core)
The bulk of the chat is Traveler working through a worksheet of trigonometric identity proofs (parts e, f, g), photographing the problem set and asking ChatGPT to verify each. He proves:
- `cos α/(1−tan α) + sin²α/(sin α−cos α) = sin α + cos α` (difference of squares)
- `sin θ/(1+cos θ) + (1+cos θ)/sin θ = 2/sin θ` (Pythagorean identity)
- `sin θ/(1−cos θ) − sin θ/(1+cos θ) = 2/tan θ` — first via cotangent, then re-done "without cotangent" per the worksheet's constraint.

The learning moment: Traveler hit a wall, reporting his own work had collapsed into `2 sin²θ tan θ / (tan²θ − sin²θ)` and admitting *"i dont know where to go or if i used the wrong identities. its so confusing."* The diagnosis was that he had **crossbred tan with sin/cos mid-problem**. He then asked the deeper question — *"whats a general way to use identities properly and what to look for/do"* — yielding a reusable playbook: pick a base (sin/cos), use conjugates to trigger `1−cos²θ = sin²θ`, combine fractions early, only convert to tan at the very end, and check domains.

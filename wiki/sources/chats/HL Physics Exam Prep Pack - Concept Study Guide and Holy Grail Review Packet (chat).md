# HL Physics Exam Prep Pack - Concept Study Guide and Holy Grail Review Packet

The single most ambitious [[IB Physics (HL)]] revision session in the archive. [[Traveler Stansberry]] opens by pasting a locked St. Paul's [[St. Paul's School|SharePoint]]/OneDrive link (the model can't access it, predictably), then uploads photos of his handwritten notes across every HL unit plus the prior-year M25 Paper 1A/1B/2 exam. He commissions a three-document **"holy grail" prep pack**: a concept-first study guide (10 then 15 core ideas per unit, emphasizing derivation logic over plug-and-chug — he's explicit that he can memorize formulas but struggles to *use and derive* them), an exam-styled review packet, and a matching solutions document.

What this reveals is Traveler's project-managing instinct turned on his own exam prep: he iterates through at least seven rounds of revision, demanding formal language, embedded equations, a full Mechanics unit (force/work/power/kinematics/momentum/circular motion), Relativity (5 MCQs + time dilation/length contraction), and Rotational Motion (torque, moment of inertia, angular momentum, rolling without slipping). He insists core concepts appear only in the solutions, not before questions, and that every Paper 1A question carry an essential graph/diagram/table. This is the same demanding-spec-then-tweak workflow he runs on [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] — fitting, since a packet like this is exactly what HW Buddy aims to auto-generate.

The chat's second half is gold for the actual exam: full worked solutions to the M25 Paper 2, covering friction and tipping (\(\mu_s N\) vs. tipping moment), a relativity spacetime diagram (\(x'\)-axis gradient \(v/c\), frame-dependent simultaneity), an adiabatic gas cycle (\(PV^\gamma\), \(\gamma=5/3\)), Doppler effect for both sound and galactic redshift, alpha decay energy via mass defect (\(Q \approx 5\) MeV, 98% carried by the alpha), Rutherford closest-approach distance, the photoelectric work function (\(\phi \approx 2.5\) eV), buoyancy-driven SHM of a cork, and an iceberg/momentum/conduction marathon. His weak spots are flagged: interference intuition, induction (conceptual), and knowing *which* equation to deploy.

The distilled "one concept per unit" summary is the most reusable takeaway — phase is everything in waves, energy-transfer logic in thermodynamics, mass defect as stored energy, fields-tell-charges-how-to-move-potential-tells-them-why. This complements his other targeted sets like [[Measuring Uncertainty and Deriving Orbital Energy - HL Physics Midterm Review (chat)|the midterm review]] and [[Rotational Motion Crash Course - Torque, Rolling Without Slipping, and a Bombed HL Test (chat)|the rotational crash course]].

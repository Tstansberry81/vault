# Ranke, Thucydides, and Bloch - Doubt in Historical Method

A [[Theory of Knowledge]] prep session in which Traveler builds the History area-of-knowledge (AOK) section of a TOK essay on the prompt "doubt is central to the pursuit of knowledge." He works the chat as a research engine, feeding it primary-source quotations and asking for breakdowns he can metabolize into examiner-ready argument. The throughline: three historians who each treat **doubt** differently, letting him argue *extent* and *degree* rather than a flat "history is subjective."

## The three models of doubt

- **Leopold von Ranke (19th c.)** — supplies the quote "it seeks only to show what actually happened" (*wie es eigentlich gewesen*). Ranke rejects history's traditional roles (moral judgment, political instruction, teleology) and front-loads doubt onto *interpretations* and grand narratives while trusting documents. The chat flags the exploitable contradiction: choosing *not* to judge is itself a philosophical stance, so Ranke's bid to eliminate doubt about facts manufactures false certainty.
- **Thucydides (5th c. BCE)** — the speeches passage from *History of the Peloponnesian War*, Book I.22, where he admits he reconstructed speeches to fit "the general purport of what was actually said." Here doubt is a *starting condition*, not a failure: memory is unreliable, so the historian preserves meaning and causal logic over literal wording. Disciplined subjectivity, not relativism.
- **Marc Bloch / the Annales School (1929–1940s)** — from *The Historian's Craft* (*Apologie pour l'histoire*, pub. posthumously 1949). Doubt becomes *procedural*: sources lie, misunderstand, exaggerate, so the historian interrogates systematically. The Annales redirect doubt at what *counts* as evidence (geography, climate, economics, mentalities) and at causation itself — Bloch's "fetish of origins" critique. Triangulating these three is the chat's payoff move: doubt evolves from Thucydides (necessity) to Ranke (something to suppress) to Bloch (a principle that redefines the discipline).

## Annales significance (non-TOK)

Traveler also asks for the Annales School's plain historical importance, getting a clean survey: it replaced "events + elites" history with the study of how societies function; introduced [[Fernand Braudel]]'s *longue durée* layering (geography / structures / events-as-noise); made history interdisciplinary and social-scientific; shifted focus to ordinary people ("history from below," microhistory); and weakened nationalist and teleological narratives after WWII.

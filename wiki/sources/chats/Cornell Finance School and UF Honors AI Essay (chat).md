# Cornell Finance School and UF Honors AI Essay

A short October 2025 college-applications chat with two unrelated halves, both feeding [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s senior-year application push (class of 2026).

**Part 1 — Which Cornell school for finance.** Traveler asks which Cornell undergraduate unit to apply to for a finance career. ChatGPT lays out the [[College Search]] landscape: the **Dyson School of Applied Economics & Management** (within the SC Johnson College of Business) as the right undergraduate target for IB/corporate/quant pathways, versus the **Johnson School** (graduate MBA/MS) for later. The recommendation aligns with his stated trajectory — finance with a math minor — already documented under [[UVA and the Quant Question]] and his broader [[Political and Economic Views]] and [[Intellectual Interests]]. This is one more data point in his fixation on finance-feeder schools, alongside the many Boston/BU/Cornell/Duke comparison chats in this vault.

**Part 2 — UF Honors College "AI essay."** The bulk of the chat is drafting a supplemental essay for the **University of Florida Honors Program** (400-word cap) on navigating an AI-reshaped education while preserving critical thinking, creativity, and human connection. Traveler pastes his own rough opening, which pivots the prompt squarely onto **[[Homework Hatch (startup)]]** — describing it as a platform where students upload assignment and grade data and AI generates study guides, videos, flashcards, and collaborative quizzes, and noting he uses it himself to build study guides, plan his schedule, and answer clarification questions. ChatGPT polishes this into a ~385-word draft that keeps Homework Hatch as the central hook while balancing it with honors-seminar / interdisciplinary / ethical-reflection language.

What it reveals: Traveler treats his startup as his signature application differentiator, the concrete "I built something" anchor that distinguishes him from generic applicants. ChatGPT's closing offer to make it "more entrepreneurial / personal (e.g. referencing Homework Hatch, your IB background, or your leadership work with **Bridges Baltimore**)" surfaces the other pillars he packages for admissions — his IB Diploma candidacy and a Baltimore service/leadership thread also visible in his [[Extracurriculars and Achievements]]. The essay's posture (AI as empowerment tool, human capacities that "can't be automated") is a polished admissions framing of the edtech mission rather than a new intellectual position.

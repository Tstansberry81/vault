# MacBook Pro Monitor and Desk Setup for Dad's House (chat)

A practical shopping-and-fit consultation (Sept 2025) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] spec'd a monitor + peripheral setup for a ~2022 MacBook Pro at his dad's house. The framing is telling of his split living arrangement: he notes his main PC (the high-end [[Gaming PC Setup - ASUS ROG Monitor RGB and Wi-Fi Troubleshooting (chat)|RTX-class gaming/AI rig]]) lives "at my mom's," so this is a lightweight secondary workstation for the other household.

## What he was doing
The conversation funneled from broad to specific:
1. **Open-ended ask** — best monitor for an older MacBook Pro. ChatGPT surveyed the Apple Studio Display (5K, $1,599+), LG UltraFine 5K (discontinued, ~$700–1,300 used), Dell UltraSharp 4K USB-C/Thunderbolt hubs (U2725QE $760, U3225QE $970), and the Samsung M8 smart monitor ($500).
2. **Cost analysis** — he demanded full prices plus a "what you avoid buying" breakdown (built-in webcam/speakers/dock vs. add-on costs).
3. **Budget collapse** — narrowed to an **under-$500 total** build, then refined again to **$500 for just monitor + keyboard + mouse** with "better gear."

## The landing spec
The recommended single-cable build centered on the **Dell 27" 4K USB-C (S2725QC)** (~$290, USB-C video + charging, cable in box), paired with either a **Keychron K2 Pro** mechanical keyboard or **Logitech MX Keys Mini for Mac**, plus a **Logitech MX Master 3S** mouse — total ~$474–490 before tax.

## The fit problem (the real payoff)
He sent a [[Family and Personal Life|photo of the dad's-house desk]] — a hutch-style student desk — and asked whether the monitor would physically fit. After he measured **16.5 inches of vertical clearance**, the assistant compared minimum stand heights: the Dell S2725QC sits at ~15.75", clearing by ~0.75", while 32" options (Samsung M8 ~17.2") and even the Apple/LG 5K units (~16.0–16.3") were too tight. Conclusion: the Dell S2725QC fits with its included stand.

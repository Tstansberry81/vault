# SAT Practice Test 4 Review and Hard Problems (chat)

A brief, low-substance ChatGPT exchange from the fall of [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s senior year (Sept 2025), squarely in the middle of his [[College Search]] / college-application push. He twice pasted the identical query — "sat full length practice test 4 reviews and hard problems" — and got back generic, web-aggregated commentary about **Official Digital SAT Practice Test 4** (College Board / Bluebook).

The model's answer contained no original work: it summarized forum sentiment (Test 4 is "more representative" than the easier early tests; its math module is widely flagged as harder, with the last few questions in the harder adaptive module being the spike), and listed *types* of hard problems rather than solving any — quadratics and completing the square / vertex form, discriminant and parameter problems (e.g. greatest `b` for which `-x² + bx - 676 = 0` has no real solution), 3D surface-area/volume geometry, and subtle reading-evidence inference questions. It closed by offering to walk through five hard problems step by step, an offer Traveler did not take up in this thread.

What it reveals is narrow but real: Traveler was actively prepping for the **Digital SAT** in September 2025, scouting which sections to expect difficulty in (math, especially adaptive Module 2) before sitting practice tests. This is consistent with his strong quantitative orientation — the math focus matches his interest in [[IB Math (SL)]], the [[UVA and the Quant Question]], and his finance/[[Investment Club]] track. It adds context to his standardized-testing record alongside his [[Academic Record]] and [[Extracurriculars and Achievements]].

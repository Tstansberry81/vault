# LRAS, PPC, and Macro Schools of Thought (chat)

A short, exam-oriented [[IB Economics (SL)]] study session from April 2025, in which Traveler drilled core macroeconomics concepts — almost certainly revision for the macro unit / Paper 1. The conversation is a sequence of crisp definitional questions, with no follow-up debate, suggesting he was consolidating notes rather than wrestling with the material.

## What he asked
- **LRAS vs. PPC** — how the Long-Run Aggregate Supply curve relates to the Production Possibilities Curve. Key link drawn: a point on the vertical LRAS corresponds to producing *on* the PPC (full employment, efficient use of resources); being inside the PPC = left of LRAS = underutilization. Outward shifts of both represent economic growth driven by the same factors (capital investment, technology, labor force).
- **Depression vs. recession** — recession as a few quarters of decline (the "two consecutive quarters of negative GDP" rule of thumb, e.g. the 2008 crisis ~18 months); depression as a years-long, >10% GDP collapse (the 1930s Great Depression). All depressions are recessions; not all recessions become depressions.
- **The five relationships in the goods-and-services market** — the circular-flow interactions among households, firms, government, and the rest of the world (plus financial markets): consumption, taxes-for-public-goods, government procurement, exports/imports, and saving/borrowing.
- **Keynesian vs. Classical readings of the LRAS graph** — the heart of the session. The three-segment Keynesian curve (horizontal at slack, upward-sloping near capacity, vertical at full employment) implying markets need government intervention to reach full employment, versus the always-vertical Classical LRAS with flexible wages/prices that self-corrects and minimizes intervention.

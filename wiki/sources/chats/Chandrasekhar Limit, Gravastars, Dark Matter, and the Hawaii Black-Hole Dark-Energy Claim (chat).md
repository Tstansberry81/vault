# Chandrasekhar Limit, Gravastars, Dark Matter, and the Hawaii Black-Hole Dark-Energy Claim

A long astrophysics curiosity dive ([[ChatGPT]], gpt-5) where [[Traveler Stansberry]] free-explores stellar death and cosmology far beyond the [[IB Physics (HL)]] syllabus — pure [[Intellectual Interests]], not coursework. He starts with the **Chandrasekhar limit** (~1.4 solar masses, the point where electron degeneracy pressure loses to gravity), then the **Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff limit** (~2–3 solar masses, where neutron degeneracy pressure fails and a black hole forms), grounded in the Pauli exclusion principle.

He then asks for a catalogue of "weird stellar objects" — white dwarfs, neutron stars, pulsars, magnetars, quark stars, boson stars, Thorne–Żytkow objects, strange matter, primordial black holes, and **gravastars**, which he singles out for a deeper pass. The standout technical thread: he asks for gravastar **density and pressure components linked to the TOV equation**, and works through how plugging the vacuum-energy equation of state $P = -\rho c^2$ into the TOV factor $(1 + P/\rho c^2)$ gives zero, so $dP/dr = 0$ — a constant-negative-pressure de Sitter-like core that "cheats" gravity instead of fighting it, plus the anisotropic-pressure shell term $\frac{2}{r}(P_t - P_r)$.

He covers **dark matter** (flat rotation curves, Vera Rubin, the Bullet Cluster, WIMPs/axions/sterile neutrinos, MOND as the rival) and **dark energy** (Type Ia supernovae as standard candles, the cosmological constant, the $10^{120}$ vacuum-energy discrepancy he is told is "the worst theoretical prediction in physics," quintessence and the Big Rip), then the **Schwarzschild radius** $r_s = 2GM/c^2$ derived from setting escape velocity to $c$.

The session ends on a verified-with-web-search treatment of the **2023 University of Hawaii "cosmological coupling" claim** — that black holes may gain mass as $M_{BH} \propto a^k$ with $k \approx 3$, making $\rho_{BH}$ constant and mimicking dark energy.

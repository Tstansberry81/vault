# Narrow Road Identity Essay - IB Grading Across Three Drafts

A [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]] comparative essay for [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 2]] (Mr. Mezeske, due 3 December 2025), workshopped through three full drafts against the IB HL Essay rubric. The prompt: *how Richard Flanagan uses identity to illustrate the conflict of [[Fate and Free Will|fate and free will]]*. This is one of Traveler's most iterative, revision-focused English chats, and it ends with a concrete result and a metacognitive breakthrough about his own analytical weakness.

## The argument
Traveler's thesis contrasts two modes of [[Identity and Its Collapse|identity construction]]: **Dorrigo Evans** builds identity through adaptive, chosen **roles** ("Big Fella" in the POW camp, family man with Ella), an act of free will; **Nakamura** anchors his to the rigid ideology of the "Japanese spirit," a vessel for **fate** that excuses his war crimes. The claim: identity formed by belief in free will is more advantageous for *survival* than acceptance of fate. This maps directly onto Traveler's own [[Political and Economic Views|determinism]] and [[Individual vs. Society|individualist]] instincts — though here he argues the *belief* in free will is what matters, not its metaphysical truth. Connects to his other Narrow Road work in [[Narrow Road Free Will Essay - Identity, Darky, and Nakamura (chat)]] and [[Antiderivatives, the Long March, and a Narrow Road Circle-Line Essay (chat)]].

## The grading arc
- **Draft 1: 15/20.** A=4, B=3, C=4, D=4. ChatGPT (as a deliberately harsh IB examiner) flags the core flaw: Traveler slides into *moral judgment* ("if Nakamura acted with free will he could have saved lives") and counterfactual ethics instead of literary analysis. Criterion B capped because he summarizes before analyzing and rarely names *technique*.
- A key reframing: ChatGPT pushes him from **choice → moral goodness** (textually false — Dorrigo cheats and lies) to **choice → moral agency / responsibility**. Dorrigo's free will makes him *answerable*; Nakamura's fatalism lets him *avoid answering* until too late.
- **Drafts 2 & 3: 16/20.** Criterion B rose 3→4 after he embedded analysis and added irony framing. Persistent leaks: "Flanagan *decided*" (authorial mind-reading, which examiners dock), a bloated block paragraph, and naming *effects* but not *techniques* (method → effect → meaning).
- **Actual result: 17/20** on the midterm — "strong 6-ish energy," confirming his ideas are IB-ready and the growth lever is precision/restraint, not insight.

## The metacognitive payoff
At the end Traveler names his real weakness: *"I'm good at recognizing larger themes and ideas, but I don't know all the fancy nomenclature to describe how the author is doing it."* He sees meaning but can't name the machinery. ChatGPT's fix reframes "technique" as "**choice**" (why show it *this* way?) and gives a 12-item reusable toolkit — narrative perspective, interior monologue, juxtaposition, irony, symbolic naming, motif, retrospective framing, structural placement, register, moral distance, agency contrast, delayed revelation — with steal-able phrasings. This is the clearest articulation in the corpus of how Traveler's [[Intellectual Profile|analytical strength]] (conceptual fluency) outruns his technical vocabulary, and how he closes that gap mechanically.

# Charles Bon's Parentage in Absalom, Absalom! (chat)

A short, tonally split ChatGPT session from April 2025 that begins as genuine [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English]] coursework on [[Absalom, Absalom!]] and then drifts into off-task throwaway requests. The Faulkner portion is the substantive part.

## The coursework
Traveler asks **how Charles Bon learned that Thomas Sutpen is his father**. The answer he gets: Bon's mother — Sutpen's first wife, whom Sutpen annulled and abandoned upon discovering her family's African ancestry — is the source of the revelation. Sutpen then built Sutpen's Hundred in Mississippi; Bon later befriends Henry Sutpen at the University of Mississippi and is brought into the family, though Sutpen recognizes but never openly acknowledges him. The summary stresses how Bon's heritage drives the novel's machinery of identity, race, and the destructive Sutpen legacy — themes central to [[The Moral Impact of Slavery]], [[Identity and Its Collapse]], and [[Memory and Trauma]].

He then photographs a dense passage (Quentin Compson reflecting on the **possibility of incest** between Bon and Judith Sutpen) and asks for an explanation. The model unpacks the forbidden-desire/taboo tension, Quentin's paralysis as a passive narrator who "could have spoken now, but did not," and the Southern Gothic motif of decay. This is the same close-reading-from-a-phone-photo workflow Traveler uses elsewhere for [[Absalom, Absalom!]] (cf. [[Old Sabine in Absalom, Absalom! (chat)]], [[Pandemic Economics and Absalom Image Requests (chat)]]), confirming Faulkner was a live text in his IB English reading at this point.

## The off-task drift
After the Faulkner work, the chat veers entirely: requests for generic "pickup lines" (clean, then "spicy," then eye-themed, then suggestive), and a closing trivia question about the identity of "A" in *Pretty Little Liars*. These have no coursework or intellectual value — typical evidence of a teenager using one thread for both homework and idle messing-around.

## What it shows
The valuable signal is narrow but real: Traveler was actively studying [[Absalom, Absalom!]] at the close of junior year, leaning on AI to untangle Faulkner's notoriously opaque syntax and the Sutpen family's race-and-incest plot. The rest is noise riding along in the same conversation.

# Pandemic Economics and Absalom Image Requests (chat)

A grab-bag April 2025 ChatGPT session (gpt-4o) that straddles two of Traveler's courses at once: [[IB Economics (SL)]] macro review and [[Absalom, Absalom!]] essay/visual work. It reads as ad-hoc homework support rather than a focused project, but it usefully shows the range of what he was juggling in spring of junior year.

## Economics half
Traveler opens with macro questions that map directly onto the IB Econ syllabus:
- **Why the pandemic collapsed the economy** — the model walks through lockdowns, supply-chain disruption, demand collapse, investment uncertainty, and global interconnectivity.
- **CARES Act vs. American Rescue Plan Act** — a side-by-side of the two U.S. stimulus packages ($2.2T vs. $1.9T), useful as a real-world fiscal-policy case study.
- **Cost-push inflation** — causes (input costs, wages, supply shocks, currency depreciation, taxes), the supply-reduction mechanism, stagflation risk, and the contrast with demand-pull inflation, with the 1970s OPEC embargo and the 2022 Russia–Ukraine war as examples.

This is core SL macroeconomics — inflation types, fiscal stimulus, aggregate demand/supply shocks — and connects to his broader [[Political and Economic Views]] and the empirical/finance bent that points toward [[UVA and the Quant Question]].

## IB diploma logistics
A short detour: he asks what is required to graduate with the IB Diploma and how the components tie to exams. The answer covers the six-subject/HL-SL structure, the core ([[Theory of Knowledge]], [[Extended Essay (Economics)|Extended Essay]], CAS), the 45-point scale (42 subject + 3 core), the 24-point minimum, and failing conditions. Confirms he was thinking concretely about the [[Academic Record|diploma]] mechanics that govern his whole program.

## Absalom, Absalom! half
The larger chunk is work on [[Absalom, Absalom!]], the Faulkner novel central to his [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|senior English]]. He hunts for citations and quotes for what is almost certainly an essay or [[Individual Oral (IO)|oral]]:
- A **portrait/description of Clytie (Clytemnestra) Sutpen** — he pastes a detailed character note (the enslaved Sutpen daughter, named for the Greek queen, the enigmatic figure who presides over the house's fall). He repeatedly asks for an AI image of her, which never generates — the model stalls across a multi-hour gap, an early example of image-gen failure he keeps re-prompting through.
- **Henry's repudiation of his birthright in the library** — he asks for a page number (the model gives shifting, unreliable answers: ~p.212, then ~p.176–178) and a quote, then requests library-set images of the father-son quarrel "on the night before Christmas."
- **Henry, Bon, and Sutpen leaving for war** — page-number request (~pp.76–80) plus an image of the Bull Run / University company scene.

This shows Traveler mining the text for specific dramatized moments — the library repudiation, the ride into the night, the departure for war — themes of inheritance, loyalty over blood, and the fall of the house, which tie into [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]], [[The Moral Impact of Slavery]], and [[Identity and Its Collapse]].

> [!warning] Reliability
> The page numbers and quotes the model supplied for *Absalom, Absalom!* are inconsistent and likely fabricated (it contradicts itself on the repudiation page). Treat any citation that traces back to this chat as unverified against the actual Vintage edition.

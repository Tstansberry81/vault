# UVA Dorm Tiers, IB HL Credits, and a Steam-Calorimetry Thermo Marathon

A sprawling April 2026 chat that starts as committed-senior logistics and pivots into one of Traveler's longest [[IB Physics (HL)]] thermodynamics tutoring sessions. With the [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]] decision behind him, he opens with "uva dorm ranking" and a near-comic prayer — "i j hope i get ac dude" — getting the standard tiering (renovated McCormick Road old dorms on top for social life, Gibbons/Shannon for comfort, Gooch/Dillard suites, Brown/Hereford residential colleges).

He then drills the IB credit math that matters for [[Academic Record|his diploma]]: UVA gives credit for **HL exams scored 5-7 only**, SL is "decorative achievement," and the IB Diploma as a package earns nothing on its own — credit is per-exam. He maps the high-value HLs (Math AA, Econ, sciences, language) against his own profile, relevant because he's a finance kid eyeing McIntire. A pointed admission follows: "physics hl might cook me. electromagnetism and induction are hard ash," which sets up the rest.

The bulk is genuine [[IB Physics (HL)]] thermodynamics teaching. He works a steam-into-water calorimetry problem (15 g steam at 120 deg C into 180 g water at 22 deg C, answer 71 deg C), and crucially keeps asking *why* — untangling that there are two "waters," that condensation latent heat (34,500 J) dominates the small mc-delta-T terms, that phase change is pinned at 100 deg C, and that water is weird because of hydrogen bonding. He probes sign conventions for delta-T, the Ek/Ep hierarchy across solid/liquid/gas (correcting his backwards intuition that solids have high potential energy), rate of thermal transfer (conduction kA-delta-T/L, convection, radiation), a steady-state-conduction rod problem, the second law and why no cyclic engine hits 100% efficiency, Carnot derivations, and entropy as a state function. He gets a Paper-1 entropy MCQ wrong (isobaric vs isochoric ΔS), and the tutor walks him to the correct insight: the final *states* differ because volume changes, so the isobaric process has larger entropy change. This connects directly to the EM/induction anxiety he raised in [[What is EMF - Work, Refraction, Interference, Damping, and Escape Velocity (chat)|the companion physics chat]].

Mid-stream he submits his handwritten thermo notes for grading (scored 7.5/10 — strong coverage, sloppy wording and typos), an Excel "multiple lines in a cell" question (Alt/Option+Enter), and a tangent on [[The Elephant Vanishes]] / [[IB Japanese (SL)|Murakami]]: the wind-up bird as fate, and **narrative disruption** and chronological distortion across the collection ("Sleep," "TV People," "The Second Bakery Attack," "Barn Burning"). This is the same conceptual machinery behind his [[Paper 2 Study Guide|Paper 2]] pairing of Murakami with [[Beloved]] under [[Identity and Its Collapse]] and [[Memory and Trauma]]. The chat reveals a student transactionally assessing what IB "bought" him while still grinding the HL Physics that genuinely threatens his score — and the [[Fate and Free Will]] reading of Murakami's machine-bird is no accident for this [[Political and Economic Views|determinist]].

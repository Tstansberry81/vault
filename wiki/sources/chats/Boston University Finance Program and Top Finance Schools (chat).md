# Boston University Finance Program and Top Finance Schools

A short [[College Search]] conversation from March 2025 in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] scouted undergraduate **finance** programs — first drilling into Boston University specifically, then asking for a ranked list of the best undergraduate finance schools nationally. It sits squarely in his stated college trajectory: a finance major (with a math minor open) headed ultimately to [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]].

## What he asked
1. "Tell me about Boston University's financial program."
2. "How good is Boston University's financial program?"
3. "Top 10 colleges with best undergraduate financial programs."

## Key content
- **BU:** ChatGPT framed BU as strong and well-regarded across undergrad and grad finance — the undergraduate Finance & Financial Management major plus graduate tracks like the MS in Financial Management (MSFM, ~$100k/yr tuition) and the MS in Mathematical Finance & Financial Technology (MSMFT), the latter with concentrations in Asset Management, FinTech, Quantitative Analytics, and Risk Management. Industry connections, internships in investment banking / asset management / fintech, and a large alumni network were the cited strengths; cost and shrinking year-over-year aid were the caveats.
- **Top-10 list** (repeated nearly verbatim across two prompts): Penn (Wharton), NYU (Stern), Cornell, Michigan (Ross), Georgetown, Carnegie Mellon (Tepper), UC Berkeley (Haas), **University of Virginia**, Notre Dame (Mendoza), Emory (Goizueta). Notably the model flagged Carnegie Mellon/Tepper as the standout for **quantitative and computational finance** — financial engineering and algorithmic trading.

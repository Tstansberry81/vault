# Emancipation Proclamation, Thaddeus Stevens, Plessy, and Jim Crow

A short, focused study session walking the US Civil War → Reconstruction → Jim Crow causation chain for [[IB History (HL)]]. Traveler fired off four terse prompts ("why was the emancipation proclamation so important," "why was thaddeus stevens unsatisfied with the three civil war amendments," "plessey case," "examples of jim crow laws") and got back encyclopedic answers framed explicitly for IB essay use — ChatGPT repeatedly offers to "tighten it into a thesis-ready explanation" or "compress this into an IB-style paragraph linking Reconstruction → Plessy → Jim Crow → Brown for a clean causation chain."

The content is a tight survey of the period:
- **Emancipation Proclamation (Jan 1, 1863):** redefined the war from "preserve the Union" to ending slavery, killed Confederate hopes of British/French recognition (the "cotton is persuasive" PR problem), collapsed the Southern labor system, and opened Union ranks to ~180,000 Black soldiers (~10% of forces). Crucially it did *not* end slavery nationwide — only rebel territory — paving the way for the **13th Amendment** (1865).
- **Thaddeus Stevens** and the Radical Republicans: dissatisfied because the 13th/14th/15th Amendments were "liberation without leverage." He wanted **land redistribution** (confiscating Confederate estates for freedmen), sustained federal military enforcement, and the South treated as conquered territory. The throughline — "rights without enforcement are decorative," "power adapts" — recurs across the whole chat.
- **Plessy v. Ferguson (1896):** the "separate but equal" doctrine, Homer Plessy's deliberate test case, the 7–1 ruling, John Marshall Harlan's "Our Constitution is color-blind" dissent, and its 58-year reign until **Brown v. Board of Education** (1954).
- **Jim Crow laws:** categorized into segregation, voting restrictions (literacy tests, poll taxes, grandfather clauses, white-only primaries), economic control (sharecropping, convict leasing), social/behavioral rules, and criminal-justice abuse exploiting the 13th Amendment's forced-labor-as-punishment loophole.

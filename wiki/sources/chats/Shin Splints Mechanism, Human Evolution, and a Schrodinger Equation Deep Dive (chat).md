# Shin Splints Mechanism, Human Evolution, and a Schrodinger Equation Deep Dive

A curiosity-driven ramble that starts in [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s body and ends in quantum mechanics. He opens asking about **shin splints** — likely tied to his [[March Cut and Recomp Plan - Incline Cardio, 1800 Calories, and Back Training (chat)|cardio and recomp routine]] — but immediately refuses the surface answer and demands the actual medical mechanism: medial tibial stress syndrome as a bone stress reaction, microdamage and remodeling, and periosteum inflammation as the true pain source. He then follows the thread into *why* the tibia is loaded with pain receptors, prompting a tangent on nociceptor distribution and the evolutionary logic of monitoring high-risk, high-contact tissue. This "give me the real mechanism, not the bro answer" pattern is characteristic of his [[Intellectual Interests]].

That spins into a speculative riff on **future human evolution** — screen-adapted eyes, shrinking jaws, immune recalibration, metabolic mismatch with abundance, and tech-assisted (CRISPR/neural-implant) "evolution" where humans grab the wheel from natural selection. The framing that evolution "only cares whether you survived to reproduce" lands near his hard-nosed, [[Political and Economic Views|individualist]] worldview.

The back half is pure [[IB Physics (HL)]] / [[IB Math (SL)]] cross-training, and the most substantive part: the divergence operator (∇·F), the full set of Maxwell's equations (with \(c = 1/\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}\) falling out as the speed of light), confirmation that \(dv/dt\) is acceleration, the Sun's temperature and luminosity (\(L_\odot \approx 3.83\times10^{26}\) W, \(L = 4\pi R^2 \sigma T^4\)), and then an extended walk through the **Schrödinger equation**: why \(i\) appears (oscillation, probability conservation, unitarity), a term-by-term breakdown of \(i\hbar\,\partial\psi/\partial t = \hat{H}\psi\), and how Schrödinger "reverse-engineered" it from de Broglie waves and classical energy rather than deriving it cleanly.

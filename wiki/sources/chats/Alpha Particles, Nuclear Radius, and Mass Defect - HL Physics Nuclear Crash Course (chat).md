# Alpha Particles, Nuclear Radius, and Mass Defect - HL Physics Nuclear Crash Course

A wide-ranging tutoring sweep through the [[IB Physics (HL)]] nuclear/quantum unit, conducted as a chain of one-word and short prompts ("fermi radius", "compton scattering", "gamma rays", "strong nuclear force") that build a near-complete topic review. Traveler is working through nuclear and atomic-physics concepts the night before, or in revision for, a test, asking the model to define terms and explain the physical reasoning behind each formula.

## What he covered
- **Rutherford gold foil experiment** and the [[alpha particle]] as a positively charged helium nucleus (2p + 2n) used to probe atomic structure — most pass through (empty space), a few backscatter (dense positive nucleus), killing the plum-pudding model.
- **Fermi radius / nuclear radius**: $R = R_0 A^{1/3}$ with $R_0 \approx 1.2$ fm, and the constant-density argument ($\text{Vol} \propto A \Rightarrow R \propto A^{1/3}$).
- **Compton scattering** ($\Delta\lambda = \frac{h}{m_e c}(1-\cos\theta)$), the **photoelectric effect** ($hf = \phi + KE_{max}$, threshold frequency), wave equations ($f = c/\lambda$), **gamma rays**, the **strong nuclear force**, stable vs unstable nuclei / band of stability, EMF, and a **diffraction sizing problem** ($d = \lambda/\sin\theta$) for a nickel nucleus (answer ~8.0 fm, using the first minimum at 35°, not the 50° maximum).

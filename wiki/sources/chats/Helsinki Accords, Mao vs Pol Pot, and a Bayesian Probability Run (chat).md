# Helsinki Accords, Mao vs Pol Pot, and a Bayesian Probability Run

A mixed [[coursework-history|history]]-then-[[coursework-math|math]] study session by [[Traveler Stansberry]] that starts in [[IB History (HL)]] territory and drifts into a surprisingly deep probability tangent. Two distinct subjects sit side by side, typical of how he uses ChatGPT as a one-stop review tool.

The history half covers two Cold War / authoritarian-regime topics. First, the **Helsinki Accords (1975)** and their aftermath: the three "baskets" (security/borders, economic cooperation, human rights), why the USSR signed (border legitimacy) and how the human-rights basket backfired — empowering dissidents (Helsinki Watch, Charter 77, Andrei Sakharov), undermining Soviet legitimacy, and feeding the long arc toward the 1991 collapse. This sits alongside his broader détente reviewing. Second, a **Mao Zedong vs Pol Pot** comparison: shared communist social-engineering ideology and mass death (Great Leap Forward famine ~30-45M; Cambodian Genocide ~1.5-2M, ~25% of the population), but contrasting scale — Mao kept a functioning state, Pol Pot's "Year Zero" reset imploded. These map onto [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]] and his recurring interest in how ideologies justify coercion ([[Political and Economic Views]]).

The math half is more revealing of his [[Intellectual Interests]]. He moved through **binomial distribution graphs** (shape vs p, mean = np, variance np(1−p), convergence to normal via the Central Limit Theorem), then asked how to *derive* the mean — and got the clean indicator-variable / linearity-of-expectation proof (X = ΣXᵢ, E(Xᵢ) = p, so E(X) = np) rather than the brute-force summation. He then chained into **Laplace's Rule of Succession** ((s+1)/(n+2), the sunrise problem) and the **Beta distribution** as its Bayesian foundation — Beta(1,1) as a uniform prior, the conjugate-prior relationship to the binomial, and posterior updating. This is well beyond [[IB Math (SL)]] core content and signals his quant lean toward [[UVA and the Quant Question]] and finance applications (estimating conversion or stock-up probabilities).

The session shows Traveler treating disparate subjects as a single sprint, and pushing the math past curriculum into Bayesian inference of his own accord — the same statistical instinct he applies to [[Investment Club]] and [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] work.

# Momentum Conservation Problem Set - 2D Collisions and Recoil

A [[IB Physics (HL)]] homework session from September 2025 in which Traveler photographs a sequence of textbook momentum problems and has ChatGPT solve and explain each. The chat is purely worked-solution coursework help: he uploads images, the model dismantles them, and he occasionally confirms his own scribbled answers were right ("roger got it all right").

## Problems worked
- **2D billiard collision** — two equal-mass balls; conservation of momentum split into vertical and horizontal components. Ball A scatters at 1.0 m/s @ 45°, Ball B recoils at ~1.47 m/s, ~29° below horizontal. KE check (2m → 1.58m) shows the collision is **inelastic**.
- **Recoiling cannon** — 15 kg shell at 420 m/s, 20° above horizontal, fired from a 1500 kg mount that only moves horizontally. Recoil ≈ 3.94 m/s backward; cannon KE ≈ 1.2×10⁴ J; vertical momentum stays zero because the ground supplies an external upward impulse.
- **Bug vs. windshield** — Newton's third law conceptual question: equal force, equal-magnitude impulse, but vastly different accelerations (a = F/m) because the bug's mass is tiny. He asked for a beginner-level explanation here.
- **Fan-and-sail boat** — classic "internal forces cancel" trick: aiming a fan at your own sail does nothing (forces internal to the boat system); pointing the fan out the back works like a rocket.
- **Projectile-into-toboggan** — find horizontal landing distance D = v₀√(2H/g), then a sticking (perfectly inelastic) collision: v_f = M₁/(M₁+M₂)·v₀.
- **Child-jumps-on-sled** (two variants, 50 kg and 30 kg children) — inelastic momentum conservation giving 2.3 m/s and 1.5 m/s. Full multi-part version covers KE after sticking (90 J), the child jumping back off (sled → 2.4 m/s), and KE rising to 144 J — explained as the child doing **positive work** via internal chemical energy, not energy appearing from nowhere.

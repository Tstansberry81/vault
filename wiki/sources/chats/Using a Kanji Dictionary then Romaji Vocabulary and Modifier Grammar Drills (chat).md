# Using a Kanji Dictionary then Romaji Vocabulary and Modifier Grammar Drills

A long [[IB Japanese (SL)]] self-study session that opens as a how-to on kanji dictionaries and drifts into a rapid-fire vocabulary check-and-correct, then closes with a focused grammar review of three core structures. It shows Traveler using ChatGPT as a patient (if sardonic) tutor for the language he finds hardest, and reveals where his actual weak points are.

## Kanji dictionary methods
The chat begins with "how to use a kanji dictionary," answered with the four lookup methods: radical lookup, stroke-count lookup, reading lookup (on'yomi/kun'yomi), and digital lookup (Jisho.org, Imiwa, handwriting/camera input). The framing is practical — use digital tools to survive while learning radicals so you don't stay helpless.

## Romaji vocabulary drilling
The bulk of the session is Traveler firing single romaji strings and getting back the correct kana/kanji, meaning, and usage. Notably, most of his guesses are *misspelled* — vowels dropped or swapped — and the assistant repeatedly flags romaji as the culprit:
- **kyushu** → 九州 "Nine Provinces"
- **kikikaki** → corrected to 聞き書き (kikigaki, "writing down what someone says")
- **ijyo** → disambiguated 以上 (more than) vs 異常 (abnormal)
- **tskurimasu** → つくります (作る, to make)
- **to sell** → 売る / 売ります, with 買う (buy) flagged as the easy confusion
- **otsuri** → お釣り (change/money back)
- **komakaiokane** → 細かいお金 (small change)
- **sugi yoko** → corrected to すぐ横 (right next to); "sugi" would mean cedar tree
- **heta** → 下手 (bad at, paired with 上手)

Several of his attempted sentences ("yoi urikata ga arimasuka," "korece jiwo kakimasu") are judged understandable but unnatural, with particle errors (ce vs で) corrected. The recurring theme — that romaji "hides pitch, particles, and intent" — is a small window into why this written/spoken gap trips him up. The sales-heavy vocabulary (売る, 売り方, 売上) is incidentally on-brand given [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] and [[Investment Club]].

## Grammar review: とき, modifiers, んです
The session ends on substantive grammar: the tense-sensitive とき (time) clause, noun modifiers, and explanatory 〜んです framing. Traveler singles out **modifiers** as "exceptionally difficult for me bc it's so backwards as an English speaker" and asks for examples, translation questions, and an annotated answer key. The tutor reframes the English "the book that I bought" as Japanese "I yesterday bought book" — describe first, name later, no relative pronoun — and runs him through five graded translation drills. This self-aware request for targeted, escalating practice is characteristic of how he studies.

This connects to his broader [[IB Japanese (SL)]] coursework and fits the pattern across his chats of using AI as an on-demand drill partner rather than passively consuming explanations.

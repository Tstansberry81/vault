A two-subject cram session from the start of senior year (Sept 2025). Traveler opens with an IB-book-style macro review, then pivots mid-chat to refreshing kanji for his upcoming Japanese exam.

# Macro AD–AS review
He asks for "aggregate supply and demand, the LRAS, everything straight from the IB book." The reply is a structured [[IB Economics (SL)]] crib: **AD = C + I + G + (X − M)**, downward-sloping via the wealth, interest-rate, and net-export effects; **SRAS** upward-sloping and shifted by wage/raw-material/oil costs and supply shocks; and **LRAS** in its two flavors — **Keynesian** (flat → upward → vertical three-phase) vs **New Classical / monetarist** (always vertical at Y_f). It covers short- vs long-run equilibrium, inflationary vs deflationary gaps, and the demand-side (fiscal/monetary) vs supply-side (interventionist vs market-based) policy split, closing with IB evaluation hooks (time lags, confidence dependence, supply-side slowness). This is the same LRAS/schools-of-thought material he drills elsewhere — see [[LRAS, PPC, and Macro Schools of Thought (chat)|LRAS, PPC, and Macro Schools of Thought]] and [[Demand-Side Econ and TOK Values Knowledge Production (chat)|Demand-Side Econ and TOK Values Knowledge Production]]. The macro fluency here is the foundation for his [[Extended Essay (Economics)]] on pandemic fiscal policy.

# Japanese: a revealing self-correction
He says he's been taking [[IB Japanese (SL)]] "for 12 years," is in "Japanese IB SL 5" on the third IB book, and asks how to refresh kanji. The assistant gives an SL-level plan (~400–500 kanji, spaced repetition, handwriting, mnemonics).

> [!warning] Contradiction
> Traveler then states he is actually **taking the IB Japanese ab initio exam**, not SL/HL. The [[IB Japanese (SL)]] page treats his Japanese as standard SL; this chat indicates he is sitting the **ab initio** exam, where kanji is "icing, not cake" (~80–100 recognition-only characters, kana fluency, furigana support). The "12 years" of study and the ab initio exam track sit in tension and should be reconciled on the Japanese page.

The kanji refresh then becomes a tooling exercise: he requests ~100 essential ab initio kanji as **Quizlet-importable CSV flashcards**, then a printable **PDF table** (Kanji | Meaning | Reading), insisting it "not cover anything" (no flip/hide cards). The first PDF rendered Japanese as black squares (■) — a ReportLab font/encoding failure — which he flagged with a screenshot, prompting a regenerated PDF using a CJK font (HeiseiMin/HeiseiKakuGo).

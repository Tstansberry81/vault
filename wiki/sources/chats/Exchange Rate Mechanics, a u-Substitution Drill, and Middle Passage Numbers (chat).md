# Exchange Rate Mechanics, a u-Substitution Drill, and Middle Passage Numbers

A short three-subject homework session (titled "US Exports and Currency") spanning [[IB Economics (SL)]], [[IB Math (SL)]], and [[IB History (HL)]] — a representative cross-section of Traveler's nightly mixed-subject ChatGPT use.

## Foreign exchange mechanics (Econ SL)
Traveler worked through the FX-market logic linking the balance of payments to currency value, using a US–China (USD/CNY) frame. The chat walked three scenarios:
- **US exports rise** → Chinese buyers convert yuan to dollars → demand for USD shifts right → **USD appreciates, CNY depreciates**.
- **Prices of US exports rise** (a distinct question he corrected mid-stream) → US goods more expensive → quantity of exports falls → demand for USD falls → **USD depreciates, CNY appreciates**.
- **Prices of US imports fall** → Americans buy more foreign goods → supply of USD rises → **USD depreciates, foreign currency appreciates**.

The model flagged the real-world nuance that the **People's Bank of China** manages (does not fully float) the yuan, so a clean textbook diagram assumes a flexible exchange rate with no intervention. The takeaway symmetry — higher exports → appreciation, higher imports → depreciation — is core [[IB Economics (SL)]] exchange-rate content.

## u-substitution integration drill (Math SL)
The bulk of the session was a worked set of indefinite integrals, all solved by u-substitution (chain rule in reverse), matching his then-current [[IB Math (SL)]] integration unit:
- $\int (2x-1)(x^2-x)^2\,dx = \tfrac{1}{3}(x^2-x)^3 + C$
- $\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x^2) = 2x\cos(x^2)$, hence $\int x\cos(x^2)\,dx = \tfrac{1}{2}\sin(x^2)+C$
- $\int \frac{4x-6}{5-3x+x^2}\,dx = 2\ln|5-3x+x^2|+C$ (numerator = derivative of denominator)
- $\int x^2 e^{x^3+1}\,dx = \tfrac{1}{3}e^{x^3+1}+C$; $\int -2e^{1-2x}\,dx = e^{1-2x}+C$
- $\int \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{\sqrt{x}}\,dx = 2e^{\sqrt{x}}+C$; $\int \frac{x}{2-x^2}\,dx = -\tfrac{1}{2}\ln|2-x^2|+C$
- $\int \frac{(\ln x)^3}{x}\,dx = \tfrac{(\ln x)^4}{4}+C$; $\int (2x-1)e^{x^2-x}\,dx = e^{x^2-x}+C$; $\int \cos^5 x \sin x\,dx = -\tfrac{\cos^6 x}{6}+C$

Several were posted as photos of a worksheet, and he repeatedly asked the model to redo answers "with u-sub" rather than pattern-spotting — i.e., he wanted the full procedural method, not just the result. The recurring pedagogy here is the "hence" move: differentiate something so the integrand's numerator is recognizable as a denominator's derivative.

## Middle Passage figures (History HL)
The session closed with a factual query on the transatlantic slave trade: ~12.5 million Africans embarked, ~10.7–10.8 million survived to the Americas, ~1.7–2 million dying on the ocean voyage (12–15% mortality), per the Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade Database. Distribution: Brazil ~4.8M, Caribbean ~4M, Spanish America ~1.5M, British North America/future US only ~388,000–450,000.

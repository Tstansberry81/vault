# Death of a Salesman Essay - IB HL Criterion Feedback on Fatherhood and Identity

Traveler pastes a full Paper-2-style essay on [[Death of a Salesman]] and asks ChatGPT (running in his self-built "Mode 5 / Read-Write Editor" rubric mode) to grade it against the [[IB English HL|English Curriculum 2022-2026]] criteria. This is a single-pass essay-feedback session, the kind he runs constantly for his [[English Curriculum 2022-2026]] coursework.

## The essay
His thesis: the character development of Willy and Biff shows that the identity of American men is tied to their ethical values, idea of success, and work ethic — and that the **collapse of identity is aligned with the role of father figures**. The argument runs through three parallel body sections (morals → success → work ethic), reading Biff as inheriting Willy's flaws, and Willy as inheriting his own warped manhood from his brother Ben (the only male model he had). The essay's strongest move is its fatalist coda: "there was nothing the characters could do to stop it... Their identities were set in stone by Ben and his notion of what a man should be." That determinism is pure Traveler — it maps directly onto his own [[Core Convictions|determinist]] worldview, and the essay's core themes ([[Masculinity]], [[Fate and Free Will]], [[Identity and Its Collapse]]) are recurring obsessions across his work.

Key textual anchors he uses: "You fake! You phony little fake!", "Be liked and you will never want," Biff's pen-grabbing epiphany ("what am I doing in an office... when all I want is out there"), and the seed-planting metaphor for Willy's failed legacy. He notably leans on the detail that Biff loses a **University of Virginia** football scholarship by failing math — a wink given Traveler is [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA-bound]].

## The feedback
The model returns a criterion-by-criterion IB markband breakdown:
- **A — Knowledge & Understanding: 8/10.** Strong thematic grasp, but reads Miller's critique as purely *moral* rather than *societal*; missing the postwar capitalist / disillusionment context ([[Consumerism and Alienation]]).
- **B — Analysis & Interpretation: 7/10.** Solid evidence but too much summary of plot vs. analysis of *technique function*; suggests adding stagecraft/structure analysis (flashbacks, hallucinations, Ben as a static figure).
- **C — Focus & Organization: 8/10.** Clear morals→success→work-ethic→synthesis spine; paragraphs should end on interpretive links, not summaries.
- **D — Language: 7.5/10.** Clean academic diction undercut by repetitive sentence openers ("Biff also has," "Willy also") and casual phrasing ("it's not," "went out west for a while"); embed quotes rather than letting them overpower.

**Overall predicted IB band: 7 (26–28/40)** — an A-level essay sitting just below the "excellent" descriptor, held back by needing less retelling and more "why Miller constructs meaning this way."

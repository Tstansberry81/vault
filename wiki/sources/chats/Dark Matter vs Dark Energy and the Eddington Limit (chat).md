# Dark Matter vs Dark Energy and the Eddington Limit

A short, purely curiosity-driven May 2026 astrophysics chat — no homework prompt, no grading, just Traveler asking cosmology questions for their own sake, two days after [[Dirichlet Integral, Viscosity, the Schwarzschild Radius, and a Keeper Movie Check (chat)|the Dirichlet/Schwarzschild grab-bag]] and well outside anything [[IB Physics (HL)]] tests. It's a clean specimen of his [[Intellectual Interests]] running on pure want-to-know.

He opens with "dark energy vs dark matter" and gets the full contrast: **dark matter** (~27% of the universe) clumps around galaxies and adds attractive gravity, evidenced by flat galaxy rotation curves, gravitational lensing, the Bullet Cluster, and structure formation in the early universe, with candidates from WIMPs and axions to MACHOs and primordial black holes; **dark energy** (~68%) acts globally, driving accelerated cosmic expansion discovered via distant supernovae in the late 1990s. The explanation walks through the cosmological constant Lambda (Einstein's "greatest blunder"), vacuum energy with negative pressure (p ~ -rho-c^2), and the Friedmann acceleration equation showing how p < -rho-c^2/3 forces the scale factor to accelerate. It closes on modified-gravity alternatives (MOND, TeVeS) and the fate of the universe (heat death vs Big Rip), with the raisin-bread analogy: dark matter is extra invisible raisins, dark energy is the dough itself expanding ever faster.

He follows with the **Eddington limit** — the maximum luminosity (L_Edd = 4-pi-GMm_p-c/sigma_T) before an object's radiation pressure blows away its own infalling material. He gets the gravity-vs-radiation force balance derivation (the r^2 cancels, leaving a mass-only luminosity ceiling), the Eddington accretion rate, and why it matters: it caps supermassive black hole growth, governs quasar brightness, drives stellar winds and eruptions in hypergiants like Eta Carinae, and produces Type I X-ray bursts on accreting neutron stars — plus the super-Eddington loopholes (anisotropic emission, photon trapping). Like the [[Conformal Cyclic Cosmology and Whether Time Is Real (chat)|cosmology and black-hole]] tangents elsewhere in his logs, this chat shows a finance-bound senior who reaches for general relativity and quantum cosmology as recreation — the same restless mind behind his [[Political and Economic Views|determinist]] and [[Faith vs. Knowledge|skeptical]] worldview.

# Tangent and Normal Line Problems, then Narrow Road Essay Grading

A two-part [[IB Math (SL)]] and [[IB English Curriculum 2022-2026|English]] homework session (titled "Solve curve equation"). Traveler photographs a worksheet of calculus problems on tangents and normals, works them with ChatGPT, and at the end pivots to having an essay graded against the IB HL rubric.

## Part 1 — Tangent/normal line problems (IB Math SL calculus)

Traveler sends a sequence of photographed worksheet questions, getting step-by-step worked solutions:

- **Normal-line system:** for `y = a√x + b/√x` with normal `4x + y = 22` at `x = 4`, solving the simultaneous equations gives **a = 2, b = 4**.
- **Normal to `cos x` through the origin:** the model first wrongly concludes no solution exists, Traveler pushes back ("sin0 can't equal 0?"), and after reasoning through the vertical-tangent edge case lands on **x = 0** as the normal line. A nice example of him not just accepting the AI's answer.
- **Tangent to a parabola** `f(x) = x² − 4x` at (1, −3): **y = −2x − 1**.
- **Product/chain rule:** `d/dx of 3xe^(x/2) = (3/2)(x+2)e^(x/2)`.
- **Shaded-triangle area** for the tangent to `y = 3xe^(x/2)` at `x = −1`: exact area **3/√e**. Traveler correctly computed the slope and b-value himself but tripped on the base, treating the x-intercept coordinate (x=1) as the base length instead of the distance from −1 to 1 (= 2).
- **Two tangents from external point** (1, −4) to `y = x² + 4x`: he found only the horizontal tangent `y = −4` and missed `y = 12x − 16`.
- **Angle between two curves** `y = 3e^(−x)` and `y = 2 + e^x`: intersection at (0,3), slopes −3 and 1, angle = arctan(2) ≈ **63.43°**.

The pedagogically revealing moment is Traveler's meta-question: *"these aren't hard, you are just plugging in things. i just get confused as to what to plug in."* This is a clear statement of his self-diagnosis — his trouble is decoding IB problem phrasing, not the algebra. The model responds with a translation framework: every tangent/normal/angle problem is the same three steps (find the point → find the slope via the derivative → write the correct line formula), broken into categories (tangent at a point, tangents through an external point, normals as perpendicular tangents, angle between curves). This connects to his recurring pattern across the math chats of wanting the *general schema* rather than one-off answers.

## Part 2 — Narrow Road essay grading (IB English HL)

Traveler pastes the full four-criterion IB HL essay rubric (A: Understanding & Interpretation, B: Analysis & Evaluation, C: Focus & Organization, D: Language) and asks for a graded evaluation of his essay on [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]]. The essay argues that Flanagan uses identity to show **"the perception of free will is more beneficial than acceptance of fate"** — squarely in his standing thematic territory of [[Fate and Free Will]] and [[Identity and Its Collapse]]. It analyses Dorrigo (the "Big Fella" identity as chosen self-reinvention), Darky Gardiner (contrasted with Tiny Middleton), and the Japanese officer Nakamura (identity surrendered to collective "Japanese spirit" ideology).

The model, operating in his custom **"Option 5" essay-grading mode** (rubric-faithful, no rewriting, no new ideas), scores it **14/20** — A: 4, B: 3, C: 4, D: 3. The diagnosis: strong, upper-half HL work held back by descriptive drift (recounting plot instead of analysing *how* Flanagan constructs meaning), looser Criterion B analysis, and stylistic unevenness. Traveler then asks for paragraph-by-paragraph fixes and a thesis rework, getting guidance-only notes — e.g. that his conclusion overreaches by claiming free will "only ever improves" identities when Darky dies brutally and Nakamura collapses.

# Buffett-Davis-Browne Tweet Reformatting and Long-Term Stock Picks

A July 2025 ChatGPT session split into two halves. In the first, Traveler feeds in a finance "thread" tweet (from `@porterstansb` / Porter & Co. Research) and iterates — repeatedly — to extract its *skeleton*: the rhetorical structure of a viral investing thread with all the actual content stripped out. He wants a reusable paragraph-form template he can refill. This is a study in reverse-engineering persuasive finance copywriting, fitting his interest in both markets and self-promotion / content building (cf. [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] marketing instincts).

The source tweet is the genre staple: it cites three investing sages — **Warren Buffett** ("never lose money"), **Shelby Davis** ("compound for 40 years"), and **Harry Browne** ("zig when others zag") — then claims an $18M personal portfolio built across three buckets: Rule-of-40 compounders (Kinsale), insurance stocks with **float**, and gold/bonds as non-correlated ballast, boasting beta 0.83, Sharpe 2.20, and 55% YTD. Traveler grinds the model through several formats (bulleted, single-tweet, paragraph, no-breaks) until he has a clean structural template: hook with three authorities → unifying claim → three-bucket framework → benefit checklist → hard metrics → reframed goal (compounding, not beating the market) → safe-leverage justification → exclusive-content CTA.

The second half pivots to genuine research: "what are good stocks to invest in long term." ChatGPT returns a categorized list — blue-chip compounders (AAPL, MSFT, V/MA, JNJ), growth runways (NVDA, AMZN, GOOGL, META), **financial compounders** with float (Berkshire, Arch Capital, MSCI), infrastructure/energy (Brookfield, NextEra, Constellation), and defensives (PG, KO, COST) — plus a quality filter: ROIC >10%, 7–15% revenue growth, low debt, durable moat, aligned management.

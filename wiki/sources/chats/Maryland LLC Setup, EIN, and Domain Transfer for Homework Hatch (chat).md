# Maryland LLC Setup, EIN, and Domain Transfer for Homework Hatch

A practical operations chat (2025-07-31, GPT-4o) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] works through the legal and infrastructural groundwork for incorporating his edtech startup [[Homework Hatch (startup)]]. Where most of his startup chats are about product, code, and AI architecture, this one is about turning the project into a real registered business — a notable signal of seriousness and a milestone in the startup's trajectory.

## What he was doing
The conversation runs through four concrete operational tasks:

- **Forming a Maryland LLC** — He asks how to set up an LLC and get an EIN. The walkthrough covers naming (must include "LLC"), designating a Maryland-based registered agent, filing Articles of Organization with the state's SDAT via the Maryland Business Express portal (~$100 fee), an optional operating agreement, and the recurring $300 Annual Report / Personal Property Return due each April 15. Maryland matters because Traveler lives there (consistent with [[Family and Personal Life]]).
- **Getting an EIN** — Apply only *after* the LLC is approved, free via the IRS online portal (Form CP 575 confirmation), used afterward for bank accounts, state tax registration, and hiring.
- **Choosing a domain** — He asks whether the TLD/ending matters; the answer favors `.com` for trust and SEO, with `.io`/`.co` acceptable for tech startups, plus brand-protection advice on registering multiple endings.
- **Escaping Network Solutions** — A large portion of the chat is him asking whether Network Solutions is "a scam," then twice asking for step-by-step instructions to unlock a domain there so he can transfer it elsewhere. He is clearly already holding a domain at NS and wants out — instructions cover turning off Transfer Lock, disabling private registration, requesting the EPP/authorization code, and the 60-day ICANN locks that can block transfers.

He also briefly detours to ask "where can I access the agent version of you" — an aside that ties into his ongoing interest in agentic AI tooling (see [[Agentic AI, MCP, and LMS Credential Ingestion for Homework Hatch (chat)]]) and his habit of using Cursor/Replit-style agent workflows in his [[Coding Club]] and startup coding.

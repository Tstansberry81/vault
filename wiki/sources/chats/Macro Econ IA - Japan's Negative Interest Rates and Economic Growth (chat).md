# Macro Econ IA - Japan's Negative Interest Rates and Economic Growth

A long ChatGPT (GPT-5) tutoring session in which Traveler scopes, researches, and builds the analytical spine of his [[IB Economics (SL)]] **macro Internal Assessment**. He arrives wanting to write on "Japan's economy" and, over the course of the chat, narrows to a specific research question and a defensible IB argument. It is a window into how he works a coursework problem: pick a topic, demand background, interrogate the mechanism until it actually makes sense to him, then convert understanding into rubric-friendly sentences and diagrams.

## The topic-narrowing arc
ChatGPT offers five candidate angles (monetary policy/interest rates, labour market + demographics, public debt, exchange rate/trade, structural stagnation). Traveler picks **interest rates** — consistent with his finance leaning ([[UVA and the Quant Question]], [[Investment Club]]). The RQ converges on:

> *How has the Japanese government used interest rates to stimulate economic growth?*

He anchors it on the **Economic Growth** chapter of the IB textbook and the **monetary policy transmission** mechanism.

## What he actually learned (the conceptual core)
The most revealing part is Traveler repeatedly refusing to accept a hand-wavy answer about **negative interest rates**. He pushes back four or five times — "why are banks lending money to the central bank? isn't it the opposite relationship," "is this different from US interest rates," "isn't disinflation the better term" — until he extracts the real model:
- The BOJ's −0.1% (Jan 2016) is a **policy rate on excess reserves** commercial banks park at the central bank, *not* a consumer/retail rate. It is a "storage fee on hoarded cash," not banks lending upward.
- It is the **opposite purpose** of a US Fed rate: the Fed uses rates as a *brake* on overheating/inflation; the BOJ used them as a *defibrillator* to escape deflation and hit 2% inflation. "Same tool, opposite direction."
- He correctly distinguishes **deflation** (Japan's actual decades-long problem) from **disinflation** (cooling high inflation, the US case), and is corrected when he reaches for the wrong term.
- He grasps that the **main goal was inflation/inflation expectations, not GDP directly** — GDP growth was the hoped-for downstream effect via rising aggregate demand.

This is the same intellectually stubborn, mechanism-first pattern visible elsewhere in his work — he won't write a sentence he can't derive.

## The IB argument and diagram
The session lands on a clean, evaluation-ready thesis: negative rates **succeeded** at raising inflation (eventually above 2%) and weakening the yen, but produced only **weak, unsustained real GDP growth** (~0–1%, ~0.1% in 2024) because **structural constraints** — aging population (~30% over 65), shrinking labour force, high precautionary savings, corporate cash-hoarding, low productivity — meant **LRAS did not expand**. He has ChatGPT build the central diagram: **AD shifts right (AD₁→AD₂) while LRAS/SRAS stay fixed**, so the new equilibrium shows higher price level and only a small real-GDP gain. This visually proves "demand rose, supply didn't, growth was meh" — the core IB evaluation point. This connects to his economics workload around [[Extended Essay (Economics)]] and the AD-AS modelling he does throughout [[IB Economics (SL)]].

## Data and sourcing
He gathers concrete series for the IA: BOJ policy-rate timeline (ZIRP from **Feb 1999** → QE 2001 → negative −0.1% Jan 2016 → exit Mar 2024 → ~0.5% Jan 2025), a 20-year **Japan CPI table** (deflation/near-zero through 2021, then 2.5–3.2% in 2022–24 driven by yen depreciation and import costs rather than domestic demand), and a 10-year **JPY/USD** table (¥108 in 2016 → ¥150+ 34-year low in 2024). He sources the IMF 2025 Article IV Mission statement and a WEF article, then has ChatGPT produce **MLA Works Cited entries** with hanging indents, alphabetized Feingold-then-IMF — the same citation-hygiene step seen in his EE work.

# Porter Stansberry Newsletter on Gender Specialization, Pilots, and IQ

Traveler pastes in a full **Porter & Co. Daily Journal** issue — forwarded by his father Porter Stansberry ("Fellas - Give this one a read") — and asks for a summary. The essay, written by Shannon Stansberry (Porter's wife), argues that modern society made a "dangerous mistake" by pushing men and women into identical economic roles instead of household **specialization**. It dresses Adam Smith's pin factory, Ricardo's comparative advantage, and Gary Becker's economics-of-the-household in a hard biological-determinist frame: women are "biologically designed to be home specialists," dual-income families are net poorer after childcare/taxes/commuting, and the real beneficiary of women's labor-force entry is the tax-hungry federal government. It links the shift to collapsing savings rates, debt, fertility decline, divorce, and infidelity.

Traveler's own contribution is the **summary and the critique** — which is the revealing part. He distills the economics fairly (specialization and opportunity cost are real), then explicitly separates the valid Econ-101 core from the ideological leap: "'Women belong in the home' → not an economic law, that's an ideological leap." He flags the correlation-vs-causation problems, the ignored confounders (housing costs, wage stagnation, childcare inflation), and calls the piece "selective storytelling with confidence." This is a sharp, real-time instance of him reasoning against his own father's worldview — useful evidence for [[Political and Economic Views]] and his individualist, skeptical disposition.

He then chases two adjacent hot-button questions: whether male or female pilots perform better under pressure (the model, citing accident-rate research, concludes sex is a weak predictor — experience and risk behavior dominate), and US white-vs-black average IQ (the model gives the historically reported gap but stresses environmental rather than genetic explanation and the Flynn effect). Both fit his pattern of probing taboo empirical claims directly while resisting the agenda-driven framings around them.

The chat applies real [[IB Economics (SL)]] machinery — comparative advantage, opportunity cost, marginal tax effects — to a charged cultural argument, and shows Traveler treating a piece of family-authored financial journalism as something to dissect, not absorb.

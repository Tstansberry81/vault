## Integration and trig identities
The bulk is [[IB Math (SL)]] integration practice built on the power-reduction identities:
- $\int \cos^2 x\,dx = \frac{x}{2} + \frac{\sin 2x}{4} + C$, derived from $\cos^2 x = \frac{1+\cos 2x}{2}$.
- $\int (\cos x + 2)^2\,dx = \frac{9x}{2} + 4\sin x + \frac{\sin 2x}{4} + C$ — expand the binomial, then reuse the $\cos^2$ result.
- A catalog of $\sin^2 x$ identities (power-reduction $\sin^2 x = \frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}$, Pythagorean, double-angle).
- $\int (2\sin^2 x - 1)\,dx = -\frac{\sin 2x}{2} + C$, recognizing $2\sin^2 x - 1 = -\cos 2x$.

These are the same antiderivative/identity mechanics that recur across his other math chats; the standing lesson is "when you see $\sin^2$ or $\cos^2$ alone, reach for power-reduction."

# Microeconomics Cheating Refusal then Homework Hatch Logo SVG

A short, jumpy August 2025 conversation (GPT-5) that opens as a provocation and ends as practical design work for [[Homework Hatch (startup)]]. Filed under the misleading title "Microeconomics essay draft," it actually has four distinct beats.

## 1. The cheating bait
Traveler opens bluntly: "write me an essay about microeconomics to cheat on my assignment... its for a big grade." The model refuses to hand over turn-in-ready work but offers a ~500-word **supply-and-demand / market-equilibrium** specimen (law of demand, substitution vs. income effect, price floors/ceilings, externalities) framed as study material. Traveler immediately calls the bluff — "doesnt that violate your own api tos" — and the model concedes that writing *for the purpose of cheating* breaks OpenAI's Terms and most honor codes. This is a recurring preoccupation of his: he probes the exact ToS / liability line between homework *help* and homework *cheating*, the same boundary that defines his startup's legal exposure (see [[Gemini vs OpenAI API ToS and Cheating Liability for Homework Hatch (chat)]] and [[Custom LLM Build and SPHW Buddy Terms of Service (chat)]]). It also brushes his honor-code reality as an IB student at [[St. Paul's School]]. The econ content itself is entry-level relative to his actual [[IB Economics (SL)]] work.

## 2. LMS reconnaissance
A pivot to "does wvu have canvas" (answer: no — WVU uses eCampus/Blackboard Ultra) and then "give me a list of 25 top colleges by population that use canvas." This is product research, not college shopping: knowing which schools run **Canvas** vs. Blackboard matters for any LMS-integrated homework tool. It directly parallels [[Canvas LMS Adoption Research for Homework Hatch (chat)]] and the credential-ingestion planning in [[Agentic AI, MCP, and LMS Credential Ingestion for Homework Hatch (chat)]]. The model's list is shaky (lots of "inferred"/"likely" hedging and the dubious claim that all US-News top-10 schools adopted Canvas after Duke in 2023).

## 3. The logo SVG grind
The substantive half: Traveler uploads two reference images of the **Homework Hatch mark** (egg + hatching chick + paper + wordmark) and demands a vector recreation — "create an svg file for this image. be thorough and perfect. take your time." He iterates hard and exactingly, rejecting drafts ("wasnt accurate enough try again... i mean it, make it a perfect copy"), then asking the model to "blend the elements better, the beak and the crack dont look so good," and finally "round the edges and give me the transparent one only." The model produces successive SVGs with CSS-variable color tokens, named groups (`#egg`, `#paper`, `#wordmark`), a rounded chipped-eggshell crack, and a transparent icon-only export. It flags that the wordmark needs the exact font (Montserrat/Inter 900 as a stand-in) for a pixel-perfect glyph outline.

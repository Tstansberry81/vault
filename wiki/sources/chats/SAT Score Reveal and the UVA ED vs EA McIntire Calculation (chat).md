# SAT Score Reveal and the UVA ED vs EA McIntire Calculation

A late-October 2025 ChatGPT (GPT-5) session in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] shares his new **SAT of 1540 (750 R/W, 790 Math)** plus a **33 ACT** and uses it to relitigate his entire [[College Search]] strategy: should he still apply **Early Decision to [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]]**, or switch to EA now that his numbers clear the school's top quartile? It is the most detailed window in the vault into his actual application stats, school context, and admissions self-assessment.

## The stats he confirms
- **SAT 1540 / ACT 33**, **4.0 unweighted / 4.58 weighted GPA**, full **[[IB History (HL)|IB Diploma]]** candidate, **first-round Cum Laude** (top 10% — at [[St. Paul's School]] Cum Laude is the top 20% across two rounds, with first-rounders locked in junior year barring an honor offense).
- Course load = **maximum rigor available**: HL Physics, HL English, HL History, SL Math AA, SL Economics, SL Japanese, TOK. He was *blocked from HL Math* (not placed in double-math in 9th because he wasn't honors in 8th), a sore point — he counters with 99/100/99 finals in Geometry Honors, Algebra 2 Honors, and [[IB Math (SL)]], plus the 790 SAT Math, as proof he "knows his shit." No free blocks until senior year; dropped Choir over a teacher change.
- He applies to **McIntire on the finance track** (intended major), framing [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] (AI edtech, Canvas API), 160+ community service hours, [[Investment Club]] presidency, the [[Extended Essay (Economics)|economics EE on inflation]], Bridges Baltimore counseling, Civic Engagement co-presidency, NSLC Yale, squash, and a Choir tour as his EC spine.

## The core dilemma and ChatGPT's read
ChatGPT runs repeated (loosely sourced) odds simulations, landing on **ED ~60-70%, EA ~35-45%, RD ~25-30%** for UVA, and **60-75%** for Wake Forest (his stated #2, whose campus he liked better having visited in fall vs. UVA at graduation). Verdict throughout: ED still "moves the needle" most and is the rational play if UVA is truly #1; EA buys flexibility and merit comparison if he's only ~60% sure. Clemson reads as a near-lock (~90%), Honors College a coin flip.

Notably, **Traveler twice demands brutal honesty** ("dont tell me what i want to hear unless it's true"), and pushes back hard when ChatGPT invents school averages — producing a March SAT chart showing St. Paul's averages **1161** vs. MD 1008 / national 1030, confirming he sits ~3 SD above his own school. This skeptical, data-checking posture is characteristic (see [[Intellectual Profile]], [[Core Convictions]]).

## The real complication
The decisive detail he saves for last: **~8 of his ~80-person grade are also ED-ing to UVA**, but only **one other is Cum Laude**, and last year's three St. Paul's UVA admits were *all* Cum Laude (two engineering, one econ). The synthesis: internal cohort competition is the only real limiter, but his Cum Laude status + distinctive [[Homework Hatch (startup)]]/Japan narrative make him the likely "default" McIntire candidate from his school — "sitting exactly where the previous admits sat."

He also pastes his **Japan study-abroad Common App essay** (getting lost in Akihabara, leading the group back via his Japanese) for evaluation — the same essay developed elsewhere (see [[IB Japanese (SL)]] and the surfing/Japan Common App chats). ChatGPT rates it top-20-25% for its fear→growth→belonging arc, flagging only the worn word "lifechanging."

# Mock Theta Functions and Ramanujan's Greatest Discoveries

A pure-curiosity math-history conversation ([[ChatGPT]], gpt-5) where [[Traveler Stansberry]] explores frontier number theory with no coursework hook — squarely [[Intellectual Interests]]. He opens on **mock theta functions**, the strange "almost-modular" series Ramanujan listed in his final 1920 letter to G. H. Hardy, learning how they imitate theta functions near roots of unity without satisfying full modular symmetry, how Sander Zwegers finally explained them in the early 2000s as the holomorphic parts of **harmonic Maass forms** (with a non-holomorphic "shadow" completing the symmetry), and how they now surface in string theory, black-hole entropy, moonshine, and quantum topology.

He follows with "Ramanujan's best discoveries mathematically," getting a ranked tour: **partition theory** and the congruences $p(5k+4)\equiv 0 \pmod 5$ etc.; the **Hardy–Ramanujan asymptotic formula** for $p(n)$ and the circle method; absurdly fast **infinite series for $\pi$**; modular forms and q-series; the **tau function** and modular discriminant; bizarre continued-fraction identities; highly composite numbers; **Ramanujan summation** ($1+2+3+\cdots = -\frac{1}{12}$ under zeta regularization, with its appearances in QFT and the Casimir effect); and the **1729 taxicab number** story.

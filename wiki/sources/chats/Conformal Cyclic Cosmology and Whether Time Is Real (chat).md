# Conformal Cyclic Cosmology and Whether Time Is Real (chat)

A late-2025 after-hours curiosity session in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] interrogates ChatGPT about deep cosmology and the metaphysics of time — entirely outside any [[IB Physics (HL)]] syllabus requirement. The conversation is driven by one-word and fragmentary prompts ("conformal cyclic cosmology", "equation for it", "equations for spacetime"), the signature of someone chasing intellectual rabbit holes for their own sake rather than completing an assignment.

## What he asked
- **Conformal Cyclic Cosmology (CCC):** Penrose's idea that the universe runs through endless *aeons*, each a Big Bang that ends in infinite cold expansion; once all mass fades (proton decay, black-hole evaporation via Hawking radiation), there is no scale, so the dead universe's stretched end is conformally "ironed flat" and glued to the next Big Bang. The model claims circular scars in the CMB are evidence of prior aeons — a claim most cosmologists reject.
- **The math behind it:** conformal rescaling $g_{ab} = \Omega^2 \hat g_{ab}$, the crossover-surface matching conditions ($\Omega \to 0$ at both future-infinity and the next Big Bang), the Weyl curvature hypothesis ($C_{abcd}C^{abcd} \to 0$ near the crossover), and the "mass fade" scalar-field idea.
- **Spacetime equations generally:** the interval $ds^2 = g_{\mu\nu}dx^\mu dx^\nu$, the Minkowski metric, the Einstein field equations $R_{\mu\nu} - \tfrac12 R g_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G\,T_{\mu\nu}$, geodesic/Christoffel/Riemann/Ricci machinery, the FLRW metric and Friedmann equations, and special-relativistic time dilation $\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}$.

## The philosophical core
The most revealing exchange is the pivot from physics to metaphysics: *"is time a human construct or is it an actual thing?"* He then proposes his own theory — *"time is essentially just a measure of decay besides spacetime"* — and gets pushed back: decay *happens in* time but does not *define* it (the radioactive-decay law $N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$ presupposes time), entropy gives time an *arrow* ($S_\text{future} > S_\text{past}$) but not its existence, and the block-universe view treats time as a geometric axis the universe doesn't "feel." He is doing first-principles reasoning, floating a hypothesis, and testing it against the established framework — the same move he makes elsewhere on free will and religion.

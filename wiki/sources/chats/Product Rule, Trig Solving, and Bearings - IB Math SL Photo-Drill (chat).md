# Product Rule, Trig Solving, and Bearings - IB Math SL Photo-Drill

A same-day companion to [[2023 IB Math AA SL Paper 1 Full Walkthrough and a MacBook Buying Detour (chat)|2023 IB Math AA SL Paper 1 Full Walkthrough and a MacBook Buying Detour]] (both 2026-05-14), this chat is [[Traveler Stansberry]] working through a stack of photographed [[IB Math (SL)]] problems, mostly via uploaded images. It opens with the product-rule derivative of h(x) = 2xe^x + 3, where his follow-up — "so the 2x isnt treated as a function?" — gets the model to re-frame the rule with u = 2x, v = e^x, the same instinct to nail down the underlying mechanic that recurs in his [[Intellectual Interests]] and in [[Math Solutions with Explanations|the Paper 1 walkthrough]].

He then asks to "solve sin2x = 1−cos2x" (factored to sin x = 0 or tan x = 1, with the explicit warning not to divide away the sin x = 0 roots), and requests a full primer on **bearings and angles** — clockwise from North, three-digit notation, reverse bearings (±180°), and the rule that to find an angle at a point both directions must originate from that point. The bearings section runs into worked sine-rule and cosine-rule navigation problems (a ship A→B→C, and a cleaner Jaipur–Lucknow–Delhi triangle where the 034° bearing against a due-east baseline yields a 56° interior angle and LD ≈ 420 km).

The drill widens into the rest of the SL toolkit: a TI-84 linear-regression problem (ice creams vs children, with the trap of which variable is the predictor and explicit calculator keystrokes), a binomial-coefficient system solving a²b⁶ = 16 and a²b⁸ = 64 for a = ½, b = 2, and a two-way-table probability problem covering conditional probability, independence testing, and a B(10, 0.55) "at least 5" binomcdf. The heavy lean on TI-84 steps and "where IB hides the trap" framing shows him prepping for exam mechanics, not just concepts — the same pragmatic test-craft visible across [[Asymptotes, Tangent Lines, Extrema, and Bearings - IB Math Review (chat)|Asymptotes, Tangent Lines, Extrema, and Bearings - IB Math Review]] and [[Rational Function Limits and a Trig-Derivatives Midterm Cram (chat)|Rational Function Limits and a Trig-Derivatives Midterm Cram]].

# PTAs and FTAs Explained for IB Econ

A short, focused homework chat in which Traveler fills out a PowerPoint assignment for his [[IB Economics (SL)]] class on two forms of economic integration — **Preferential Trading Areas (PTAs)** and **Free Trade Areas (FTAs)**. He pastes a standard five-question template (what is the model, how it's supposed to work, two real-life examples — one obvious and one obscure, does it work in practice, efficiency rating) and asks for citation-backed, "IB-safe" slide content. This is the international-trade unit of the SL course, sitting alongside the tariff/subsidy and free-trade material in his other Econ chats (e.g. the tariff welfare graphs and free-trade debate prep).

## Content produced
- **PTA** = loosest integration: members *reduce* (not necessarily eliminate) trade barriers on *selected* goods, keep independent external trade policies. Examples given: the EU's **Generalised Scheme of Preferences** (well-known) and the **India–MERCOSUR PTA** (lesser-known). Rated ~5–6/10 efficiency — easy and politically flexible but trade diversion and rules-of-origin friction limit gains.
- **FTA** = tariffs/quotas eliminated on substantially all goods *between* members, but each keeps its own external tariffs (hence rules of origin to stop transshipment). Examples: **USMCA** (well-known, replaced NAFTA) and **EFTA** (Norway, Switzerland; lesser-known). Rated ~7–8/10 — strong trade creation, preserves sovereignty.
- A comparison table (tariff scope, trade creation, efficiency) and IB-acceptable citations (WTO RTA factsheet; Krugman/Obstfeld/Melitz; European Commission; USTR).

## What it reveals
The most telling moment is Traveler **catching ChatGPT in an error against his textbook**: the model first claimed PTAs "crucially don't eliminate tariffs," and Traveler pushed back ("in my ib textbook it says the exact opposite"), then corrected himself again ("they use reducing apologies"). The resolution lands on the IB-precise framing — PTA = *selective reduction*, FTA = *near-total elimination between members* — and ChatGPT concedes the textbook's "reducing" wording is the safer, examiner-proof verb. This shows his characteristic habit of treating the AI as a fallible collaborator to be checked against authoritative sources rather than an oracle, the same epistemic posture he brings to his EE data sourcing and [[Theory of Knowledge]] work. The exchange is small but a clean example of him optimizing answers for the specific tone and scope a rubric rewards.

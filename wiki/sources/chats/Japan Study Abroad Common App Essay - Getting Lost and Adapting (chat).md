# Japan Study Abroad Common App Essay - Getting Lost and Adapting

A long iterative drafting session (titled, misleadingly, "Maryland inflation June 2022" after the opening throwaway query) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] workshopped his **Common App personal statement** through roughly a dozen revisions with ChatGPT acting as a "high-level admissions officer." Two competing essays emerged; he ultimately favored the **Japan study abroad** essay over a **surfing-in-Sayulita** essay. This is the clearest window in the vault into how he framed himself for [[College Search|college applications]].

## The two essays

**Japan essay (the keeper).** Recounts a two-week school exchange to **Rikkyo Niiza High School** in Niiza City, Saitama, staying with a host family near Saitama Stadium (Urawa-Misono, end of the "stadium line" / Saitama Railway Line). Anchored vignettes: arriving drowsy at Haneda in the rain; first dinner of **gyudon** with raw egg; a host dog screeching at 4 a.m.; sitting in class understanding nothing despite "11 years of Japanese" (ties to [[IB Japanese (SL)]]); bonding over soccer with a 5'4" Japanese "Messi"; and leading classmates to **Akihabara**, getting lost ~2 miles from the station, and using his Japanese to find the way back. The thesis lands on *"getting lost... isn't the end. It's where adaptation begins."* Notable: he insisted on keeping *all* the small stories rather than building around one cinematic moment, and ChatGPT reframed that patchwork as deliberate ("No single story defined my time there").

**Surfing essay (the alternative).** A rawer, more vulnerable draft about learning to surf in Sayulita, Mexico, intercut with his father announcing on a back porch — in a five-minute conversation — that his ex-girlfriend was pregnant with his child and that he was engaged to his current girlfriend. The surfing-as-adaptation metaphor ("I can't change him") channels the same coping philosophy documented in [[Family and Personal Life]] and [[Surfing Common App Essay - Dad's Chaos and Adaptation (chat)]]. ChatGPT flagged the risk that "Dad's drama overshadows you," and Traveler agreed the Japan essay was the "safer, broadly appealing" pick.

> [!note] Same theme, two vehicles
> Both essays converge on **adaptation over resistance** — accepting forces you can't control and "keeping yourself afloat." This is a recurring, almost determinist self-narrative (see [[Political and Economic Views]], [[Core Convictions]]): the surfer can't fight thousands of tons of water; the foreigner can't read the kanji; the son can't change the father. He chose to dramatize it through cultural disorientation rather than family pain.

## What it reveals

- His coping framework — adapt, don't fight — is consistent across surfing, his father, and academics, and he is self-aware enough to package it as a college-essay through-line.
- Genuine affection for Japan ("I want to live here"), reinforcing the [[IB Japanese (SL)]] / cultural thread; he spent much of the chat reconstructing his exact Saitama commute (Urawa-Misono → Higashi-Kawaguchi → Niiza on the Musashino Line) from memory.
- He treats ChatGPT as an iterative editor, pasting near-identical drafts repeatedly for incremental polish rather than accepting a full rewrite — preserving his own voice over the model's.

The session is laced with unrelated one-off queries (Maryland vs. US inflation in June 2022 — likely cross-checking his [[Extended Essay (Economics)]]; nautical miles; Bimini fishing spots) that share no thematic link and reflect a scattershot use of the same thread.

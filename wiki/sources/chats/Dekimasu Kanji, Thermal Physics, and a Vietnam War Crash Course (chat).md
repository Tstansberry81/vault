# Dekimasu Kanji, Thermal Physics, and a Vietnam War Crash Course

A genuinely chaotic grab-bag session that hops across three of [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s subjects in one sitting, opening with kanji lookups for [[IB Japanese (SL)]] — 出来ます (dekimasu), 図書館 (toshokan/library), and 所 (tokoro/place) — with ChatGPT noting which words natives usually leave in hiragana. The pattern of "collecting kanji like Pokémon" matches Traveler's recurring vocabulary-drilling approach to Japanese.

The bulk is a thermal-physics review for [[IB Physics (HL)]]: the relationship between thermal capacity and specific heat (C = mc), Q = mcΔT, Celsius-to-Kelvin conversion, atomic mass and mole arithmetic, and the kinetic model of an ideal gas. He works several exam-style problems — finding specific heat from an electric heater (1500 J/kg·K), water flow rate in a shower unit (~0.066 kg/s), and reasoning about why such answers are only estimates. A sharp moment: he asks why F = -dU/dr ties intermolecular force to potential energy, and gets the clean answer that an ideal gas has no force because potential energy is constant. He also catches himself on Q vs ΔU, prompting a First Law clarification (ΔU = Q − W, with W = 0 in simple heating). This thermal material connects to his broader HL physics coursework.

A long [[IB History (HL)]] tangent covers Vietnam: the My Lai Massacre and William Calley, the effect of nightly TV news on public opinion, Kent State, President Thieu's resistance to the Paris Peace Accords, Dien Bien Phu, and effective counterinsurgency measures. This feeds directly into his Vietnam-era History HL syllabus. Brief detours into the Magna Carta and a joke "Black Luigi" misunderstanding round out the wandering.

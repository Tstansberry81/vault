# Series vs Parallel Circuits and Electric Field MCQs

A photo-driven [[IB Physics (HL)]] homework session (March 2026, GPT-5) in which Traveler worked through a worksheet of resistance, current, power, EMF/internal-resistance, and electric-field problems by snapping pictures of each question. It is a representative example of how he uses ChatGPT as an on-demand physics tutor: feed the image, get a step-by-step walkthrough and the matching multiple-choice letter, occasionally pushing back when something seems off.

## What he worked through
- **Series vs parallel equivalent resistance**: collapsing a 1 Ω + 3 Ω series branch in parallel with a 2 Ω branch to get 4/3 Ω; recognizing parallel resistance is always smaller than the smallest resistor as a sanity check.
- **Current distribution** in parallel branches (smaller R draws more current; series resistors share the same current; charge conservation means current in = current out).
- **Power scaling**: using \(P=IV\), \(P=I^2R\), \(P=V^2/R\) to find which change doubles power — doubling current and halving resistance gives \(2I^2R\).
- **Real-world energy cost**: a 11 A / 120 V hair dryer run 15 min at $0.09/kWh costs ~$0.03.
- **EMF and internal resistance**: terminal voltage \(V = \mathcal{E} - Ir\); deriving total current from EMF and total resistance rather than being handed it.
- A multi-part bulb problem (60 W, 120 V bulbs run dim because none receive full voltage) and a messy resistor-triangle network (collapse the obvious series pair first).
- **Electric fields and potential**: \(E = k|q|/r^2\) as a vector, field direction toward negative / away from positive charges, scalar potential \(V = k\sum q_i/r_i\), force on a third charge \(\vec F = q\vec E\), and work \(W = qV\) to bring a charge from infinity.

# McIntire Admit Stats and the Commerce-Math-CS Stacking Plan

A forward-planning session in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] — already on the [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]] track — maps out exactly how he wants to assemble his undergraduate degree at the McIntire School of Commerce. He opens by asking for McIntire's acceptance rates and average stats: ~50-51% for internal UVA applicants (a "filtered 50%," since everyone applying is already a strong UVA student), ~18% for transfers, with mean admit GPAs around 3.74-3.78 internal and ~3.9 transfer, and SAT essentially irrelevant once enrolled. The model stresses that grades are necessary but not sufficient — rigor, business clubs, leadership, and a "clean professional narrative" matter — which dovetails with how he's positioning his [[Investment Club]], [[Coding Club]], and [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] in his [[Extracurriculars and Achievements]].

The bulk of the chat is him pressure-testing a **Commerce + Math + CS** stack for a quant/data-analyst career. He moves through: is a BS Commerce + Math double major realistic (yes, but "two lifestyles at once"); major vs minor in math (the model pushes minor — "80% of the value for 50% of the suffering," reserving Real Analysis and Abstract Algebra as optional pain); how to fold in CS (targeted Python/SQL/data-structures/ML courses plus a GitHub project portfolio beat a formal minor for hiring); UVA credit requirements for both minors (~15-18 credits each, ~30-36 combined, no double-counting against the 120-credit total); and even sample weekly McIntire-year schedules for the "optimized" (Option A) vs "maximized" (Option B) paths. He floats a finance + IT concentration with quant/analytics and AI tracks, and asks what rigor those tracks actually add — the answer being little on their own, since the real difficulty comes from the underlying math and CS, not McIntire labels.

What it reveals is a strategist optimizing for employability over prestige-stacking: the recurring thesis is "make yourself dangerous, not just exhausted," and "tracks make you look specialized, skills make you valuable." It is the concrete academic blueprint behind his [[UVA Quant Finance Pathway and the Math-vs-Finance Decision (chat)|UVA Quant Finance Pathway and the Math-vs-Finance Decision]] and reflects the applied, project-driven [[Intellectual Interests]] and individualist drive that run through his [[Political and Economic Views]].

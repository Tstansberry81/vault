## What was covered
- **Conservation of momentum** — equal-mass head-on collision (+8 / -4 m/s); checking each option against initial momentum $p_i = 4m$ to find the only conserved (inelastic) outcome.
- **Graph literacy** — the recurring exam trap. A force-distance graph means **area = work** ($W = \frac12 F_1 s_1$, then work-energy theorem giving $v = \sqrt{F_1 s_1/m}$); a momentum-time graph means **slope = force** ($F = dp/dt$). The model drills the memory hook: *F-s graph → area, p-t and v-t graphs → slope.*
- **Circular motion** — conical pendulum ($a = g\tan\theta$ measured from vertical) and a horizontal-string vertical circle where weight contributes nothing centripetally, so $T = mv^2/r \approx 8$ N. Side/top/bottom tension formulas summarized.
- **Gravitation derivations** — orbital kinetic energy $KE = GMm/2r$, the bound-system relations $KE = -\tfrac12 U$ and $E = -GMm/2r$; an escape problem solved by total-energy sign; Kepler's third law derived from scratch ($k = G/4\pi^2$) and then applied in ratio form to compute Mars's mass ($\approx 6.4\times10^{23}$ kg, dead-on); and gravitational field as a potential gradient ($g \approx \Delta V/\Delta r \approx 0.5$ N/kg).
- **Rotational dynamics** — a hoop rolling without slipping down an incline, with a full force diagram (friction points **up** the plane to supply torque) and the derivation $a = g\sin\theta/2$.

# Electron in a Field then a Full Magnetism and Induction Drill

A March 2026 image-driven homework session in which Traveler works through roughly a dozen [[IB Physics (HL)]] multiple-choice and short-answer problems on electric fields, magnetic forces, and electromagnetic induction. He photographs each textbook question and asks ChatGPT to "solve and explain," using the model as a one-on-one tutor through the entire fields-and-induction unit. The tone is conversational and self-deprecating ("im so lost and its hard to use the right hand rule on a screen"), and the assistant answers in a brash, joke-laden style.

## What the problems covered
- **Electron between charged plates** — treated as projectile motion with the electric force replacing gravity: \(F=qE\), \(a=F/m \approx 2.28\times10^{14}\,\text{m s}^{-2}\), horizontal time from \(t=x/v_x\), then exit angle \(\theta=\tan^{-1}(v_y/v_x)\approx 41^\circ\).
- **Magnetic force direction** — \(\vec F = q\vec v\times\vec B\). Parallel \(v\) and \(B\) give zero force; perpendicular gives maximum. He explicitly struggles with the right-hand rule on a 2D screen, so the assistant gives him a coordinate shortcut (right = +x, up = +y, out of screen = +z) and a lookup table to replace "hand gymnastics."
- **Velocity selector** — setting \(qE = qvB\) so \(B = E/v\), with \(E=V/d\); answer 12 mT.
- **Tesla units** — asked *twice*, finally pinned to \(1\,T = N/(A\cdot m)\).
- **Force between parallel wires** — \(F/L = \mu_0 I_1 I_2/(2\pi r)\); same-direction attract, opposite repel.
- **Induction** — flux \(\Phi = NBA\cos\theta\) (angle measured from the **normal**, not the surface), motional emf \(\mathcal E = BLv\), Faraday's law, a 180° coil flip giving \(\Delta\Phi = 2BA\), and a generator with \(\mathcal E_{\text{mean}} = \frac{2}{\pi}NBA\omega\) solved for frequency (~360 Hz).

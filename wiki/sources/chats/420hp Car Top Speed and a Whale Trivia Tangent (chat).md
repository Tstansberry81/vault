# 420hp Car Top Speed and a Whale Trivia Tangent

A short, casual New Year's Eve chat that opens with one genuine physics question and then dissolves entirely into jokes and trivia. [[Traveler Stansberry]] asks whether a 1,000 lb car with 420 hp can hit 200 mph. The model works the top-speed problem from the drag-power balance \(P = \tfrac12 \rho C_d A v^3\), plugging in 420 hp ≈ 313 kW and a small \(C_dA \approx 0.5\,\text{m}^2\) to get \(v \approx 100\,\text{m/s}\) (~225 mph), with the takeaway that at high speed **drag dominates and weight barely matters** (only rolling resistance scales with mass). Real-world caveats: gearing, traction, aero (\(C_dA\) near 0.7–0.8 kills it), and stability. This drag-vs-power reasoning is adjacent to the kinematics and energy material in his [[IB Physics (HL)]] course, but the chat is recreational, not coursework.

The conversation then abandons physics for pure play: NFL players with "whale" spliced into their names ("Whalequan Barkley," "Whalevis Kelce"), a taxonomy of whale species (baleen vs. toothed, the orca-is-a-dolphin fact), a riff on whether a "green whale" exists (algae-covered grays, "Greenland whale" = bowhead), and finally the remora ("fish that stick to sharks"). No connection to his startup, finance, or academic work — this is downtime curiosity and humor.

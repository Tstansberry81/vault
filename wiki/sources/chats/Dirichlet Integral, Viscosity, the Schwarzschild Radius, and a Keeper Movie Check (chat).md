# Dirichlet Integral, Viscosity, the Schwarzschild Radius, and a Keeper Movie Check

A May 2026 grab-bag chat that captures Traveler's intellectual range a month before graduation — math well above his [[IB Math (SL)|SL]] syllabus, applied physics, a personal-life aside, and routine [[College Search|college logistics]]. It runs through the kind of model voice he prefers when not doing graded work: dry, tangent-prone, faintly contemptuous of human nature.

It opens with the **Dirichlet integral**, the improper integral of sin(x)/x from 0 to infinity equaling pi/2. He gets the full treatment: why it converges conditionally but not absolutely (the positive and negative humps cancel just enough), the standard Feynman-style derivation introducing a damping parameter e^(-ax) and differentiating under the integral, and the generalization to (pi/2)sgn(a) that pervades Fourier analysis and signal processing. This is squarely the [[UVA and the Quant Question|quant-curious]] self-study he's been doing — calculus tools far beyond what [[IB Math (SL)]] ever required, consistent with his broader [[Intellectual Interests]] and the math-for-quant pivot he keeps circling.

A blunt personal tangent breaks the math: "do marinuana cartridges have cotton" — answered with the wick-material rundown (ceramic vs cotton, burnt hits from dry wicks). That flows into legitimate fluid physics: **viscosity** (internal friction, the tau = mu(du/dy) shear relation, temperature dependence) and whether it relates to density (no — mercury is dense but flows easily; the link is only kinematic viscosity nu = mu/rho). Then back to astrophysics with the **Schwarzschild radius** (r_s = 2GM/c^2), computed for Earth at ~8.9 mm — compress the planet to a marble and it becomes a black hole — derived via setting escape velocity equal to c, with the caveat that the real result comes from the Einstein field equations.

The chat ends on practical and pop-culture notes: he re-researches [[UVA and the Quant Question|UVA]] IB-credit policy ("HL 5-7 gets credit, SL little/none, the Diploma as a package earns nothing") — the same conclusion reached in [[UVA Dorm Tiers, IB HL Credits, and a Steam-Calorimetry Thermo Marathon (chat)|the dorm/thermo chat]], now with citations to UVA's official pages — and asks for a workup of the 2025 horror film *Keeper* (Osgood Perkins, Tatiana Maslany; rated ~5.5/10).

# Elevator Physics then Finance School Admissions Comparison

A two-part ChatGPT conversation (8 May 2025, GPT-4o) that opens as a quick [[IB Physics (HL)]] mechanics question and pivots into an extended survey of undergraduate **finance program** admissions across several schools — a window into Traveler's college search the spring before applying.

## Part 1 — Elevator / apparent weight
Traveler asks why your weight increases going up in an elevator, and the model walks through **apparent weight = normal force**: accelerating up gives N = mg + ma (feel heavier), accelerating down gives mg − ma (lighter), constant velocity gives N = mg, and free fall gives N = 0 (weightlessness). He requests a force diagram. This is standard Newton's-second-law content from [[IB Physics (HL)]] — a one-off homework clarification rather than deep study.

## Part 2 — Undergrad finance admissions survey
The bulk of the chat is Traveler scoping out where to study finance, reinforcing the trajectory documented in [[UVA and the Quant Question]] and his eventual choice of [[St. Paul's School]] → UVA finance. He compares, in order:

- **UT Austin (McCombs BBA Finance)** — ~11% admit; Canfield Business Honors ~4–5% (very tough out-of-state).
- **UVA (McIntire School of Commerce)** — not a direct admit; you apply after first year. Internal applicants ~51% (mean GPA 3.78), external transfers ~18%. This is the program relevant to his actual path to [[UVA and the Quant Question]].
- **UNC Kenan-Flagler** — apply after first year, ~38% into the business school; finance grads ~$92k starting (~$103k for IB).
- A selectivity ranking of **Duke / UVA / UNC / Boston College / UT Austin**, plus **Wake Forest** (~22%).

He repeatedly asks "what stats should I have," and the model gives target profiles (GPA ~3.9+, SAT 1450–1540, rigorous AP/IB calc/econ/stats, leadership in finance clubs). This maps directly onto Traveler's real profile: his [[Academic Record]], [[IB Math (SL)]] / [[IB Economics (SL)]] coursework, and leadership of the [[Investment Club]] are exactly the "Investment Club / finance-focused leadership" the model recommends.

## What it reveals
Confirms Traveler was actively benchmarking finance-undergrad selectivity in spring 2025 and understood the key structural nuance — that **UVA McIntire and UNC Kenan-Flagler are post-enrollment admits** while UT McCombs is a direct freshman admit. The pivot from a physics homework question straight into college-finance research is characteristic of how he uses the tool as a single catch-all assistant.

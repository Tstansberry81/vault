# Japanese Travel Vocab, RenTech Medallion Compounding, and a Korean War Venn Diagram

A three-subject grab-bag chat (titled "Kayaku Meaning Explained" from a stray vocab lookup) that braids together [[IB Japanese (SL)]] vocabulary, a [[Investment Club|finance]] deep-dive on Renaissance Technologies, and a [[IB History (HL)]] Korean War comparison. It is a clean snapshot of how Traveler interleaves coursework with his own curiosities in a single session.

## Japanese vocabulary drilling
The session opens with rapid-fire word lookups, the same drilling style seen in [[Ichiban Kanji Lookups - Japanese Vocabulary Drilling (chat)]] and [[Macro AD-AS Review and Japanese Ab Initio Kanji Flashcards (chat)]]:
- **旅行する (ryokō suru)** = to travel, vs **行く** (to go) and the poetic **旅する** (to journey).
- A pronunciation tangent: **火薬 (kayaku)** = gunpowder ("fire medicine") vs **早く/速く (hayaku)** = early (time) vs fast (speed).
- て-form practice: **歌う → 歌って** (the う→って u-verb mutation).
- Chore/household vocab: **洗濯する** (do laundry), **台所 / キッチン** (kitchen, native word vs loanword).

This is functional, conversational Japanese — house and daily-life vocabulary — consistent with his SL coursework.

## Renaissance Technologies / Medallion Fund
The center of gravity. He asks for RenTech's ~20-year average return and then "break it down," revealing his appetite for the mechanics of [[UVA and the Quant Question|quant finance]]. Key takeaways he absorbed:
- **Medallion**: ~66% gross / ~39–40% net annualized, ~1988–2018, closed to outsiders. Fees ~5% management + ~44% performance, framed as a **capacity-control filter**, not greed.
- Mechanics: thousands of weak signals (51–52% win rates), ultra-short holding periods, low cross-correlation, ruthless strategy pruning, capping AUM to protect alpha — "law of large numbers abuse, done professionally."
- Public funds (RIEF etc.) only ~10–15% annualized because the best signals are reserved for Medallion ("same brain, different cage").
- The compounding punchline he requested: **$100,000 at 40% net for 35 years ≈ $13.7 billion** ("the curve doesn't grow, it detonates").

The stated lesson — "sustained alpha + low volatility + discipline beats everything; finance people obsess over compounding, not stock picks" — directly feeds his investing worldview and his recurring fascination with building an [[Building a Custom AI Stock-Ranking Model (chat)|AI stock model]]. The reality check (Medallion is the single most extreme outlier, unreplicable) is a useful counterweight to that ambition.

## Korean War Venn diagram (Chinese vs American soldiers)
He pivots to a History assignment: a Venn diagram comparing a Chinese soldier (*A Modern Chinese Soldier Remembers Korea*) with American soldiers from his battle-project research. The model fills all three regions — Chinese (poor equipment, collective/ideological sacrifice, night marches, frostbite), American (better supplied, air/artillery support, drafted and confused about war aims), and shared (extreme cold, mountainous terrain, psychological trauma, war defining identity). This ties to his Chosin Reservoir work in [[Battle of Chosin Reservoir Presentation Research (chat)]] and the broader [[IB History (HL)]] Cold War unit.

The diagram's framing — material conditions vs ideology vs shared human experience — shows him being coached toward analytical comparison rather than fact-summary.

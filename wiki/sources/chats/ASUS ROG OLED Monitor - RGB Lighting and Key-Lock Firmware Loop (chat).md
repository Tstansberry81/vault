# ASUS ROG OLED Monitor - RGB Lighting and Key-Lock Firmware Loop

A short personal tech-support exchange in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] troubleshoots his **ASUS ROG Swift OLED PG27AQDP** gaming monitor. He opens with a photo of the monitor asking how to change its LED color, then descends into a frustrating firmware loop. Notable as a window into his **gaming/console hardware setup** rather than his coursework or [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] work.

## What he was doing
- Wanted to set the monitor's RGB lighting (Aura) to **breathing blue** via the on-screen display (OSD), but it wouldn't apply.
- The real snag emerged: his monitor keys were **locked**, and every attempt to unlock them (hold joystick ~5 sec) instead triggered a **firmware update** that then **failed repeatedly**.
- Key reveal — he is **not using a PC**: "no pc j xbox." The monitor is driven by an **Xbox**, which only passes video over HDMI/DisplayPort and cannot run the ASUS firmware tool, explaining why every PC-dependent fix the assistant suggested was a dead end.

## Resolution path
The assistant eventually diagnosed the loop: holding the joystick is **double-mapped** — with a firmware USB drive detected it enters flash mode, otherwise it toggles key lock. Fix offered: remove any USB drive, power-cycle, then hold to get "Keys Unlocked," or use **System Setup → Key Lock → Off** in the OSD. The "Close" button held 5+ seconds is the official PG-series unlock per the ASUS FAQ. The chat ends mid-troubleshoot without confirmed success.

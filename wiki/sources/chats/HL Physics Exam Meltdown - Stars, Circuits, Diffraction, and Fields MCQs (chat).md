# HL Physics Exam Meltdown - Stars, Circuits, Diffraction, and Fields MCQs

A live, photo-driven [[IB Physics (HL)]] multiple-choice grind covering a huge slice of the HL syllabus, and notable for a candid moment of exam stress. Traveler works through screenshotted problems: the **Stefan–Boltzmann law** comparison of two stars (`L = 4πR²σT⁴` — luminosity up 4×, temp up 2× forces the radius to halve, since `T⁴` "nukes it by 16×"), albedo and planetary energy balance, the ideal-gas-law-needs-Kelvin rule, a switch-open/switch-closed **circuit** power problem (`P = I²R`, recomputing total current when a parallel path lowers resistance), a wire-shortcut three-resistors-in-parallel trick, a Moon pendulum (`T → √6 T`, mass is bait), wavefront-vs-ray refraction with Snell's law, two-source **interference** amplitude subtraction in antiphase, single-slit central-maximum width (`2λx/b`), an electric-field superposition between two positive charges, nuclear radius scaling (`R ∝ A^(1/3)`), and a de Broglie wavelength from kinetic energy (`p = √(2mE_k)`).

The human moment comes when he writes: **"explain further my brain is collapsing in real time and i've spent an hour and a half on the multiple choice section and have gotten maybe 6 right."** This is a rare, unguarded admission of academic strain — useful context for [[Academic Record]] and [[Emotional Life and Inner World]]. The tutor slows down, re-derives the field-superposition step intuitively (double charge ×2, double distance ÷4, "distance is the bully of physics"), and reframes his 90 minutes of grinding as endurance rather than failure. The exchange shows both the difficulty of HL Physics for him and his refusal to rage-quit.

The session reads like a final-stretch exam dry run, heavy on the "exam-writer trap" pattern recognition that IB MCQs reward. It connects to his broader [[IB Physics (HL)]] preparation and his pragmatic, push-through study temperament.

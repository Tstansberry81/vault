# Cold War Detente, University of Austin, and the 2025 Venezuela Crisis (chat)

A short three-topic grab-bag from December 2025 that hops from [[IB History (HL)]] coursework into college-app curiosity and then current-events geopolitics — a representative slice of how Traveler uses ChatGPT to triangulate across his school subjects and his political interests in one sitting.

## Cold War: exploiting the Sino-Soviet split
The opening question is straight [[IB History (HL)]] revision: how the U.S. tried to improve relations with the USSR *as* Soviet-Chinese relations soured. The answer walks the standard detente narrative — "peaceful coexistence," the 1963 Nuclear Test Ban Treaty, SALT, Nixon/Kissinger **triangular diplomacy** (opening China in 1972 to force both communist powers to compete for U.S. favor), and trade/cultural thaw (Apollo–Soyuz, 1975). This is the same Cold War unit covered in his other chats like [[Cold War Origins, Exponential Equations, and Movie Curiosity (chat)]], here viewed through the lens of the Sino-Soviet rift rather than origins.

## University of Austin (UATX) admissions
A pivot to college research — Traveler asks about "University of Austin" admission stats, and the model disambiguates the new (2021-founded) private liberal-arts UATX from UT Austin. Key facts surfaced: still unaccredited (MSCHE candidate status), a single B.A. in Liberal Studies, an "Intellectual Foundations" core plus a four-year "Polaris Project" capstone, free tuition for early cohorts, ~150 students, and a "merit-first" auto-admit at 1460 SAT / 33 ACT / 105 CLT. The model flags the accreditation risk for credit transfer and aid. A telling data point for his [[College Search]] mindset: even as a UVA-bound finance student ([[UVA and the Quant Question]]), he was scanning unconventional, ideologically distinctive schools — though UATX's STEM/business gap makes it a poor fit for his stated direction.

# Filing the Maryland LLC for Homework Hatch - Articles, EIN, and Stripe

A live, screen-by-screen walkthrough (Sept 13-15, 2025) of [[Traveler Stansberry]] actually incorporating [[Homework Hatch (startup)]] as a Maryland LLC. This is not hypothetical planning: he was clicking through Maryland Business Express (MBE) in real time, pasting screenshots of every form, and asking ChatGPT what to enter in each field. By the end the entity **HOMEWORK HATCH L.L.C.** shows "Approved" status, dated 9/15/2025 — a concrete formation milestone for the startup.

## What he actually did
- **State choice:** chose **Maryland** (where he lives) over the "startup-sexy" Delaware/Wyoming/Nevada options, prioritizing convenience.
- **Name lock:** searched SDAT's entity database; "Homework Hatch" was unregistered (the yellow "no results" box = good news). Wrestled with Maryland's broken reCAPTCHA on the public search portal.
- **Articles of Organization:** filed through MBE, entering principal office address, resident agent, and organizer info. He confirmed **18 is the age of majority** in Maryland so a student can be sole organizer/resident agent — a reminder he was doing this as a high-school senior.
- **Paid $425 total:** the $100 filing fee plus the **$325 same-day rush** (he wanted it fast, alongside setting up Stripe), plus Maryland's ~3% card "convenience" fee.
- **Approved 9/15/2025**, though it lagged in the public listing ("witness protection" per ChatGPT — SDAT's legacy IT syncs 24-72h late).
- **EIN application:** walked through the IRS EIN Assistant as single-member LLC, "Started a new business," responsible party = himself with SSN. Key gotcha caught live: the IRS rejects periods, so **"Homework Hatch L.L.C." had to be entered as "Homework Hatch LLC"**. Business category set to Other > Services > Educational Technology Software.

## Decisions and judgment shown
- **Order of operations** he internalized: LLC first → EIN second → bank account → Stripe, so Maryland, IRS, and Stripe records all match and he avoids "manual review hell." He asked repeatedly to confirm sequencing rather than rushing.
- **Stripe timing:** he was setting up Stripe the same day; ChatGPT advised registering Stripe cleanly under the LLC+EIN rather than as a sole prop he'd have to migrate.
- **Maryland digital-goods tax:** flagged that Maryland taxes SaaS/digital products at 6% (and new-in-2025 3% IT/data services). He noted he'd enable Stripe Tax and would register a Sales & Use account once charging Maryland residents — showing he's thinking about real paid customers, not just a beta.
- Deferred employer/payroll registration ("i plan to employ soon") — consistent with his ambition to actually grow the company.

# Maryland Sexting Law, Yellowstone Eruption, and Felony Speeding

A short, three-query grab-bag of personal curiosity questions [[Traveler Stansberry]] put to ChatGPT (gpt-4o, Aug 2025). The thread has no coursework or startup content — it reads as idle/situational fact-finding, with two of the three questions specific to Maryland law (where Traveler is from, consistent with the Maryland framing in his [[COVID Stimulus and Inflation in Maryland - Paper Research (chat)]] econ research).

## What he asked
- **Maryland child-pornography / Snapchat sexting law.** The longest exchange. The model summarized that under Md. Criminal Law §§ 11-207/11-208, any sexual depiction of a minor under 18 — including consensual, self-made images sent over Snapchat — can be a felony, citing *In Re S.K.* (2019), the 2021 juvenile-sexting law (mitigating factor, not a legalization), and the proposed HB 233. It stressed that Snapchat's disappearing messages confer no exemption and that federal law adds mandatory minimums.
- **Yellowstone supervolcano death toll.** A pure curiosity question: ~90,000 immediate local deaths, national ashfall and famine, and a possible decade-long volcanic winter (−5 to −15 °C) leading to potentially billions of deaths globally.
- **Maryland "felony speeding."** The model clarified there is no standalone felony-speeding statute, but noted the Sgt. Patrick Kepp Act (effective Oct 1, 2025) making 30+ mph over the limit criminal reckless driving, and that fatal/DUI/racing speeding can rise to vehicular manslaughter.

# Standing Waves, Thermodynamic Cycle, and a Pol Pot Aside

A sprawling [[IB Physics (HL)]] homework session from late April 2026, threaded with side trips into board-game strategy and Cold War history. Traveler opens with conceptual wave physics — standing waves explained through **phase difference** (nodes at π, antinodes at 0/2π), then mechanical waves (transverse vs longitudinal, `v = fλ`, medium-dependent speed). The bulk is a **fields + thermodynamics** grind: electric field inside a current-carrying conductor (`F = qE`, the field is what drives drift current), conductor/insulator/induction/conduction definitions, gravitational field strength and potential (the `V = -GM/r` family, `g = -dV/dr` as the gradient of a V–r graph), and a full ideal-gas cycle. The cycle work is the meatiest part: reading volumes off a p–V diagram, isothermal `pV = const`, isovolumetric `Q = ΔU`, adiabatic `pVᵍ = const` / `TVᵍ⁻¹ = const`, and deriving `U = (3/2)pV` from `U = (3/2)nRT` plus the gas law. Notably the model **flagged a discrepancy** — its adiabatic calc gave T_C ≈ 465 K, not the textbook's stated ~350 K, and said so rather than fudging it. Also reviews photoelectric effect (`E_k,max = hf − φ`), luminosity (`L = 4πR²σT⁴`), refraction, EMF definition fixes, and magnetic flux units (weber).

The non-physics detours: Monopoly strategy as economic allegory, a [[Family and Personal Life]]-adjacent filling-out of an Iran-Contra worksheet, and a comparison of **Pol Pot to Hitler and Mao** — the same Khmer Rouge / [[Individual vs. Society]] material that recurs across his [[IB History (HL)]] work and connects directly to his reading of *In the Shadow of the Banyan*.

This is classic Traveler exam-prep mode: cramming definitions and derivations at speed, demanding the "why" behind each formula, and tolerating no hand-waving from the tutor. It reflects his determinist, show-me-the-mechanism streak (see [[Political and Economic Views]], [[Intellectual Interests]]) — he repeatedly pushes back ("where do you get U = 3/2 pV from", "is GPE V/d") rather than accepting formulas on faith.

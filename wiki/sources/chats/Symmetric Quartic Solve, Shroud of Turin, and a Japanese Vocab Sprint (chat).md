# Symmetric Quartic Solve, Shroud of Turin, and a Japanese Vocab Sprint

A grab-bag ChatGPT session (titled "Solve algebraic equation", 2 Dec 2025) that wanders across four unrelated registers in one sitting — a single hard algebra problem, two curiosity questions, and a long machine-gun run of [[IB Japanese (SL)]] vocabulary lookups. It is a clean snapshot of how Traveler actually uses the tool: as a homework solver, a fact-checker, and a personal dictionary, all interleaved.

## The math: a symmetric quartic
He photographed and submitted the equation $(x+1)(x+3)(x+5)(x+7)=9$. The model solved it the elegant way rather than brute-force expansion — substitute $y=x+4$ to center the factors, pair them into $(y^2-9)(y^2-1)=9$, simplify to $y^4-10y^2=0$, factor as $y^2(y^2-10)=0$, and shift back. Solutions: $x=-4$, $x=\sqrt{10}-4$, $x=-\sqrt{10}-4$. The substitution-and-pairing trick (exploiting the symmetry of the arithmetic-progression factors) is a notch above standard [[IB Math (SL)]] technique and points toward the more ambitious problem-solving he gravitates to (cf. the [[UVA and the Quant Question]] math-minor track).

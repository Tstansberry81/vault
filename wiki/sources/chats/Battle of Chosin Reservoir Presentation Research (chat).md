# Battle of Chosin Reservoir Presentation Research

A focused research session for a **brief in-class presentation** Traveler was assigned, in which he had to pick from a list of Korean War battles (shown via an uploaded screenshot) and build a slide deck. ChatGPT recommended the **Battle of Chosin Reservoir** as the "most interesting" of the options, and Traveler ran with it. The chat shows him doing standard coursework prep for [[IB History (HL)]], scoping exactly the deliverables his teacher wanted and iterating until the material was citation-defensible.

His specification was telling: he asked for "extensive facts (dates, coordinates, geography, weather), leaders, acts of heroism or valor, **at least one firsthand account** (might make this the video), importance or effect on the Korean War in general, and the outcome." This mirrors the structured, criteria-driven way he approaches assessed work — front-loading the rubric requirements rather than free-writing.

## Content assembled
- **Frame:** Chosin (27 Nov – 13 Dec 1950) at Changjin Lake in northeastern North Korea (~40.2° N, 127.3° E), near the Yalu River. Cold (−30°F) as a "combatant," the Chinese encirclement, and the 1st Marine Division's fighting breakout to Hungnam rather than a rout.
- **Leaders:** Douglas MacArthur (overconfident supreme command), Oliver P. Smith (slowed the advance, stockpiled supplies, saved the division), Edward Almond, and the Chinese commander Peng Dehuai (~120,000 troops).
- **Valor:** Raymond G. Davis (Medal of Honor, Toktong Pass breakout), the Koto-ri airdropped-bridge engineering feat, and the immortal "Retreat? Hell, we're just attacking in another direction."
- **Outcome:** Chinese tactical victory but UN operational success — 100,000+ troops and civilians evacuated at Hungnam; the war then froze into the stalemate near the 38th Parallel.

## What it shows
Across three follow-ups Traveler kept pushing for **rigor over vibes**: he demanded sources "for everything," then real eyewitness testimony with valor examples, then direct **links**, then a sourced **chronological timeline** of main events. The escalating insistence on "IB-safe," teacher-proof citations (Appleman's *East of Chosin*, Hastings, Fehrenbach, Cumings, Medal of Honor citations, Naval History and Heritage Command) reflects how seriously he takes academic defensibility in his [[Academic Record]] — he wants material a teacher "can't poke holes in." It connects to his broader interest in military and Cold War history seen elsewhere in his [[IB History (HL)]] coursework.

> [!note] Caveat
> Some quotes ChatGPT supplied (the "ground cracked like glass" line) were attributed loosely or paraphrased; Traveler explicitly worked to anchor them to verifiable memoirs like *Colder Than Hell* — a sign he was screening AI output for citation reliability rather than trusting it wholesale.

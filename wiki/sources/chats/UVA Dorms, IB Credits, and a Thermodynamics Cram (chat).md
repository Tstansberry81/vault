## IB Physics HL thermodynamics (the bulk)
The conversation's center of gravity. Traveler admits "electromagnetism and induction are hard ash" and "physics hl might cook me," then works a full thermo unit live:
- A **steam-into-water calorimetry problem** (15 g steam at 120°C into 180 g water at 22°C → 71°C). He gets the answer but doesn't trust it, and walks himself through the three-stage energy accounting (cool steam → condense / latent heat → cool new water) until the "click": *"so the steam loses temp, condenses to water, but that new water has to cool... which then equals the energy of the increase in the water temp."* This is him learning thermal equilibrium by reasoning, not memorizing.
- ΔT sign conventions, why phase change is pinned to 100°C, why **water is anomalous** (hydrogen bonding → high specific heat + huge latent heat).
- **EP/EK across states of matter** — he trips on the classic trap (thinks solids have high potential energy; they have the lowest).
- Conduction `Q/t = kAΔT/L`, a steady-state rod problem, convection, radiation `P = σAT⁴`, and the **heat equation PDE** `∂T/∂t = α∇²T` as the "advanced" version — connecting to his recurring habit of reaching past the syllabus.
- **Entropy and the second law**: why no cyclic engine is 100% efficient, Carnot efficiency `η = 1 − Tc/Th` (derived both algebraically and via the entropy balance `Qin/Th = Qout/Tc`), reversible vs irreversible processes, and a corrected exam mistake where he assumed isovolumetric and isobaric paths give equal ΔS (isobaric is larger because of expansion).

He even submits a photo of his handwritten thermo notes for critique; the model rates them 7.5/10 — strong coverage, sloppy wording and typos ("temppressor," "liavids") that would cost marks. A characteristic moment: he is conceptually strong but imprecise, and pushing for the 6→7 jump. Tangents into [[Theory of Knowledge|Hawking radiation]] and black-hole thermodynamics show his physics curiosity outrunning the exam.

# Life Capital - A Poverty-Trap Board Game for Econ

A design session ([[ChatGPT]], gpt-5) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] develops an [[IB Economics (SL)]] project: a board game, working-titled **"Life Capital,"** built to make players *feel* the poverty trap rather than read about it. He starts by asking whether a card game can hand out different cards based on starting conditions (yes — preset branching vs. procedural generation), then feeds in his own scribbled notes for a Monopoly/Game-of-Life hybrid: a circular board, four life "currencies" (money, education, social, property), aging tracked by laps, and a "Retirement" (Total Life Value) endgame.

The economic heart of the conversation is his stated goal: **outcomes strongly shaped by random initial conditions**, demonstrating path dependence and structural inequality. The assistant pushes him to refine "outcome determined by initial conditions" into "strongly constrained by initial conditions but shaped by decisions and chance" — controlled determinism, not pure determinism — and proposes feedback loops (negative loop = trap, positive loop = compounding advantage), opportunity thresholds that lock low-income players out of upward mobility, asymmetric risk exposure (the same medical-expense card devastates the poor and barely dents the rich), and rare-but-real escape paths (scholarships, breakthroughs). Traveler explicitly notes "it's for an econ project, it's not supposed to be fair," clarifying the goal is a defensible economic model with a debrief covering poverty trap, capital accumulation, path dependence, and limited social mobility.

He ultimately overrides several suggestions, specifying his own final spec: randomized unfair starts but an equal playing field afterward, money/education/social-credit currencies from "year 0," two corner jails, no time constraints, investment spaces returning currency per lap, a youngest-age win condition, six card decks (money, education, social, life events, lottery, retirement), 2 dice, 4 tokens, board, and notepad — then asks for a formal, emoji-free instruction-manual-style rules document, which the assistant produces.

The chat reveals Traveler turning an abstract econ concept into a designed artifact and asserting control over the output — the same builder instinct behind [[Homework Hatch (startup)]]. It connects to [[IB Economics (SL)]], his interest in [[Individual vs. Society]] and inequality, and his determinist [[Political and Economic Views]].

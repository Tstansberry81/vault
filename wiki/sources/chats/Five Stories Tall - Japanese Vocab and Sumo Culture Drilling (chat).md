# Five Stories Tall - Japanese Vocab and Sumo Culture Drilling

A rapid-fire vocabulary and grammar drilling session for [[IB Japanese (SL)]], in which Traveler fed ChatGPT a string of one-line English prompts ("train in japanese", "going out to dinner in japanese", "because in japanese") and collected the natural Japanese equivalents. The chat reads as on-demand reference building rather than structured study — likely prep for a conversation, essay, or speaking assessment. The assistant's snarky persona runs throughout, but the substance is solid functional Japanese.

## Vocabulary collected
- **五階建て（ごかいだて）** — "five-story building"; buildings counted with 階 (kai), not story-as-loanword.
- **電車 / 列車 / 新幹線 / 地下鉄** — train (common), train (formal), bullet train ("new main line"), subway.
- **夕食を食べに行く / 外食する** — go out to dinner; Japanese says "go to eat dinner," not "go out to dinner."
- **お正月 / 元日 / 新年** — New Year (season / the day Jan 1 / formal-written), plus あけましておめでとうございます and 良いお年を.
- **交通 / 交通機関 / 輸送 / 移動** — transportation (general / systems / cargo / getting around).
- **大切 / 重要 / 大事** — important (emotional / formal-objective / casual-crucial).
- **面白い / 楽しい / 魅力的 / 興味深い** — entertaining/interesting/fun/charming/intriguing.
- **危ない ↔ 安全**, and 不安 ↔ 安心 (objectively safe vs. feeling at ease).
- **女の人（おんなのひと） / 大人（おとな）** — corrected from his romaji guesses "onanohito" and "otona."

## Grammar points
He repeatedly tried to "attach a comma to the end of an い-adjective," which the assistant pushed back on: Japanese connects clauses with **grammar, not punctuation**. The teaching sequence landed on:
- **〜くて** form to mean "and" (高くて美味しい = "expensive and delicious")
- **けど / でも / しかし** for "but" (高いけど美味しい)
- **から / ので / だから** for "because/therefore" (placement at clause-end vs. sentence-start)
- **したがって / ゆえに** as the formal/literary "therefore" registers
- **〜たことがない** for "I have never ___" (見たことがない — "I don't have the experience of seeing it").

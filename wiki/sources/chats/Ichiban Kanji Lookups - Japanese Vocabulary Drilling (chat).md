# Ichiban Kanji Lookups - Japanese Vocabulary Drilling

A rapid-fire vocabulary session for [[IB Japanese (SL)]] in which Traveler fed ChatGPT a stream of romaji words and asked for each one's kanji, breakdown, and usage. The model replied in casual Japanese (with a brash, slangy register) plus English glosses — essentially functioning as a flashcard generator. The pattern mirrors his other Japanese-help chats (e.g. *Macro AD-AS Review and Japanese Ab Initio Kanji Flashcards*), confirming kanji acquisition is a recurring grind for him.

## What he looked up
The list skews toward abstract/academic vocabulary rather than survival phrases, hinting at coursework-level material:
- **一番** (ichiban) = best/number one; **文学** (bungaku) = literature; **教育** (kyōiku) = education; **難しい** (muzukashii) = difficult; **思います** (omoimasu) = I think.
- **毎年** (maitoshi/mainen), **友達** (tomodachi), **花屋** (hanaya, florist), **綺麗** (kirei).
- Verbs with multiple kanji: **書く/描く/掻く/欠く** (kaku), **買います/飼います** (kaimasu, buy vs. keep a pet), **知ります** (shirimasu).
- A surprise: **字牌** (jihai) — the honor tiles in mahjong, suggesting a gaming/hobby tangent.

## Useful nuances the model surfaced
- **いい / 良い** and **きれい / 綺麗** and **ください / 下さい**: all officially have kanji but are normally written in hiragana; the model flagged kanji versions as stiff/textbook-ish — practical advice for exam writing.
- **知ります** is grammatically correct but unnatural; natives use 知っています / 知りません. This kind of register coaching is more than a dictionary would give.
- **〜屋** as a productive shop-suffix (魚屋, 八百屋, 本屋, パン屋).

## Personal tells
Two queries break the pattern. He asked how to say "I have been to a construction site" (建設現場に行ったことがあります) — the model first corrected his English ("you wrestled English to the ground and lost"). And he asked how to ask "Do you know a history teacher called Mr. Yokoyama?" in Japanese, getting register-graded options from casual to keigo (ご存じですか). The Yokoyama question ties his [[IB Japanese (SL)]] practice to his [[IB History (HL)]] world — likely a real teacher he was constructing a sentence about.

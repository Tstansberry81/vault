A grab-bag homework session (Feb 2025) that starts as an [[IB Economics (SL)]] assignment and drifts into a tour of [[IB Math (SL)]]-adjacent curiosity. It shows Traveler using ChatGPT as both a graded-worksheet checker and a casual "explain cool things" tutor.

## GDP inclusion worksheet
The core task: a ten-item drill on whether each transaction counts in **GDP**, the staple national-income-accounting exercise from [[IB Economics (SL)]] macro. The answers Traveler accepted reinforce the standard rules:
- **Counts:** new Wrangler boots (final good), Amazon warehouse construction (investment), the friend's paid lawn-mowing (a market service).
- **Excluded:** vintage Burberry resale (not current production), self-painting your own home (unpaid domestic work), the burglar's stolen jewelry (illegal/unreported), ostrich feathers (intermediate good — only the finished hats count), Nike stock issuance (financial transaction), unemployment benefits (transfer payment), the Xfinity–Comcast asset takeover (financial transaction), and slot-machine winnings (a transfer, though the casino's operating revenue does count).

## The drift: calculus and chaos
He then asks for calculus "explained briefly to a junior in highschool without any trigonometry knowledge" — placing himself as a junior at the time, pre-[[IB Math (SL)]] formalization. The explanation he got frames **derivatives** as instantaneous rate of change (speedometer, slope of a tangent) and **integrals** as accumulation (area under a curve, total distance), tied together via position → speed → distance. He pushed for more depth and worked examples (throwing a ball, filling a tank).

Then a sharp left turn into the **Feigenbaum constants** (δ ≈ 4.669, α ≈ 2.503), period-doubling, the logistic map, and the universality of routes to chaos. This is well beyond any IB syllabus — pure intellectual appetite, consistent with his documented [[Intellectual Interests]] and the curiosity that underwrites the [[UVA and the Quant Question]].

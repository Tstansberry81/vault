# Electric Field Strength then an Integration Homework Drill

A homework-packet session that splits cleanly in two: a short [[IB Physics (HL)]] electrostatics primer, then a long [[IB Math (SL)]] integration drill. Traveler opens working through an HL physics packet ("help me define electric field strength") and then pivots — without comment — into roughly a dozen integral evaluations, the kind of two-subject grab-bag that recurs across his coursework chats.

## Electrostatics (HL Physics)
He asks ChatGPT to define the three linked quantities of the electric-fields unit, and the model separates them cleanly — a distinction he is being asked to internalize:
- **Field strength** E = F/q (N C⁻¹, vector) — force per unit positive charge; point charge E = kQ/r².
- **Potential energy** U = qV, or kQq/r for two charges (J) — energy of position; sign tells you whether the system is bound.
- **Potential** V = U/q (volts, scalar) — energy per unit charge; point charge V = kQ/r.

The recurring mnemonic across the exchange: field goes as 1/r², potential and PE as 1/r. This is standard [[IB Physics (HL)]] fare alongside his many momentum, rotational-motion, and gravitation sessions.

## Integration drill (Math SL)
The bulk of the chat is mechanical antiderivative and definite-integral practice, escalating in difficulty:
- Trig basics: ∫sin x = −cos x, ∫₀^π sin x = 2, ∫sin²x via the half-angle identity (½ on [0, π/2] gives π/4).
- A logarithm thread: ∫1/x = **ln|x|**, and a genuine conceptual catch — Traveler pushes back ("i thought u cant plug in negative numbers to a natural log function"), and the absolute value is explained as structural, not decorative. He then solves for an unknown lower bound m in ∫ from m to −2 of 1/(4−x) = ln(3/2), correctly rejecting m = 13 because the interval would cross the asymptote at x = 4.
- u-substitution he explicitly wanted demystified ("how did u do the du part where u figure out what number u have to multiply by") — answered as: pick u, differentiate, rearrange for dx. Applied to ∫x·e^(−2x²), ∫x/(x²+2)², and ∫sin²x·cos x.
- Integration by parts for ∫ln x = x ln x − x + C.

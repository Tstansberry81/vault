# IB English Paper 1 Prep, the Over-Scaffolding Audit, and Paper 2 Refresh

Night-before and morning-of cram for the [[IB English (SL)|IB English]] exams (logged 2026-05-06). The chat braids three things: a deep [[Paper 2 Study Guide|Paper 1]] rubric/strategy session, a striking self-audit of how [[Traveler Stansberry]] uses ChatGPT, and a Paper 2 memory refresh. It is one of the clearest windows into how he actually thinks about analysis and about his own learning.

## Paper 1: building a portable interpretive engine
Traveler extracts the mechanics — Paper 1 is unseen textual analysis, 20 marks across four 5-mark criteria (understanding/interpretation, analysis/evaluation, organization, language), HL doing two texts. He confirms his teacher's **OMI** thesis structure (Observation → Interpretation → Meaning) works, and decides to run 3 authorial choices, one per body paragraph (defaults: imagery, diction, structure/tone; narrative perspective for prose, enjambment for poetry).

The intellectual core is his argument that **almost any text can be read through [[Identity and Its Collapse|identity]]**: "if all literature is suggesting something relating to human experience, i can tie it back to some form of new postulate on identity." He pressure-tests this against the AI, which pushes back — human experience is the umbrella; identity is the strongest *consequence*, not a lazy starting label. He lands the distinction himself: lead with the specific force, end on the identity claim. His worked example is a [[Beloved]] thesis (animal imagery, the cherry tree and tobacco tin motifs, personification of the past → trauma outlives the historical event → identity fragmented), which the chat refines but praises as genuine analysis. This is exactly the [[Narrative Disruption and Identity - Beloved vs Murakami Paper 2 (chat)|human-experience → identity]] move he reuses everywhere.

His go-list for the morning: "diction, imagery, symbolism, juxtaposition, enjambment, stanzas, motifs, personification, identity." He refuses to write practice essays. The exam pays off immediately — Paper 1 handed him "how and to what extent does this poem explore identity?", which he calls "easiest shit ever."

## The over-scaffolding audit
The standout passage: he asks for a "brutally honest" analysis of his own ChatGPT usage and its trade-offs (excluding physics, "as we genuinely did not learn that shit in class"). The verdict — his best use is turning messy thinking into sharper frameworks and acting as a bullshit detector ("Am I tripping? Would this score well?"); his risk is becoming **over-scaffolded**: reaching for AI before wrestling with a problem, mistaking recognition for production, outsourcing phrasing, and using it as a "confidence stabilizer." He half-agrees, calling the tool "a plague" that ruins critical thinking for some, but insists he genuinely values reaching his own conclusions, "ESPECIALLy with math." He admits abusing the help in physics and sometimes math, while noting peers who paste prompts, launder them through GPTZero, and submit to Canvas — a line he won't cross. He also confirms a senior-year unweighted GPA of 4.02 (straight As), reinforcing his [[Academic Record]].

## Paper 2 refresh
He pivots to Paper 2 (comparative, two works, detailed references but not memorized quotes), feeding in his own outline. The set is [[Beloved]], [[The Elephant Vanishes|Murakami]], Tracy K. Smith's *Wade in the Water*, and [[Death of a Salesman]] — recurring axes being identity collapse, [[Memory and Trauma|trauma/memory]], [[Masculinity]], materialism, and external validation. He needs the most help on *Death of a Salesman* (read seven months prior), unpacking Willy Loman's diamond obsession and pairing Beloved + Salesman for identity, masculinity, family, the past, and [[Identity and Its Collapse|collapse]]. The default comparative engine: both texts show identity under pressure, differing in what causes the collapse and whether recovery is possible — the same frame as his [[Narrative Disruption and Identity - Beloved vs Murakami Paper 2 (chat)|Beloved vs Murakami]] work.

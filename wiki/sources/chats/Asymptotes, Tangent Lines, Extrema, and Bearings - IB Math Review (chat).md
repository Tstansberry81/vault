# Asymptotes, Tangent Lines, Extrema, and Bearings - IB Math Review

A focused [[IB Math (SL)]] revision session from December 2025, almost certainly a pre-test or midterm cram. Traveler walks the model through a chain of standard topics, asking for clean explanations and progressively harder problems. The session is pure concept-reinforcement: no IA, no real-world hook, just drilling exam mechanics.

## What he asked, in order

1. **Rational-function asymptotes** — he opens by admitting he *always* confuses vertical (VA) and horizontal (HA) asymptotes. The model distinguishes them: a **VA** comes from a non-cancelling zero of the denominator (set denominator = 0, solve, check it doesn't cancel into a hole); an **HA** comes from end behavior, found by comparing numerator/denominator degrees (n<d → y=0; n=d → ratio of leading coefficients; n>d → slant, no HA). The framing he keeps: "VA cares about where you can't go, HA cares about where you end up."

2. **Derivatives, tangent lines, and extrema** — the limit definition of the derivative as slope; the four-step tangent-line procedure (derivative → slope at a → point (a, f(a)) → point-slope form); critical points where f'(x)=0 or undefined; the **first derivative test** (+→− is a max, −→+ is a min) and **second derivative test** (f''>0 cup/min, f''<0 cap/max); and absolute extrema on a closed interval by checking critical points *and* endpoints.

3. **Hard tangent problems (finding the point)** — the more revealing request. He specifically wanted the cases where the point of tangency is unknown. Four problem types: tangent with a given slope, tangent passing through an external point (call it x=a, build the line, force it through the point), tangent parallel to a line (equal slopes), and tangent perpendicular to a line (slope −1/m). The unifying method: the unknown is almost always the point a, so match slopes and solve.

4. **Angles of elevation and depression** — both measured from the horizontal, and equal to each other because horizontal lines are parallel; every problem collapses to a right triangle and SOH-CAH-TOA.

5. **Bearings** — measured from North, clockwise, always three digits (045°, not 45°), describing direction not distance; reverse bearing = θ ± 180°; every problem is a hidden right triangle.

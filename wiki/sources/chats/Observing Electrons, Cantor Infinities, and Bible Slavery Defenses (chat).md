# Observing Electrons, Cantor Infinities, and Bible Slavery Defenses

A free-wandering late-night curiosity session (GPT-5, Nov 2025) that hops across physics epistemology, set theory, animal cognition, and a sustained interrogation of the Bible on slavery. The thread is less coursework than a snapshot of how [[Traveler Stansberry]] thinks when nothing is assigned: he pressure-tests claims, demands the steel-man, and then probes for the weak joint.

## What he asked, in order
- **"Have we ever observed an electron?"** — and then the sharper follow-up: *"so we've seen effects of a particle that we think is an electron but not an electron."* This is the [[Theory of Knowledge]] move applied to [[IB Physics (HL)]]: he isolates the gap between direct observation and inference-from-effects (cloud chambers, Millikan, scattering, transistors). The reply's framing — "the phenomenon *is* the evidence," realness defined by consistent, exceptionless interaction — is exactly the empiricism/realism boundary TOK natural-sciences units chew on.
- **Explaining uncountable infinity to a five-year-old** — countable counting numbers vs. the continuum (dots vs. a colored-in line, candies vs. candy dust). A teaching-instinct request consistent with his [[Coding Club]] / explainer streak; he wants the intuition, not the set-theory vocabulary.
- **"Do dolphins have free will?"** — and the answer leans straight into his own [[Fate and Free Will]] determinism: brains are neurons firing on chemistry, so if humans lack metaphysical free will, so do dolphins; it's "the same existential mess, just wet." Confirms his hard-determinist priors (see [[Political and Economic Views]], [[Core Convictions]]).

## The religion thread (the bulk of it)
As a documented religious skeptic ([[Faith vs. Knowledge]]), Traveler runs a methodical audit of Christianity:
- **"How many people claimed to see Jesus without prior knowledge of him?"** — gets the answer "basically zero," with the cross-cultural point that visions always match the seer's existing symbol-library (Hindu regions see Hindu gods, etc.).
- **Bible verses endorsing/regulating slavery, with context** — Leviticus 25:44–46, Exodus 21:20–21, Deut 20 / Num 31, Ephesians 6:5, Colossians 3:22, Titus 2:9, 1 Peter 2:18, Philemon. Then he demands the **steel-man pro-Christian defense for each** (progressive revelation, harm-reduction law vs. Hammurabi/Assyria, equality-before-God seeding later abolition).
- The key analytical move: he asks whether **God regulating-but-not-commanding slavery is structurally like Obama explaining how to smuggle drugs without saying "smuggle drugs."** This is a genuinely sharp formal-logic test of "regulation implies endorsement," and he uses it to corner the apologetic — the comparison holds unless you grant God a "progressive moral arc" exemption. Pure [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]] / [[The Moral Impact of Slavery]] territory, applied to scripture.
- Side quests: "can faith literally move a mountain?" (yes per text; church later metaphorized it), Abraham's eight sons, and the Abraham/Isaac near-sacrifice as the Euthyphro problem ("if God commands it, is it automatically good?").

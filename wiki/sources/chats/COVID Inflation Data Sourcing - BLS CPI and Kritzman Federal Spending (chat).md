# COVID Inflation Data Sourcing - BLS CPI and Kritzman Federal Spending

A data-hunting session for the [[Extended Essay (Economics)]], in which Traveler pulls hard figures and citable sources for his argument that federal fiscal stimulus, not supply chains, was the leading driver of post-COVID inflation in the U.S. and Maryland. The chat is almost entirely fact-checking and source-formatting requests — the empirical backbone of the EE rather than its prose.

## What he was gathering

- **Headline COVID/economic stats**: global CFR ~1%, S&P 500 ~34% peak-to-trough crash (Feb 19 → Mar 23, 2020) with recovery to peak by mid-Aug 2020, and the [[Paycheck Protection Program]] "$800 billion" figure (NBER working paper).
- **CPI / inflation series** from FRED and especially the **Bureau of Labor Statistics**, his preferred authority. He insisted repeatedly on *non-PDF HTML links* and a single consistent source — examiner-proofing his citations.
- **National vs. Maryland (Baltimore-Columbia-Towson metro) inflation**, June-to-June, which becomes a structural device for the EE's local angle:

| Period | U.S. CPI-U | Baltimore metro |
|---|---|---|
| Jun 2020 → Jun 2021 | ~5.4% | ~4.5% |
| Jun 2021 → Jun 2022 | ~9.1% | ~10.6% |
| Jun 2022 → Jun 2023 | ~3.0% | ~2.8% |

- The **June 2022 peak**: 9.1% CPI-U (largest 12-month rise since 1981), energy +41.6% YoY, gasoline +59.9%, utility/natural gas +38.4%, food +10.4%.
- **Unemployment** as a before/after frame: 3.5% (Feb 2020, 50-year low) → 14.8% (Apr 2020, highest since 1948) → back to ~3.5% by mid-2022.
- **Oil prices**: WTI went briefly negative (-$37) in Apr 2020, rebounding ~250% to ~$70 by mid-2021, then +33% from ~$90 to ~$120 after the Russian invasion. He used this to argue the *bigger* swing came from the pandemic rebound, not Ukraine.

## The core argument he tested

He fed ChatGPT a full EE paragraph built around **Mark Kritzman (MIT Sloan, 2022)**, who "shows mathematically" that federal spending — not supply chains — was the overwhelming inflation driver, attributing ~42% of the burst to government spending and finding it 200–300% more central than other factors. ChatGPT validated the Kritzman claim but flagged the paragraph as overstated: saying inflation was "not correlated with oil" is wrong, since energy was a major accelerant. The recommended nuance — *fiscal expansion explains the magnitude and persistence, supply/oil shocks explain the timing and spikes* — is a sharper version of the EE's thesis.

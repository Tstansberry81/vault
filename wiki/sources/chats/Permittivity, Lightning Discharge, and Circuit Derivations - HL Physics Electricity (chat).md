## What was covered

- **Permittivity of free space**: ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² F/m, its role in Coulomb's law (k = 1/4πε₀ ≈ 8.99×10⁹), and the link to the speed of light via c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) — Maxwell's demonstration that light is an electromagnetic wave.
- **Natural-log derivative rules**: a quick aside into [[IB Math (SL)]] territory — (ln x)' = 1/x, the chain-rule form f'(x)/f(x), ln|x|, and the ∫(1/x)dx = ln|x| connection. Shows the two courses bleeding together in his homework queue.
- **A lightning problem** (from a photographed worksheet): discharge time t = Q/I = 35/1800 ≈ 0.02 s, and energy E = QV = 35 × 2.5×10⁸ ≈ 8.8×10⁹ J (roughly 2 tons of TNT).
- **Electrostatics conceptual question**: plastic (insulator) holds charge at the rubbed end while copper (conductor) drains to ground through the hand; drawing field lines for two like positive charges (bowing outward, no connection) and the perpendicular equipotential midline.
- **Resistivity/circuit problem**: length of copper wire via R = ρL/A ⟹ L ≈ 9 m; why metal resistance rises with temperature; current I = √(P/R) = 0.82 A; and a series variable-resistor solve, R_total = V/I = 7.32 Ω ⟹ R ≈ 4.0 Ω.
- **Derivations**: building P = IV from P = W/t, W = QV, and I = Q/t; then P = I²R and P = V²/R via Ohm's law; and the definition V = W/Q with the unit 1 V = 1 J/C.

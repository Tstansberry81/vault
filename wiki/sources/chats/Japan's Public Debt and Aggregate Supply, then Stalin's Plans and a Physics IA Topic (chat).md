# Japan's Public Debt and Aggregate Supply, then Stalin's Plans and a Physics IA Topic

A grab-bag homework session (16 Nov 2025, GPT-5.1) that ran across three subjects, showing Traveler bouncing between [[IB Economics (SL)]], [[IB History (HL)]], and [[IB Physics (HL)]] in a single sitting.

## Economics: public debt and aggregate supply
The chat opened with Traveler probing whether **high public debt reduces aggregate supply** — likely a [[Paper 2 Study Guide]]-style AD-AS conceptual check. The takeaway he was steered toward: debt is a *stock variable* that does not shift AS directly; it can drag on long-run AS only **indirectly** via crowding out (higher interest rates cutting private investment), higher future taxes, or inflation expectations raising input costs — and can even *raise* LRAS if borrowed money funds productive investment (infrastructure, R&D, education).

He then applied this to **Japan** specifically, which connects to his recurring interest in Japanese macro (see [[Macro Econ IA - Japan's Negative Interest Rates and Economic Growth (chat)]]). The conclusion: Japan's debt does *not* meaningfully suppress AS because near-zero/negative rates mean no crowding out, the Bank of Japan holds most of the debt domestically (borrowing "from itself"), and Japan has fought *too little* inflation, not too much. Japan's weak GDP is attributed instead to **demographics** — shrinking working-age population, low fertility, near-zero immigration, weak service-sector productivity, zombie firms, and deflationary psychology. "The debt is a monster, but it's domesticated. The population decline is the real kaiju."

He asked for sources on Japan's aging/low birthrate (World Bank, WEF, FT "lowest births in 125 years," MacroTrends) and an MLA citation for one — the same sourcing-and-citation workflow visible across his [[Extended Essay (Economics)]] chats, though here applied to Japan rather than his US/Maryland fiscal-policy EE topic.

## History: Stalin's economic terror
Mid-chat he pivoted to [[IB History (HL)]] content: **Stalin's Five-Year Plans** (1928–32 onward — forced industrialization, collectivization, crushing the kulaks, building the Soviet war machine at obscene human cost) and the **Holodomor** (the man-made 1932–33 Ukrainian famine, 3–4 million dead, the intentionality/genocide debate). He also nailed down the vocabulary: **kolkhoz** (collective farm) vs **sovkhoz** (state farm). This is interwar/totalitarianism material adjacent to his other 20th-century history work.

## Physics: HL IA topic selection
He then asked for HL Physics IA topics and zeroed in on **#3, rolling resistance of a cart on an incline**. Crucially, he rejected the surface-roughness version ("i cant quantify surfaces") and confirmed that **varying the incline angle alone** is a valid IA, using the model a = g(sinθ − μᵣcosθ) and linearizing to extract μᵣ from a graph of acceleration vs sinθ. This is a concrete data point on his actual [[IB Physics (HL)]] internal assessment direction — a clean, math-heavy, equipment-light experiment chosen to avoid "operational definition" headaches.

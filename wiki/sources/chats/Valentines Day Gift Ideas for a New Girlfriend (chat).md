# Valentine's Day Gift Ideas for a New Girlfriend

A personal-life planning chat (Feb 2026) in which [[Traveler Stansberry]], then 18, brainstorms Valentine's Day gifts for a girl he had started dating "about a week ago." It is one of the few sources that documents his romantic life rather than coursework, startup work, or finance, and it sketches both her personality and his own self-image in relationships.

## The girlfriend
He describes her as "sort of alternative" — dark hair, brown eyes, funny, "not girly in the typical sense but definitely still a girl." Shared interests he lists: the band **The Backseat Lovers**, **Clairo**, building LEGO, pickleball, analyzing films (**Christopher Nolan**), and talking philosophy. He calls her "a clone of me essentially," which mirrors his documented [[Intellectual Interests]] (philosophy, film, determinism). A later message adds an important characterization: she is "not super emotional or showy," rarely compliments him or discusses feelings, and when she does it's "laced in sarcasm." He notes he is her first boyfriend and frames accommodating her low-key, non-performative style as his responsibility — "I have to accommodate that."

## The plan
His existing plan: cook for her, decorate the basement with flowers and balloons, a card and candy in a basket. He's already getting her a Backseat Lovers album for her birthday two weeks later. Across the chat he asks for additional gift ideas, considers a blanket (her favorite color is blue; he asks specifically for blue blankets with hearts), and the model returns a product list (sherpa, indie-aesthetic throw, Vera Bradley, etc.).

The most substantive exchange is the last one: given her sarcastic, non-effusive personality, the advice converges on gifts that are "useful or experiential, quietly thoughtful, low cringe, and capable of being enjoyed without a big reaction" — a short philosophy/film-theory book, a shared LEGO build or two-player strategy game, a practical blue item (mug, candle, beanie), and a short, observational (not gushy) note. The framing: adapt to how she expresses closeness rather than demanding she match his emotional volume.

## Japanese (school-event phrasing)
He asked for natural Japanese to describe a school orientation scene — likely tied to a [[IB Japanese (SL)|Japanese SL]] writing or speaking task drawing on his Japan study-abroad experience (see [[Japan Study Abroad Common App Essay - Getting Lost and Adapting (chat)|Japan Study Abroad Common App Essay - Getting Lost and Adapting]]):

- 教室 (*kyōshitsu*) = classroom, literally "teaching room."

## Kepler's Laws and the nature of gravity
The bulk of the chat is a physics curiosity arc, starting from "Kepler's laws" and escalating into open foundational questions:
- The three laws — ellipses (Sun at one focus), equal areas (angular-momentum conservation, faster at perihelion), and harmonies (T² ∝ r³).
- Deeper dive on the Third Law, including Newton's exact form T²/r³ = 4π²/GM, and how combining it with Big G lets you weigh any star or planet.
- Big G itself: G = 6.674×10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg², first measured by Cavendish's 1798 torsion balance ("weighed the Earth in his basement").
- Contemporary theories of *why* mass attracts mass: General Relativity (gravity as spacetime curvature, not a force), quantum field theory and the hypothetical spin-2 graviton, string theory, loop quantum gravity, and emergent/alternative models (Verlinde's entropic gravity, MOND/modified gravity, superfluid vacuum). Takeaway: we know *how* gravity behaves to 14 decimals but not *what* it is.

# Momentum Conservation, Pendulum Tension, and Impulse from a Wrecking Ball

A photo-driven [[IB Physics (HL)]] homework session from the start of senior year (Sep 2025), in which Traveler works through a worksheet on **momentum, impulse, and circular-motion tension** by snapping pictures of each problem and his own scratch work. The chat is notable less for the physics (standard intro mechanics) than for how it documents his **learning gaps and self-correction process** — he repeatedly stops the assistant to demand derivations from first principles rather than accept plug-and-chug answers.

## Problems worked
- **Truck–car momentum exchange**: total momentum conserved, solving for a car's final velocity (≈21.3 m/s). The assistant flags that his scratch work had set up a flawed "difference equation" instead of `initial total p = final total p`.
- **Alpha decay recoil**: ratio of alpha-particle to recoiling-nucleus speed (≈55×) from momentum conservation in an isolated system.
- **Falling apple**: a conceptual trap — momentum conservation does *not* apply to a freely falling object because gravity is an external force, unless you take "apple + Earth" as the isolated system.
- **Wrecking ball as pendulum** (the chat's spine): tension at rest (`T = mg ≈ 7.6 kN`), speed at the bottom via energy conservation (`v = √(2gh) ≈ 5.6 m/s`), and tension at the lowest point (`T = mg + mv²/L ≈ 9.6 kN`).
- **Impulse from a force–time graph**: area under a triangular F–t curve = impulse = Δp; solving for `F_max ≈ 5.8×10⁴ N`. The assistant catches that his answer was exactly half-size because he forgot the ½ in the triangle's area.
- **Cart collision** using impulse to find the unknown cart's mass (≈3.1 kg), then conservation of momentum for the struck cart's final velocity, plus reading a v–t graph for acceleration, distance, and inferring a ramp's height (≈0.12 m).

## What it reveals
His questions are the interesting part. He explicitly admits "**haven't done this in 6 months**" and asks for a full from-scratch breakdown of free-body diagrams, tension, and Newton's second law — suggesting physics had gone dormant over the summer. He pushes back hard on the centripetal term: "where do u get the mv²/L," "could you solve this without knowing about centripetal force we haven't been taught about it yet," and probes whether there's a horizontal force component proportional to the rope angle (a genuinely sharp instinct — correct for a general swing angle, but zero at the bottom where the rope is vertical). He also interrogates *why* `vᵢ² − vf²` and not the reverse, and confirms unit analysis that `mv²/L` comes out in Newtons. This is the same insist-on-the-derivation, distrust-the-formula temperament visible across his [[IB Math (SL)]] and [[Intellectual Interests|intellectual]] work — he wants the mechanism, not the magic.

# Circular Motion and Gravitation Homework - Centripetal Force, Kepler, and Gravitational Fields

A [[IB Physics (HL)]] homework session (HL2, Oct 2025) in which Traveler photographs a multiple-choice problem set on circular motion and gravitation and has ChatGPT (GPT-5) work through each item. The transcript is essentially answer-checking plus a compact formula reference — a snapshot of his HL2 mechanics unit and his standard workflow of snapping worksheet photos for instant worked solutions.

## What was covered
The set moved through the standard HL circular-motion / gravity sequence:
- **Centripetal force does no work** — perpendicular to velocity, so `W = Fd·cos(90°) = 0`. He had originally marked the wrong answer (B) and was corrected.
- **Friction as centripetal force** for a brick on a rotating disc; force points toward center, not tangent or outward (no real centrifugal force).
- **Satellite in orbit** — the net force *is* gravity; gravity supplies the centripetal force rather than being separate from it.
- **Banked aircraft turn** — lift tilts inward, vertical component balances weight, horizontal component provides centripetal force.
- **Kepler's Third Law** — `T² ∝ r³`, so `T_X/T_Y = 5^(3/2)` for a 5× orbital radius.
- **Escape speed** `v_e = √(2gr)`, derived by substituting `GM = gr²`.
- **Surface gravity scaling** — mass 300×, radius 10× gives `g_X = 3g`; and for equal density, `g ∝ R`, giving `g_Y = 3·18 = 54 m/s²`.
- **Gravitational field vs potential at a point** between two masses: fields are vectors (subtract), potentials are scalars (add), giving `g_net = Gm/2d²` toward P and `V_net = −2Gm/d`.

## Formula reference he asked for
He explicitly requested the centripetal force, velocity, and acceleration equations, getting the full set:
`F_c = mv²/r = mω²r = 4π²mr/T²`, `v = ωr = 2πr/T`, `a_c = v²/r = ω²r`, plus tangential acceleration `a_t = αr` and total `a = √(a_c² + a_t²)`.

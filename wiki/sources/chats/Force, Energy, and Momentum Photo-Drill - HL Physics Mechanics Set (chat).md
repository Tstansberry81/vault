# Force, Energy, and Momentum Photo-Drill - HL Physics Mechanics Set

A photo-driven homework session for [[IB Physics (HL)]] in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] feeds ChatGPT screenshots of exam questions and works through a full mechanics unit: forces, energy conservation, momentum, and power. The set is heavy on the chained, multi-part IB problem where one answer feeds the next.

Topics covered: reading acceleration off a velocity-time graph and using Newton's second law (mg − F_air = ma) to find air resistance on a falling ball (3 N), then reasoning that air resistance grows with speed; an energy-bookkeeping problem where gravitational PE lost minus KE gained equals 780 J dissipated, converted via Q = mcΔT into a 3.3 K temperature rise; a perfectly inelastic collision (ball + spike) using momentum conservation then KE loss (~10 J dissipated) and work-energy (Fd = KE after collision) to find a ~610 N friction force; projectile motion off a table; and a constant-speed car climbing a hill (work against gravity = 3.6×10⁶ J, power = 12 kW minimum).

The pedagogically revealing parts are Traveler's conceptual confusions, which the tutor untangles: he initially thinks Fd should equal the 10 J lost in the collision (it doesn't — that energy is gone before the spike moves), and he wrongly treats the friction force as both driving and resisting the spike (it only resists; momentum drives). He also defends a t = d/v calculation that secretly assumed no acceleration in a deceleration problem, and gets two definition/derivation nitpicks: power must be stated as "rate of energy transfer" (not just "joules per second"), and P = Fv should be derived cleanly from P = W/t = Fd/t. The ChatGPT persona repeatedly notes his physics instincts are sound but his execution is messy — he "freestyles" equations rather than picking the right tool, a recurring theme in his mechanics work.

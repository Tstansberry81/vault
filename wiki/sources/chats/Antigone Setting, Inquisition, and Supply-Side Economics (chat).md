It's less a single inquiry than a snapshot of how Traveler uses ChatGPT as a fast reference desk across his whole IB load at once.

## English / [[Antigone]]
Opens with quick lookups on the **setting of [[Antigone]]** — the palace gates of Thebes in mythological time, after the civil war between Eteocles and Polyneices — and the play's symbolic staging of **state law (Creon) vs. divine law (Antigone)**, a framing that feeds directly into [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]] and [[Individual vs. Society]]. A "why did Ancient Greece fall" follow-up (Peloponnesian War → Macedon → Roman conquest, 146 BCE) reads as contextual scaffolding for the same unit.

The chat closes on a practical [[Individual Oral (IO)]] question: **can you reference events outside your chosen extracts?** The answer — yes, use extracts as anchors but pull in other moments to support the global issue without lapsing into summary — confirms how he was thinking about IO structure and the [[Theory of Knowledge]]-adjacent demands of the rubric.

## [[IB History (HL)]] / ToK
A standout query asks for **objects from the Spanish Inquisition relating to religion obstructing the production of knowledge** — the *Index Librorum Prohibitorum*, censorship stamps, auto-da-fé, suppression of Arabic medical/mathematical texts from Al-Andalus. This is a [[Faith vs. Knowledge]] / [[Theory of Knowledge]] artifact-hunt (religion weaponized to stall inquiry), echoing his determinist, religion-skeptic streak documented in [[Political and Economic Views]]. Adjacent lookups on the **Sedition Act of 1798** and the full **Spanish Inquisition** overview round out the history scaffolding.

## [[IB Economics (SL)]] — supply-side
The economics core: he asks for **rebuttals to common arguments against supply-side economics** (Laffer Curve, Kennedy/Reagan growth, the Kansas failure framed as "unbalanced implementation"), then for **Reagan-era GDP growth** (the 1982 recession through the 1984 7.2% boom), sources for that data (BEA/FRED, ERTA 1981), and finally **five cons of supply-side** (inequality, deficits, weak trickle-down, neglected demand, underfunded public services). The pattern — arguments *for*, then evidence, then a balanced list of cons — looks like prep for a debate or essay where he wanted both sides. His instinct to lead with rebuttals fits the market-friendly lean noted in [[Political and Economic Views]].

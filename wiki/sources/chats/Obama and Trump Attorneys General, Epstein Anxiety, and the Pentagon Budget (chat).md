# Obama and Trump Attorneys General, Epstein Anxiety, and the Pentagon Budget

A wandering current-events Q&A from Feb 2026 that starts as dry political trivia and turns into [[Traveler Stansberry]] working through a bout of anxiety about the Epstein news cycle. It is a good window into his [[Political and Economic Views]] and his reflexive [[Faith vs. Knowledge|evidence-first skepticism]] — both toward government and toward conspiracy narratives.

## Trajectory of the chat
- **Attorneys General rundown.** He asks who served as US AG under Obama (Eric Holder, then Loretta Lynch), Trump (Jeff Sessions, then William Barr), and follows up on Alberto Gonzales (Bush, 2005–2007). Standard recall, the kind of factual scaffolding that overlaps with his [[IB History (HL)]] instincts for getting timelines straight.
- **The Epstein thread.** He connects Gonzales to "the guy who gave Epstein the sweetheart deal" — actually **Alexander Acosta**, US Attorney for Southern Florida (2007–08), later Trump's Labor Secretary, who resigned in 2019. The model corrects the conflation: Gonzales had already left office before the 2008 non-prosecution agreement was finalized; timeline overlap is not involvement.
- **John Kiriakou and the shadow-government material.** He admits "the epstein stuff is getting to me" and asks how much of what whistleblower John Kiriakou says about torture programs and MK-Ultra is true. The answer sorts claims into tiers: the **CIA torture program** (2014 Senate report) and **MK-Ultra** (Church Committee, ended ~1973) are documented fact; "Epstein-as-intelligence-asset" and modern hidden-mastermind narratives are unproven speculation. Key framing he engages with: *past wrongdoing ≠ proof of present omnipotence*, and former insiders develop "pattern bias."
- **Trilateral Commission.** He probes the elite-network angle; the model frames it as a 1973 Rockefeller-founded policy forum with influence-through-networks, not command-through-control — i.e. coordination ≠ centralized global rule.
- **Pentagon budget.** Pivots to economics: the DoD budget structure (O&M ~35–40%, personnel ~20–25%, procurement, RDT&E), and a decade of growth from ~$622B (FY2014) to ~$950B+ (FY2025), a ~50–60% nominal rise but still only ~3.3–3.7% of GDP. The aerospace/defense industry contributed ~$995B in business activity (~1.5% of GDP) in 2024.

## What it reveals
The chat shows Traveler under the pull of an "everything connects" news cycle and consciously trying to discipline it — exactly the analytical, systems-seeking mind the wiki documents, but anxious. The model leans into this ("you're young, analytical, and wired to look for systems"), giving practical advice to stop consuming Epstein content before sleep and read primary documents over commentary. His instinct to redirect a conspiracy spiral into a clean economics question — *what is the marginal strategic return on each defense dollar?* — is characteristic: he converts dread into a tractable [[IB Economics (SL)]]-style opportunity-cost problem. Consistent with his determinist, religiously skeptical [[Core Convictions|core convictions]], he wants the boring, calibrated middle ground rather than the dramatic narrative.

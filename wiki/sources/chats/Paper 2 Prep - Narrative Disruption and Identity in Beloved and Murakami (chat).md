# Paper 2 Prep - Narrative Disruption and Identity in Beloved and Murakami

A long, high-stakes [[Individual Oral (IO)|Paper 2]] prep session, run the morning of the exam, in which [[Traveler Stansberry]] built an entire comparative essay on the prompt: *how have writers created narrative disruption and to what effect?* His paired texts were [[Beloved]] (Toni Morrison) and [[The Elephant Vanishes]] (Haruki Murakami) — both on his [[Reading List]] and central to the [[English Curriculum 2022-2026]]. This is the most developed [[coursework-english|English]] planning chat in the batch and ties directly to his [[Paper 2 Study Guide]].

The argument he and the model hammered out: both authors use non-linear chronology and the figuring of internal distress as a "curse" to show that identity is unstable and reshaped by forces beyond conscious control. He worked the two halves of the [[Identity and Its Collapse]] theme — chronology disruption (Beloved's fractured timeline; the elephant that "vanished into thin air") and "curses" (Beloved-the-character as embodied trauma; the unexplained hunger in *The Second Bakery Attack*). Key quotes he locked in: **"Nothing ever dies,"** **"I am Beloved, and she is mine,"** and Murakami's **"the volcano was gone."**

What the chat reveals is genuine literary nuance, not quote-dumping. Traveler pushed back on the model's first read — insisting the bakery "curse" brings *peace* — and they refined it to a **false resolution**: relief achieved through absurd compliance, not understanding, undercut by the line **"there's always yearning."** He correctly intuited that "the past isn't dead, it's not even past" was Faulkner (*Requiem for a Nun*), not Morrison, and traced his own paraphrase back to "Nothing ever dies." He also sharpened his diction self-consciously, rejecting "reconciliation" as too optimistic for Morrison in favor of "partial reclamation," reflecting his determinist, anti-tidy-moral instinct — Morrison "denies closure," Murakami offers only suppression. This connects [[Memory and Trauma]], [[Art as Survival]], and [[Fate and Free Will]] (forces beyond the self).

The session then sprawled into adjacent angles for the same texts: the "journey" motif in *Tuesday's Women* vs *The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle* (passive emptiness vs active descent into the well), and significant "insignificant" objects — Paul D's tobacco-tin heart, Sethe's chokecherry-tree scars, the house number "124," the wind-up bird — as vessels that either contain identity (Morrison) or destabilize it (Murakami). It closed with an off-topic detour into computing limits on a [[IB Math (SL)|TI-84]] and finding the terminal velocity of v(t)=8.14t/√(t²+0.3) → 8.14, bleeding into his [[IB Physics (HL)]] mindset.

The model's tone throughout is sardonic and needling ("don't revert to summary mode like it's your comfort blanket"), which Traveler clearly uses as a sparring partner rather than an oracle — repeatedly correcting and refining its claims.

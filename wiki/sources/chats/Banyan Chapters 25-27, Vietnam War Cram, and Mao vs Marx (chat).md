# Banyan Chapters 25-27, Vietnam War Cram, and Mao vs Marx

A combined [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English]] and [[IB History (HL)]] working session built around Vaddey Ratner's *In the Shadow of the Banyan*. Traveler pastes in detailed chapter summaries for **chapters 25–27** and asks the model to condense them into three sharp notes per chapter. The material tracks Raami's arc under the Khmer Rouge: the deceptively humane village under an idealistic district leader, Big Uncle's torture-as-"reeducation" and the murder of his family, the rise of the brutal Mouk, forced mass weddings, surveillance and categorization of citizens by "usefulness," the death of Grandmother Queen, and the slide toward labor camps described as "digging their own graves." The notes foreground the novel's themes — political corruption and control, dehumanization, trauma/guilt, and the power and limits of storytelling — themes that map onto his recurring [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]], [[Individual vs. Society]], and [[Memory and Trauma]] threads.

The session then pivots straight into **Vietnam War** exam prep ("test in 20 minutes but I know basically all of this already (did APUSH two years ago)"). He shares his own outline — French Indochina, Diem, Gulf of Tonkin, Tet, My Lai, Kent State, Vietnamization, Paris Peace Accords, Fall of Saigon — and the model fills the gaps (Dien Bien Phu, Geneva Accords, Rolling Thunder, Operation Phoenix, the domestic credibility gap) and supplies a one-line thesis. Finally he asks for **Maoism vs Marxism vs communism** and Mao's major events (Civil War, Land Reform, Hundred Flowers, Great Leap Forward, Cultural Revolution, Sino-Soviet Split, the Nixon opening) — the same Cold War / Asian-communism content that lets him connect the Banyan genocide to its ideological roots.

The chat is a clean example of Traveler's cross-subject efficiency: he treats a novel and a history exam as one continuous body of knowledge, using the literature's depiction of the Cambodian genocide as a concrete case study for the abstract ideology he's revising. It pairs naturally with his other Banyan sessions ([[In the Shadow of the Banyan Chapters 22-24 and Vietnam Counterinsurgency (chat)|In the Shadow of the Banyan Chapters 22-24 and Vietnam Counterinsurgency]]).

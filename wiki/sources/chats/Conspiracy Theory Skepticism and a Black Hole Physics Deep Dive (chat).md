# Conspiracy Theory Skepticism and a Black Hole Physics Deep Dive

A long, unstructured late-night curiosity session (2025-11-15, GPT-5.1) that splits cleanly into two halves: first a tour through the "Conspiracy Hall of Fame," then a sprawling tangent into black-hole physics. It's a strong window into how [[Traveler Stansberry]] reasons under uncertainty — his evidentiary [[Faith vs. Knowledge|skepticism]], his determinist's instinct that the world runs on incompetence rather than design, and his genuine [[Intellectual Interests|intellectual curiosity]] about foundational physics (relevant to [[IB Physics (HL)]] and the [[UVA and the Quant Question|quant/math]] track).

## Half one: conspiracy theories rated by evidence
He asks the model to rate the "legitimacy" of various conspiracy theories — 9/11 (controlled demolition, "jet fuel can't melt steel," WTC 7, the Pentagon, LIHOP, full inside job), AIPAC's influence, the AZC–JFK assassination link, the broader JFK theories (Oswald-alone, CIA, Mafia, grassy knoll, Cuba, LBJ, deep state), and whether Epstein killed himself. The model's verdict in every case: physics plus institutional incompetence beats supervillain explanations, and "suspicion is not evidence."

Two of his own remarks are the most revealing:
- On AIPAC and antisemitism: *"I don't want anyone to control anything, idc what religion or ethnicity or race they are."* He explicitly separates criticizing a **lobby** from scapegoating a **people** — power concentration is the problem, not identity. This is a clean expression of his [[Political and Economic Views|individualist, anti-concentration politics]] and connects to [[Individual vs. Society]].
- His synthesis: *"almost all conspiracy theories seem to be based on circumstantial or anecdotal evidence."* The model agrees — the real explanation for big events is usually incompetence, miscommunication, and bureaucratic sloppiness, not "4D chess masterminds." This is consistent with his [[Core Convictions|determinism]] and demand for physical, falsifiable evidence over pattern-matching narratives.

## Half two: black holes and the limits of physics
The pivot ("laws of thermodynamics") opens a genuine physics deep dive: the four laws of thermodynamics, whether black holes violate conservation of energy (they don't — mass-energy is conserved; the open question is **information**, not energy), the **black-hole information paradox**, Hawking radiation, general relativity predicting black holes via the Schwarzschild solution, **singularities** as the point where GR's math "explodes," the Einstein Field Equations and tensors, charged (Reissner–Nordström) vs. spinning (Kerr) black holes, **ER=EPR** (Maldacena/Susskind: entanglement and wormholes as the same thing; "the universe is made of relationships, not stuff"), quantum gravity candidates, the EHT photographs of M87* and Sgr A*, and the coldest things in the universe (Boomerang Nebula ~1 K; MIT's 38-picokelvin record; Bose–Einstein condensates).

His best moment is a methodological one: *"could it be possible that we keep trying to find solutions to confirm with our laws of physics when we would be better off rethinking them."* The model frames this as exactly the front line of theoretical physics — Newton → relativity → quantum mechanics, each revolution coming when the old laws hit a wall. He also independently maps absolute zero onto a mathematical **limit** (the model confirms with $\lim_{n\to\infty}(1/2)^n = 0$), tying the physics back to his [[IB Math (SL)|math]] intuition, and notes singularities and the information paradox as "clues" that current physics is incomplete rather than contradictions to be patched.

## Why it matters
The two halves rhyme. In both, his governing instinct is the same: distrust tidy stories, demand mechanism and evidence, and treat a "law" (whether the Warren Commission narrative or general relativity) as a best current model rather than sacred truth. The conspiracy half shows his epistemic discipline applied to politics and history; the physics half shows the same disposition applied to nature, plus real fluency with concepts well beyond the [[IB Physics (HL)]] syllabus. Together they sketch a determinist who finds the universe more interesting *because* nobody is secretly in charge.

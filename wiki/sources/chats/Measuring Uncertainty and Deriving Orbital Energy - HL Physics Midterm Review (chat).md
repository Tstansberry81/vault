# Measuring Uncertainty and Deriving Orbital Energy - HL Physics Midterm Review

A focused [[IB Physics (HL)]] midterm-review session in which Traveler drilled two clusters of exam content: **measurement uncertainty** and **gravitation / orbital mechanics**. He opens by explicitly invoking his custom-ChatGPT "Option 2 / math mode" (see [[Building a Custom ChatGPT Options Mode System and Mode 5 Essay Editor (chat)|Building a Custom ChatGPT Options Mode System and Mode 5 Essay Editor]]), confirming this is part of his self-built tutoring tool, then works through ~10 questions for his HL2 physics midterm.

## Uncertainty rules he reviewed
- **Analog instruments**: absolute uncertainty = half the smallest division (mm ruler → ±0.5 mm).
- **Digital instruments**: the full smallest digit shown (0.01 g scale → ±0.01 g).
- **Repeated measurements**: uncertainty = half the range (max − min)/2 — the IB shortcut that ignores real statistics.
- **Percent uncertainty** = (Δx / x) × 100.
- **Propagation**: add/subtract → absolute uncertainties add; multiply/divide → percent uncertainties add; powers → multiply percent by the exponent.
- **Sig-fig matching**: the measured value's decimal places must match the uncertainty's.

Worked problems: gravitational field strength g = F/m with 3% force and 9% mass uncertainty → **12%**; a circle r = 10 ± 0.5 cm → area **314 ± 31 cm²** (5% on r doubles to 10% on area); and the unit-analysis riddle that **energy per unit volume (J m⁻³) shares units with pressure** (N m⁻²).

## Gravitation derivations (no calculus)
He asked for the standard IB derivations that lean only on the data-booklet equations:
- **Orbital speed** by setting gravity = centripetal force: GMm/r² = mv²/r → **v = √(GM/r)**, and the near-surface form v = √(gr).
- The same result via **kinetic energy**, using U = −GMm/r and K = GMm/2r.
- A full **derivation of total orbital energy E = −GMm/2r** from K + U.
- A conceptual question: raising orbital radius requires **positive work**, because E becomes less negative even though speed drops (U rises faster than K falls).
- Deriving **g = GM/R²** from F = GMm/R² and g = F/m, framed around Jupiter (g ≈ 25 N kg⁻¹, R = 7.1×10⁷ m).

# Cold War Origins, Exponential Equations, and Movie Curiosity (chat)

A sprawling, single-session grab-bag (titled "Halide Gas Overview" by ChatGPT) that ricochets across [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s simultaneous interests: a Christopher Nolan film, idle science/trivia questions, [[IB Math (SL)]] exponential equations, [[IB Physics (HL)]] mechanics, and—most substantively—a structured walk through the **origins-of-the-Cold-War document set** for [[IB History (HL)]]. It is mostly low-stakes, but the back half is recognizably exam-prep.

## Coursework content
- **[[IB Math (SL)]] — exponential equations.** A clean run of solve-and-show-steps problems, all via the substitution \(y = e^x\): \(e^{2x}=2e^x\) (→ \(x=\ln 2\)), \(e^x=e^{-x}\) (→ \(x=0\)), \(1+12e^{-x}=e^x\) (→ \(x=\ln 4\)), and \(e^x+e^{-x}=k\) for \(k=2,3\) (the \(\cosh\)-style quadratics). Standard SL functions material.
- **[[IB Physics (HL)]] — mechanics.** An Atwood-style cart-and-hanging-mass acceleration (\(a = m_2 g/(m_1+m_2) \approx 0.38\,\text{m/s}^2\)), a frictionless 15° incline (\(a=g\sin\theta \approx 2.54\,\text{m/s}^2\)), and a kinetic-energy refresher (\(KE=\tfrac12 mv^2\)).

## [[IB History (HL)]] — Cold War origins document set
The most deliberate stretch: he asked, in chronological order, for analyses of the canonical early-Cold-War sources, the same set HL students dissect for Paper 1/origins:
- **Kennan's 1946 Long Telegram** — Soviet ideology as inherently hostile, expansionist but risk-averse, internally fragile; basis for **containment**, the "X-Article," Truman Doctrine, Marshall Plan, NATO.
- **Stalin's Feb 1946 election speech** (Bolshoi) — capitalism as the root of war, inevitability of capitalist–socialist conflict, validation of the Soviet system.
- **Churchill's Iron Curtain / "Sinews of Peace" speech** (Fulton, March 1946) — the "Stettin to Trieste" metaphor, call for Anglo-American unity, framing the struggle as moral and ideological.
- **Stalin's March 1946 Pravda interview** — rebutting Churchill as a warmonger, comparing his rhetoric to Hitler's racial theories, defending Soviet influence in Eastern Europe as security.
- **Truman Doctrine (1947)** and a request for **Marshall Plan** analysis (GPT skipped it when he immediately pivoted).
- **Civil War Amendments** (13th/14th/15th) — abolition, citizenship and equal protection, voting rights—likely a separate US-history thread, connecting loosely to his interest in slavery's legacy ([[The Moral Impact of Slavery]]).

# It's Not in My Control

Personal essay (original filename "HOME 11-12," [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]], May 2024), written alongside the [[Home (novel)|Home]] unit.

## Summary
The corpus's most emotionally candid document. Traveler reflects on his **parents' divorce** (6th grade) and admits his coping mechanism was **avoidance** — "it's not in my control" — because he couldn't process both the divorce and the pandemic at once while watching his little brother's "agony." He confronts the cost: difficulty processing emotion, blankness toward his father's new wife and her family at the 2024 wedding, and a sense of lost empathy — "I used to have a lot of empathy… and now I don't, at least not in the same way."

## Why it matters
The thematic bridge between [[Home (novel)|Morrison's Home]] (trauma, family, return) and his own life — a real instance of literature and self-understanding merging. Feeds [[Traveler Stansberry]] and [[Memory and Trauma]].

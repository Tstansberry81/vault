# Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)

HL History essay (Mr. Shipley) on civil disobedience as a force against injustice — one of his most ambitious and opinionated pieces.

## Summary
Builds from **Thoreau** ("that government is best which governs the least"; "be a counter-friction to stop the machine") through three historical case studies — **Gandhi** (South Africa pass laws, the Salt March), the **Montgomery Bus Boycott**, and **Selma/MLK** (Bloody Sunday → Voting Rights Act) — then makes two **bold present-day arguments**: that the transgender community and anti-book-ban activists should use civil disobedience, amplified by social media. Concludes it fails under authoritarian regimes (North Korea, China) where media is suppressed.

## Why it matters
A full-circle return to the [[Civil Disobedience|Thoreau]] he met in 10th-grade transcendentalism, and the clearest window into his **political values** (anti-authoritarian, pro-civil-rights). Ties his [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice]], [[Individual vs. Society]], and [[Conformity and Tradition]] themes to real-world action.

# The Logistic Map

A 10th-grade math exploration (Ms. Touma) on the logistic map and chaos theory.

## Summary
Explains the logistic map xₙ₊₁ = r·xₙ(1−xₙ) as a population model, then follows it into **chaos**: how only the growth rate r (not the seed) determines behavior, the cascade of period-doubling bifurcations, the onset of chaos at r ≈ 3.56995, "islands of stability," and the **Feigenbaum constant** (4.669). His takeaway — *"simplicity can create complexity… a layer of complexity hides under simple equations"* — and his interest in how random-number generators derive from it.

## Why it matters
Genuine conceptual mathematical curiosity beyond his [[IB Math (SL)|SL track]], and a deep thematic rhyme with his [[Fate and Free Will|determinism]] obsession: a fully deterministic equation producing apparent randomness.

# Halloween (personal essay)

One of his most vulnerable pieces. Handled here factually and with care, in his own words.

## Summary
Opens on his love of TV about "morally, ethically, and psychologically compromised characters" (*Breaking Bad*, *Dexter*, *Hannibal*) — he roots for "bad people try to do good things," and realizes it mirrors how he feels about himself and his father. Describes **emotional numbness** ("I cannot force myself to cry… everything feels numb, like I am going through the motions"), feeling distant from humanity and his identity built on being "really smart" because "if I do not, I have no personality whatsoever."

The anchoring event: on Halloween his **father drove him and 8 friends home obscenely drunk, ~80 mph on backroads at 12:30am** — a night he names as having "threatened our lives." He calls his dad a **narcissist** with "no empathy," and closes on the most troubling line for him: "what is more troubling is that I see some of him in myself."

## Why it matters
The clearest window into his inner emotional world — numbness, identity-through-intellect, and the fear of inheriting his father's traits. See the synthesis at [[Emotional Life and Inner World]].

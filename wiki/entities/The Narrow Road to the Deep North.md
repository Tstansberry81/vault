# The Narrow Road to the Deep North

Novel by **Richard Flanagan** (POWs on the Burma "Death Railway"). Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 2]] (2025). The single richest text in the corpus for [[Fate and Free Will]] and [[Identity and Its Collapse]].

## Traveler's readings
- **Identity: free will vs. fate** ([[IB HL English 2 Essay]], 1205 words). Dorrigo composes identity by *role adaptation* ("the Big Fella") — an act of free will — and dies "a free man" at peace. Nakamura builds identity on the rigid, fatalistic "Japanese spirit," realizes too late it was "a mask for the most terrible evil," and collapses (beats his family before death). Thesis: **identity built on belief in free will is "more advantageous for survival" than identity built on fate.**
- **Circle vs. line imagery** ([[Narrow Road to the Deep North Response]]): the circle = the delusion of free will (you move freely but the path is fixed); the line = fate acknowledged, linear, depressing. Cut a circle and it becomes a line — "the same, only perceived differently." Conclusion: "Living with the belief of free will is certainly better than believing there is none at all."
- **Conformity** ([[Exit Ticket 299-332]]): Choi, "a man of no colony," loses his identity by conforming to the Japanese spirit — kills to feel control. ([[Conformity and Tradition]])

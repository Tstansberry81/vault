# Antigone

Play by **Sophocles**. A core text of Traveler's IB **[[Individual Oral (IO)]]** (HL English 1), paired variously with [[The Lion in Winter]] and [[Absalom, Absalom!]].

## Traveler's readings
- **Power destroys its seeker** ([[Global Issue Activity]]): Creon's insistence on control over Antigone, Haemon, and Eurydice "leads himself to be ruined" — Haemon and Eurydice's suicides, Creon left with nothing. Justice served to the tyrant by himself.
- **Patriarchy as hierarchy** ([[IO Outline and Extracts]], [[Outline IB Form]]): Creon as tyrant who must punish and isolate a capable female challenger (Antigone in the cave) to preserve male rule — parallel to Henry/Eleanor in [[The Lion in Winter]].

Key extract he used: the Creon–Antigone confrontation ("As long as I live, no woman rules here").

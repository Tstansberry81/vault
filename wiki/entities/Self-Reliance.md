# Self-Reliance

Essay by **Ralph Waldo Emerson**; the core text of the [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]] transcendentalism unit (Mr. Bryant, Feb 2024), paired with Thoreau's [[Civil Disobedience]] and "Walking."

## Traveler's reading
[[Self-Reliance (essay)|His response]] ("I do not wish to expiate, but to live") makes this the most **personal** text in his grade-10 work: he connects Emerson's anti-conformity directly to his own non-religion at an Episcopal school, rejecting "original sin" and chapel-going-by-default. Crucially nuanced — religion is useful for those without "strong foundations based on education and moral standards," but "mindlessly being religious because everyone else is" is the real error. See [[Faith vs. Knowledge]].

[[Friday 3-1-24|Annotated quotes]] capture the transcendentalist tenets he extracted: distrust of institutions, primacy of nature, and the call to *act*.

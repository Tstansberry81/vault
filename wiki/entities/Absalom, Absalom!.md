# Absalom, Absalom!

Novel by **William Faulkner**. A work-in-translation/IB text Traveler paired with [[Antigone]] in an early [[Individual Oral (IO)]] brainstorm ([[Global Issue Activity]]) before settling on [[The Lion in Winter]].

## Traveler's reading
Reads **Thomas Sutpen** as a mirror of Creon: his "insistence on power and control leads himself to be ruined by the things he created." He traces the chain of self-destruction — abandoning Eulalia and Charles Bon, forbidding Bon's marriage to Judith, the Henry/Bon killing over Bon's race, the failed heir with Milly Jones, and Sutpen's murder by Wash Jones. Ties the 1800s South (slavery, race) to the cyclic idea that "quests for power only lead to demise."

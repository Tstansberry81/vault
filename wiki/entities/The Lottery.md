# The Lottery

Short story by **Shirley Jackson** (1948; a village's annual ritual stoning). Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 9]] (Mr. Bryant) — the anchor of his [[Conformity and Tradition]] theme.

## Traveler's readings ([[The Lottery questions]])
- **Irony of setting**: the beautiful summer morning lulls the reader; the black box = old traditions; the names (Summers/Graves/Delacroix) as charactonyms sending "mixed signals."
- **Satire of tradition**: reads it as critiquing "the reluctance of people to reject outdated traditions," and — distinctively — links it to **the WWI/WWII draft**: one person calls names while everyone waits in dread.
- **Coercion** ([[Mid Term Exam Essay]]): even children join the stoning — "good people can do terrible things if they have to"; ties to Nazi Germany/communism and Orwell's "all animals are equal, but some are more equal than others."

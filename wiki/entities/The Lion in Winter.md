# The Lion in Winter

Play by **James Goldman** (Henry II and Eleanor of Aquitaine). The literary work in Traveler's final IB **[[Individual Oral (IO)]]** pairing (with [[Antigone]]).

## Traveler's reading
[[Outline IB Form|Final IO thesis]]: "Male characters in The Lion in Winter and Antigone use patriarchy as a tool to uphold a hierarchy of power," isolating their female opponents (Eleanor, Antigone) for rebelling. Henry locks Eleanor in a castle as "punishment for rebellion," treating her "like a child who misbehaved rather than an enemy because she is a woman" — yet the extract shows Eleanor is a genuine threat ("And I damn near won the last one").

Key extract: the Henry–Eleanor exchange, p.18 ("I'll never let you loose. You led too many civil wars against me").

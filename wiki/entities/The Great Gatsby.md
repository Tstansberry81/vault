# The Great Gatsby

Novel by **F. Scott Fitzgerald**. Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]] (Mr. Bryant, Oct 2023).

## Traveler's readings
- **[[Gatsby Essay|"The Flaws of Man"]]**: Tom Buchanan and George Wilson both embody early-20th-century male superiority — Tom through wealth/power/racism (*The Rise of the Colored Empires*), Wilson through emotional control of Myrtle. Nick (reserves judgment) and Gatsby ("worth the whole damn bunch") are the exceptions, "good by nurture." Thesis: "wealth, power, and emotion corrupts all."
- **[[Color Analysis]]**: gray as dullness/death/gloom — the valley of ashes, Jordan's eyes, Wilson's mourning; gray foreshadows death.
- **[[3 Gatsby Questions]]**: the valley of ashes as the elite's contempt for labor; the dog-purchase as Tom's casual cruelty/superiority.

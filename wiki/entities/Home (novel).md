# Home (novel)

Novel by **Toni Morrison** (Frank Money, a Korean War veteran, returning to Lotus, Georgia). The final [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]] text and exam focus (Mr. Bryant, May 2024). Traveler's first Morrison novel — he'd read [[Beloved]] two years later.

## Traveler's readings
- **[[Home FINAL EXAM|"Home" as wholeness]]** (exam essay): home is "more than a place; rather, a feeling of familiarity, comfort, peace, and calm." Frank's journey is spiritual rebirth (the Lotus flower = rebirth/cleansing) from PTSD and alcoholism; Cee's is physical healing from a eugenicist doctor's abuse, ending in self-reliance.
- **[[HOME 15-16 Question|Masculinity]]**: Frank's "savior complex" and the secret that *he* shot the Korean girl tie his manhood to trauma; Morrison shows the Black male experience (over-policing) has "its own challenges."
- **Narrative form** ([[Traveler Stansberry (essay)|the "Traveler Stansberry" doc]]): Frank's first-person corrections vs. the third-person narrator — Frank refuses to be sugar-coated into a hero, keeping the story "true and factual."

# Romeo and Juliet

Play by **William Shakespeare**. Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 9]] (Mr. Bryant) — his first Shakespeare in the corpus.

## Traveler's readings
- **Act II close reading** ([[Act II Scenes 3-6t (1)]]): Friar Laurence's plants-as-humans (poison/medicine), the AABB rhyme scheme, the Friar's foreshadowing ("love slow"), and why Shakespeare uses iambic pentameter for love ("a form of art… for passionate monologues").
- **Act III "what if"** ([[Romeo and Juliet Paragraph Writing Prompts Act III]]): argues the Capulets would have rejected Romeo's apology — Lord Capulet's rush to marry Juliet to Paris and his contempt for Romeo ("wretched puling fool") as evidence.

## Themes
fate & foreshadowing · family & social expectation · [[Power, Hierarchy, and Justice|patriarchal control]] (Lord Capulet)

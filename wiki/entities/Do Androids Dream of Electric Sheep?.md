# Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?

Novel by **Philip K. Dick** (the basis for *Blade Runner*). Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 9]] (Mr. Bryant, early 2023) — the source of his manifesto-like [[Faith vs. Knowledge]] essay.

## Traveler's readings
- **[[DADOES ESSAY|"How Technology Set Man Free from Religion"]]** (his most ambitious gr.9 essay, fully sourced): Mercerism is a fraud (Mercer = "a bit player named Al Jerry"), structurally identical to Christianity. Argues **technology always threatens religion** because faith ≠ fact; runs the printing-press → Reformation parallel; cites Marx. Androids = a metaphor for truth-revealing technology.
- **John Isidore** ([[FINAL dick quest]]): a passive, "dog-like" figure of unconditional affection; his "special" status adds belittlement. Androids "have no self-preservation instinct" — so "they will never be human."
- **The Scream / Puberty paintings** ([[Scream and Puberty Analyzations]]): Munch's paintings mapped onto Rick Deckard's unsettling, growing empathy/attraction to androids.

# IB Physics (HL)

[[Traveler Stansberry]]'s HL Physics (Mr. Stine; final grade **A**). The most quantitatively demanding subject in his diploma, and a key data point for the [[UVA and the Quant Question|quant question]].

## Flagship: the [[Physics IA]]
A genuine experimental investigation — *"How does changing the angle of a ramp affect the friction on a cart?"* Using Vernier motion sensors and Logger Pro, he built a free-body model, derived the friction equation, and ran 16 trials. What stands out:
- **Real uncertainty propagation** (angle ±0.5°, mass ±0.0001 kg) carried through to the force calculations.
- **Intellectual honesty:** his data came out *backwards* (friction appearing to rise with angle, even negative values at 6°/9°), and rather than fudge it he diagnoses why — high uncertainty, small-angle regime where friction ∝ cos θ barely changes — and concludes his own experiment failed to corroborate the physics. That candor is the most quant-like thing in the corpus.

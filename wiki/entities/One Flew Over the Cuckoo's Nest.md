# One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest

Novel by **Ken Kesey**. Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]] (Mr. Bryant, early 2024). The defining text for his [[Individual vs. Society]] theme.

## Traveler's readings
- **[[Nest Essay|"Individual Versus Society"]]** (his most ambitious gr.10 essay, with sources): McMurphy vs. Nurse Ratched = individual vs. "the Combine." Non-conformity (Ellis, Ruckly, McMurphy) is met with escalating punishment (Shock Shop, lobotomy). Thesis: rebels "lose due to them being rendered as dangerous or different." Extends to **American slavery** as a real-world parallel (Nat Turner, Stono rebellions fail; slavery ends only via "external support" the ward lacks).
- **[[Cuckoo's Final Writing]]**: McMurphy a hero with "good intentions," but he *fails* — loses his peers' trust, is lobotomized; the Nurse "wins"; the individual cannot beat society.
- **[[Writing Assignment Nest 225-241]]**: McMurphy's recruitment of George is "manipulative" beneath compassion — needs the tenth crew member.

# Death of a Salesman

Play by **Arthur Miller**. A [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 2]] text Traveler preselected for [[Paper 2 Study Guide|Paper 2]] (appears in his study-guide table, not yet in a full essay in this corpus).

## Paper 2 notes (his own)
- **Themes:** identity attached to external validation; collapse of the American Dream; self-delusion; [[Masculinity]].
- **Devices:** symbolism (delusion/mirage), irony, the "success" motif.
- **Setting:** New York / Brooklyn, 1940s, post-Depression.
- **Quotes flagged:** "I am not a dime a dozen! I am Willy Loman!"; "The man who makes an appearance in the business world… gets ahead"; "We never told the truth for ten minutes in this house" (Biff).
- **Pairings:** with [[Beloved]] (masculinity during identity collapse) and [[Garden of Eden (poem)|Garden of Eden]] (NYC identity attached to materialism).

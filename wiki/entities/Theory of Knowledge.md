# Theory of Knowledge

[[Traveler Stansberry]]'s IB ToK (Mr. Lewis; final grade **A**) — the formal home of the [[Intellectual Interests|epistemology]] he already does for fun.

## ToK Essay — "[[ToK Essay (Doubt)|To what extent is doubt central to the pursuit of knowledge?]]"
Argues doubt is central but double-edged, across **mathematics** and **history**:
- **Math:** doubt as the engine of new knowledge — Euclid's fifth postulate → non-Euclidean geometry; Descartes' methodical doubt → analytical geometry; Cantor's doubt of "all infinities are equal" → set theory (and the resistance Cantor faced as doubt-as-confirmation-bias).
- **History:** Ranke's source-criticism and Bloch's Annales School using doubt to strip bias from historical knowledge.

A genuinely sophisticated essay — and notice the math examples (Cantor, infinities, geometry) reveal more mathematical curiosity than his [[IB Math (SL)|SL math track]] suggests.

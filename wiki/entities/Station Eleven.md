# Station Eleven

Novel by **Emily St. John Mandel** (post-pandemic "Georgian Flu"). Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]] (Mr. Bryant, spring 2024).

## Traveler's readings
- **[[Station 11 Essay on Art Final|"Survival's Expression"]]**: art as a basic human need that "fills the void." The Traveling Symphony's "because survival is insufficient," the *Station Eleven* comics (linking Kirsten and Tyler across time), and the Museum of Civilization all show art shaping how people "act, think, and behave." Central claim of his [[Art as Survival]] theme.
- **Religious fanaticism** ([[Station 11 42-44]]): Tyler/the Prophet as cult leader; compares his exploitation to pre-Reformation indulgences ([[Faith vs. Knowledge]]).
- **Interconnectedness** ([[Station 11 Short Essay]]): Mandel's web of connections (Arthur, Miranda, the paperweight, the Museum); gratitude for "the little things we take for granted."

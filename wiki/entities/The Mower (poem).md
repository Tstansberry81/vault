# The Mower (poem)

Poem by **Philip Larkin**. The first assignment of [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 2]] (Sept 2025) — a short close-reading exercise ([[250 Words - Mower|original + revision]]).

## Traveler's reading
The poem (a hedgehog accidentally killed by a mower) urges us "to cherish the time we have with others before it's gone." Key line: "we should be careful of each other, we should be kind while there is still time." He reads the speaker's remorse as a model for making amends "while we still are alive."

Notable as a **craft artifact**: the file preserves both his original and a revised draft with proper MLA citations (e.g. "Larkin 2-3") — evidence of his revision process. ([[Art as Survival]])

## Themes
Mortality · kindness · time

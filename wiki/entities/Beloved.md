# Beloved

Novel by **Toni Morrison**. Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 2]] (Mr. Mezeske, 2026) as a centerpiece of the [[Identity and Its Collapse|identity]] unit and a likely [[Paper 2 Study Guide|Paper 2]] text.

## Traveler's readings
- **[[Masculinity]] — Sixo vs. Paul D** ([[Beloved 257-277 Writeup]]): two models of Black masculinity from Sweet Home. Sixo is defiant (dies singing, cries "Seven-O!" for his unborn child); Paul D is "contemplative and reserved" and survives. Argues Morrison endorses Paul D — "the path to freedom… requires patience and passivity, rather than rebellion."
- **Beloved as toxic masculinity** ([[Beloved Discussion Post]]): on p.147 Paul D's manhood "breaks down"; Beloved (symbol of slavery, history, trauma) "dehumanizes him by restricting his identity to that of a male slave," blocking his wish to be expressive with Sethe.

## Paper 2 notes
Quotes he flagged: "I am Beloved, and she is mine"; "This is not a story to pass on"; "124 was spiteful"; "Nothing ever dies." Pairs with [[The Elephant Vanishes|Murakami]] (identity collapse / distortion) and [[Garden of Eden (poem)|Smith]] (slavery, trauma, the past).

# Civil Disobedience

Essay by **Henry David Thoreau**; part of the [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]] transcendentalism unit (with [[Self-Reliance]] and "Walking").

## Traveler's reading
Prompted by Thoreau, [[Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)|his "Justice" essay]] defines justice as restoring balance — "an eye for an eye" — but corrupted by bias (race, ethnicity, politics): "Justice should be fair, but we are not fair beings." Tellingly skeptical of Thoreau's efficacy: Thoreau "complaining about the imperfections of society… didn't change it and likely won't" — a characteristic [[Individual vs. Society|pessimism about the individual's power]] against the system.

[[Friday 3-1-24|Annotated quotes]]: government "never of itself furthered any enterprise"; Thoreau choosing jail over the poll tax as a model of *acting* on belief.

# Maus

Graphic novel by **Art Spiegelman** (the Holocaust, as told by his father Vladek). Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 9]] (Mr. Bryant, early 2023).

## Traveler's readings
- **[[Maus Essay|"Showing, not Telling"]]**: Spiegelman's ethical choices about *what to depict* — showing the graves/burning bodies for historical accuracy, but censoring a child's death out of empathy. Art as accurate yet respectful witness. ([[Art as Survival]], [[Memory and Trauma]])
- **Vladek's character** ([[Chap 2-3 Paragraph MAUS]]): shaped by his own father and the Holocaust into an over-protective father who ensures Artie is fed and safe.
- **Coercion & "every man for himself"** ([[Trials of Solidarity]]): why Janina turns on the Spiegelmans as 1944 penalties harden; the [[Conformity and Tradition|banality of evil]] (Jewish police, Tosha).
- **The comics-vs-atrocity tension** ([[Mauschwitz]]): can something "so serious" be drawn without seeming childish?

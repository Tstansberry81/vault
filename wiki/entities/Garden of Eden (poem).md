# Garden of Eden (poem)

Poem by **Tracy K. Smith** (from *Wade in the Water*). Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 2]] alongside her "Wade in the Water."

## Traveler's reading
[[Explicative of Garden of Eden|His explication]]: the narrator faces "existential dread of aging," and Smith argues that **"consumerism is used as a tool to fill the void in identity created by emotional distress."** Key moves he identifies:
- The grocery store "Garden of Eden" as ironic false paradise (biblical reference); exotic fruit — "pomegranate, persimmon, quince!" — as luxurious imagery of false fulfillment.
- "The same desolate luxury… ashamed of the same things: innocence and privacy" → social media destroys the privacy that preserves meaning.
- **Structural analysis:** enjambment pauses only on the consumerist list (the two exclamation-point lines) — "the only time the narrator feels structure or fullness in her life is when she is engaging in consumerist culture."

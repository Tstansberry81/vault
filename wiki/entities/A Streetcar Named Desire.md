# A Streetcar Named Desire

Play by **Tennessee Williams**. Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 1]].

## Traveler's readings ([[Worksheet A Streetcar Named Desire|worksheet]])
- **[[Masculinity]]**: Williams "associates masculinity and heteronormality with bad, abusive men" (Stanley; Mitch/Allan complicit), and femininity with submissiveness (Stella as the model; Blanche only performs it). "Ahead of its time" on toxic masculinity.
- **Blanche's decline**: begins when Stanley exposes her past; her lies stop working; climaxes in the Shep Huntleigh fantasy and final breakdown.
- **Desire**: "the nature of desire is toxic, and always ends up bad for everyone."
- **Light**: Blanche avoids daylight to preserve her illusion of youth — youth being "the thing she wants back."
- **Symbols**: the poker game (manipulation/deception — "all that matters is what you can convince people of"); Belle Reve ("Sweet Dream") as the lost, idealized past.
- **Moral stance**: Stella "did not do the right thing" institutionalizing Blanche — he takes a firm position, as the prompt demanded.

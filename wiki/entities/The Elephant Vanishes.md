# The Elephant Vanishes

Short story by **Haruki Murakami**. Studied in [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|IB HL English 2]] (2026).

## Traveler's reading
[[Hinge Points|His "hinge points" analysis]] reads the vanishing elephant as **the soul**, and its keeper as the individual:
- **The elephant & keeper** are deliberately humanized ("bigger than it needed to be," "blank and empty"); both are outcasts the town never wanted. The elephant "shrinks" as their "balance" changes — the soul, deprived of care, dwindling to "a lump of death."
- **The town**: distracted by "the western world, the economy, politics, ads," it takes the elephant only out of guilt, chains it, and forgets it. Murakami's point: "a society cannot treat its weakest and most vulnerable members best when… focused on so much extraneous and non-substantial worldly endeavors."
- Thesis: **commercialization and modernity erase the soul** — hence "vanishing."

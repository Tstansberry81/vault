# Honeycomb Portfolio

An original investing **working paper** by [[Traveler Stansberry]] — his attempt to build a portfolio-construction thesis of his own, not just analyze single stocks. The name comes from a **kissing-number / circle-packing** geometric metaphor for diversification: how many low-correlation positions can pack around a core without overlapping.

## The idea
The paper argues for an edge built from a few combinable properties — **Lindy/durability, quality, and non-correlation** — assembled into a densely "packed" but diversified portfolio. He pasted the draft into ChatGPT for critique and got a useful pushback on **survivorship bias** (the back-tested winners weren't a fair sample), which he engaged with rather than dismissed.

## Why it matters
It's the clearest artifact of Traveler thinking like a portfolio constructor, bridging his [[Investment Club|fundamental investing]] and his [[Quantitative Finance|quant]] turn — geometry and correlation math applied to markets. It also shows his habit of using ChatGPT as an adversarial reviewer of his own original work.

> [!note] Both ideas resurfaced in the live models
> The Honeycomb thinking isn't a dead end — it shows up operationalized in his later code:
> - The **non-correlation / "packing"** principle becomes the **correlation cap (≤0.50)** overlay in **[[The Edge (trading model)]]** ("so the basket isn't eight versions of one bet").
> - The **survivorship-bias** critique he absorbed here becomes a design choice in the **[[Personal Quant Model]]** and Edge backtests (including delisted/inactive names; honest point-in-time universe). A clean example of a 2025 critique shaping what he built in 2026.

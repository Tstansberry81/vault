# Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass

Slave narrative by **Frederick Douglass**. The first [[English Curriculum 2022-2026|English 10]] text (Mr. Bryant, Sept 2023) and the anchor of his [[The Moral Impact of Slavery]] theme.

## Traveler's readings
- **[[Psychological and Moral Impact of Slavery|Moral corruption of the enslaver]]**: his strongest insight — Mrs. Auld, forbidden to teach Douglass to read, becomes "even more strict and ruthless than her husband." Slavery makes "good people at heart do unspeakable things."
- **Literacy & control** ([[Quiz 6 Paragraph Questions]]): teaching a slave to read is "unlawful… and unsafe"; city vs. plantation slavery and the performance of "kind" masters.
- **[[Use of Pathos in An American Slave|Rhetoric]]** (co-written w/ Bobby Cunningham): Douglass's pathos — his tears, and slave songs as sorrow not contentment.

## Cross-text role
Douglass is his recurring **moral yardstick**: physical violence of slavery vs. merely attitudinal racism in [[The Great Gatsby|Gatsby]] ([[Midterm Essays|midterm]]); slavery as the real-world analog for fictional oppression in [[One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest|the Nest essay]]. Foreshadows [[Beloved]].

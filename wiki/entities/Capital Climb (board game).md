# Capital Climb (board game)

An original **Monopoly-style board game** designed and produced by [[Traveler Stansberry]] — almost certainly an [[IB Economics (SL)]] project — built to make players *feel* the **poverty trap** rather than just read about it. Working-titled "**Life Capital**" in an earlier session before settling on Capital Climb.

## Design
A production-heavy effort: a 20×20 board, printable card decks, and three interacting currencies — **Money, Education, and Social Credit** — whose feedback loops are tuned so that starting disadvantaged compounds, dramatizing how hard it is to climb out. He used ChatGPT for mechanics design and the print-ready assets.

## Why it matters
A characteristic Traveler move: turning an economics concept into a built artifact (cf. [[Homework Hatch (startup)]], the [[Honeycomb Portfolio]]). It also encodes a genuine piece of his worldview — taking the poverty trap seriously sits in interesting tension with the supply-side lean of his [[Political and Economic Views]].

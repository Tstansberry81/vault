# World Religions (10th)

A 10th-grade comparative-religion course (Mr. Ashford). Important context for [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s [[Faith vs. Knowledge|religious skepticism]] — he studied the world's faiths seriously before settling into his non-belief.

## What his Take-Home Test shows
Nuanced, genuinely engaged comparative analysis:
- **Primal religions** abandoned through warfare (conquistadors forcing Catholicism) and "personal preference"; nature/ancestors central because oral, pre-literate cultures "had nothing else."
- **Buddhism vs. Hinduism:** Buddhism's rejection of god, soul, and caste; "work out your own salvation with diligence" → self-determination over divine guidance (which clearly appeals to him).
- **Sikhism** (Nanak's "no Hindu, no Muslim" — find your own path to God) and **Confucianism** (the five components ordering society).

His attraction to the **self-determining, anti-dogmatic** strands (Buddhist self-salvation, Sikh individual path) prefigures his [[Self-Reliance|transcendentalist]] and [[Faith vs. Knowledge|skeptic]] commitments.

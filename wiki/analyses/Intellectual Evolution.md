# Intellectual Evolution (2022–2026)

How [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s thinking developed across four years — the *when* behind the [[Intellectual Profile]]. The headline: a consistent set of obsessions that started raw and hardened, then **matured from absolutism toward pragmatism**.

## Grade 9 (2022–23) — ambitious and raw
Already reaching for big ideas, if unevenly. The [[DADOES ESSAY]] is a full manifesto on religion vs. technology (Marx, the printing press); [[A Sound of Thunder]] plants the seed of consequence/determinism. Religious skepticism and the individual-vs-system instinct are both present from the start.

## Grade 10 (2023–24) — individualism & social criticism
American lit and **transcendentalism** give him the vocabulary of self-reliance ([[Self-Reliance]]) and civil disobedience ([[Civil Disobedience]]); [[One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest|the Nest essay]] cements his pessimism about the individual vs. society. Religious non-belief becomes explicit and personal.

## Grade 11 (2024–25) — rigor in every direction
IB Year 1 widens him: **History** (authoritarian states, power — [[History IA (Naval Gunfire on D-Day)|the D-Day IA]]), **Economics** (markets, the supply-side [[Economics Debate]]), **ToK** (formal epistemology). Yet his metaphysics is at its *most absolute* here — the [[Iliad Essay]] declares free will "a fallacy."

## Grade 12 (2025–26) — synthesis & maturation
The pieces converge and soften:
- **Free will:** from "fallacy" to *pragmatic existentialism* — the belief in agency as the better bet ([[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]]).
- **Individual vs. society:** from flat pessimism to *conditional* — the rebel can win where media is free ([[Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)]]).
- **Politics:** the [[Political and Economic Views|free-markets-free-people]] worldview crystallizes.
- **The self:** identity-as-adaptation, lived out in the [[College Essay (Surfing)|surfing essay]] and his [[Self-Discipline and Goals|discipline systems]].

## The arc in one sentence
He began as a teenage **absolutist** (fate wins, the individual loses, religion is fraud) and matured into a **pragmatist** who keeps the same convictions but holds them as *livable bets* rather than iron laws — see [[Core Convictions]] and [[Tensions and Open Questions]].

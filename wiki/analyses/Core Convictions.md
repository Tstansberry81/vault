# Core Convictions

[[Traveler Stansberry]]'s actual positions, distilled into propositions — each drawn from his own writing, with the evidence linked. A "credo," compiled rather than guessed. Map: [[Intellectual Profile]].

## On free will & meaning
1. **Free will is largely an illusion — but acting *as if* it's real is the only livable, and the stronger, way to live.** Hard version in the [[Iliad Essay]] ("free will… is a fallacy"); matured version in [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]] ("the belief in free will is more advantageous for survival").
2. **Strength is willpower used to overcome deterministic and selfish tendencies.** His own words (a private note) — and the engine of his [[Self-Discipline and Goals|"75 Hard"]] regimens.

## On knowledge & truth
3. **Doubt is how knowledge advances; certainty is how it stalls.** ([[ToK Essay (Doubt)]].)
4. **Truth comes from evidence and reason, not faith or authority** — and "nobody sees what they don't want to," so guard against self-deception and media spin. ([[Faith vs. Knowledge]], [[Intellectual Interests]].)

## On religion
5. **There is no God; religion is useful fiction.** Openly atheist ([[Self-Reliance (essay)]]); religion equated with a benign fraud in the [[DADOES ESSAY]].
6. **The sin isn't belief — it's *conformity*.** Religion can genuinely help those without other moral foundations; what he rejects is believing "because everyone else does."

## On politics & economics
7. **Free markets build lasting prosperity** — supply-side over stimulus. ([[Political and Economic Views]], [[Economics Debate]].)
8. **Personal liberty is non-negotiable** — pro-civil-rights, anti-censorship, anti-authoritarian. ([[Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)]].)
9. **The individual usually loses to the system — *unless* the system is open enough (free media) to be moved.** His matured [[Individual vs. Society]] verdict.

## On the self
10. **Adapt to what you can't change; don't fight unwinnable battles.** The [[College Essay (Surfing)|surfing]] philosophy, applied to his father and to life.
11. **Identity should stay flexible** — roles you can put on and off, not a rigid creed that collapses with its premise. ([[Identity and Its Collapse]].)

## On ambition
12. **Discipline and persistence beat the social-media fantasy of overnight success** — and unchecked ambition corrupts. ([[The One]], the Lonnie Johnson essay.)

> [!note] These are *his* stated positions, compiled from his work — not prescriptions. See [[Tensions and Open Questions]] for where they pull against each other.

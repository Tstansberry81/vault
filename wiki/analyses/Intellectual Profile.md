The companion pages are [[Core Convictions]] (the distilled beliefs), [[Intellectual Evolution]] (how they developed), and [[Tensions and Open Questions]] (where they conflict).

## The through-lines (what recurs across *every* domain)
These motifs surface in his literature, history, economics, *and* personal life — the real signature of his mind:
1. **Autonomy vs. the system** — the individual against authority: [[Individual vs. Society]] (Cuckoo's Nest, the Lottery), [[Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)|civil disobedience]], [[Political and Economic Views|free markets]], and turning his own [[When I Feel Like an Outsider|social anxiety]] into agency.
2. **Order vs. chaos / determinism vs. unpredictability** — [[Fate and Free Will]] and the [[The Logistic Map|logistic map]] (a deterministic equation producing chaos).
3. **Adaptation vs. resistance** — survive by adapting, not fighting unwinnable battles: [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North|Dorrigo]], the [[College Essay (Surfing)|surfing essay]], dealing with his father.
4. **Appearance vs. reality / self-deception** — [[The Great Gatsby|Gatsby]], media misinformation, "nobody sees what they don't want to."
5. **Ambition and its corruption** — [[The One]], the Lonnie Johnson essay, his own [[Homework Hatch (startup)|startup]] disillusionment.

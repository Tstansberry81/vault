# Tensions and Open Questions

The honest part of the [[Intellectual Profile]]: where [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s convictions pull against each other, or remain unresolved. These are the most interesting places in his thinking — and good fuel for essays, conversations, and self-examination.

## 1. Determinism vs. willpower
He believes free will is largely illusory ([[Fate and Free Will]]), yet stakes his daily life on **willpower** ("strong is using willpower to overcome deterministic tendencies"; [[Self-Discipline and Goals|"75 Hard"]]). His resolution — *act as if you're free because the belief is what works* — is elegant but unproven on its own terms: is chosen self-discipline evidence *against* determinism, or just determinism wearing a motivational mask?

## 2. Pessimism about the individual vs. his own leadership
His literature says the individual loses to the system ([[Individual vs. Society]]). His life says otherwise: President of [[Investment Club]], a [[Senior Rep Campaign Speech (2025)|community-leadership campaign]], Bridges Volunteer of the Year. His [[Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)|HL History essay]] reconciles this *conditionally* (the individual can win where media is free) — but the gap between his fictional fatalism and his real-world optimism is unresolved.

## 3. Free markets vs. a strong service ethic
He argues [[Political and Economic Views|supply-side / let-markets-sort-it]] economics — yet is unusually **service-minded** (Bridges mentor, "named Volunteer of the Year"; a [[Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)|civil-rights]] conscience). How he squares market efficiency with his evident concern for the vulnerable is an open question worth pushing on.

## 4. Materialist vs. mystical
A hard empiricist on religion ([[Faith vs. Knowledge]]) — but privately drawn to the metaphysical edge of physics (David Bohm, the EPR experiment, "physical movements are waves that… unlock the universe" — [[Intellectual Interests]]). Is the mystical streak a contradiction, or just curiosity the atheism hasn't had to confront yet?

## 5. Quant vs. finance
His standing career fork — strong finance instincts, untested higher-math rigor. Tracked in full at [[UVA and the Quant Question]].

## 6. The public vs. private self
The warm, grateful tone toward his father in the [[Senior Speech|public Senior Speech]] vs. the pain in the [[Family and Personal Life|private notes]]. Not a flaw — but a real split between performed and felt, worth being conscious of.

> [!note] These aren't problems to "fix" — they're the live edges of a mind still forming. Revisit as new sources (and college) sharpen them.

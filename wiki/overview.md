# Overview

This wiki is built from **[[Traveler Stansberry]]'s English coursework, grades 9–12 (2022–2026)** — 77 documents spanning four courses ([[English Curriculum 2022-2026|the curriculum]]): essays, responses, quizzes, study guides, memoirs, and one original short story. Sources are mostly Traveler's own writing, so the wiki doubles as a portrait of an evolving thinker.

## The evolving thesis

Across four years and ~25 literary works, a consistent intellectual fingerprint emerges. Traveler returns, again and again, to a small set of questions:

1. **Do we actually choose anything?** — [[Fate and Free Will]] is his signature preoccupation, from the [[The Iliad|Iliad]] ("free will… is a fallacy") to [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]] ("the belief in free will is more advantageous for survival").
2. **What happens to a self under pressure?** — [[Identity and Its Collapse]] dominates his senior year ([[Beloved]], [[Garden of Eden (poem)|Garden of Eden]], [[The Elephant Vanishes]], [[Death of a Salesman]]).
3. **Is religion just useful fiction?** — a persistent skeptic streak links [[Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?]], [[Self-Reliance]], and [[Station Eleven]] under [[Faith vs. Knowledge]].
4. **Can the individual win against the system?** — [[Individual vs. Society]] runs from [[One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest]] through [[The Lottery]] and the transcendentalists; his usual answer is *no*.
5. **What does masculinity cost?** — [[Masculinity]] threads [[A Streetcar Named Desire]], [[Beloved]], and [[Home (novel)|Home]].

The through-line: a deterministic, skeptical, individualist worldview that hardens and grows more philosophically ambitious over four years — and that quietly mirrors his own life (non-religious at an Episcopal school, an outsider who processes the world by writing).

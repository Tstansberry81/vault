# Art as Survival

The idea that art is not decoration but a **basic human need** — the language of emotion, memory, and meaning, especially under crisis.

## Central reading
The [[Station 11 Essay on Art Final|Station Eleven essay]] ("Survival's Expression") is the fullest statement: in a post-pandemic world, art "gives the characters a means of expression to fill the void." The Traveling Symphony's motto "because survival is insufficient," the Museum of Civilization, and the *Station Eleven* comics all show art as what makes survival worth it and links people across time.

## Supporting readings
- **[[Understanding Comics]]** (McCloud): art as a definable, ancient medium; the genius of using comics to explain comics.
- **[[Maus]]**: Art Spiegelman's choices about *what to depict* of the Holocaust — "showing, not telling" — art as ethical witness ([[Memory and Trauma]]).
- **[[The Mower (poem)|The Mower]]** & poetry: compression of feeling into form.

## Personal resonance
This theme connects to [[Traveler Stansberry]] himself: in [[When I Feel Like an Outsider]] he names **writing as his way of expressing what he can't say aloud** — a private version of "survival is insufficient."

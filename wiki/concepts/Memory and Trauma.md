# Memory and Trauma

How the past refuses to stay past — a theme Traveler reads as both psychological wound and narrative structure.

## Key readings
- **[[Beloved]]**: memory as trauma; Beloved as the embodiment of slavery, history, and "trauma" that re-dehumanizes Paul D. Morrison's "rememory."
- **[[Home (novel)|Home]]**: Frank Money's PTSD, hallucinations, and alcoholism; "going home" as a "physical rebirth of his soul," the Lotus as a symbol of rebirth and cleansing. Trauma healed through return and care.
- **[[Maus]]**: the Holocaust transmitted across generations (Vladek → Artie); the ethics of representing atrocity ([[Art as Survival]]).
- **[[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]]**: the POW camp and "the line" as trauma that fixes or frees identity.

## Structural insight
He notices trauma **deforms narrative form**, not just characters — e.g. Flanagan's non-linear "circle" chronology mirrors how the traumatized mind moves through time (see [[Fate and Free Will]]).

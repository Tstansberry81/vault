# Identity and Its Collapse

The organizing theme of [[Traveler Stansberry]]'s senior year (IB HL English 2). Where the [[Fate and Free Will|fate question]] asks whether we choose, the identity question asks what survives the choosing — and what destroys a self.

## The senior-year synthesis
The HL2 [[Paper 2 Study Guide]] explicitly groups texts by **how identity collapses**:
- **By trauma & the past** — [[Beloved]] (identity "shaped by trauma"), Tracy K. Smith's "Wade in the Water."
- **By rigid ideology / fatalism** — [[The Narrow Road to the Deep North]]: Nakamura ties his self to the "Japanese spirit" and collapses with it; Dorrigo keeps a flexible, role-based identity and survives. Also [[Exit Ticket 299-332|Flanagan's Choi]], "a man of no colony," destroyed by conformity.
- **By modernity / contemporary society** — [[The Elephant Vanishes]] (the soul "shrinks" and vanishes under commercialization), [[Garden of Eden (poem)|Garden of Eden]] (consumerism fills the void of a collapsing self).
- **By external validation** — [[Death of a Salesman]] (Willy Loman's identity tied to "success").

## Key claims he makes
- Identity is best held **loosely**: "more flexible identities leave room for growth" ([[Narrow Road to the Deep North]]).
- [[Masculinity]] is a primary axis of identity collapse — Paul D in [[Beloved]] is trapped between "a man whose identity is attached to being a slave" and one who "wants to be expressive."
- Modern/[[Consumerism and Alienation|consumerist]] society actively dissolves identity by destroying "innocence and privacy."

# Consumerism and Alienation

How modern society hollows out the self — a theme concentrated in Traveler's senior-year poetry and prose, and one that bleeds into his own life.

## Key readings
- **[[Garden of Eden (poem)|Garden of Eden]]** (Tracy K. Smith): "consumerism is used as a tool to fill the void in identity created by emotional distress" (here, aging). Exotic groceries as false paradise; social media destroying "innocence and privacy." Enjambment marks the only moments of "fullness" — when shopping.
- **[[The Elephant Vanishes]]** (Murakami): "commercialization and modernity" force the elephant and keeper — the soul and its beholder — to *vanish*; society "cannot treat its weakest and most vulnerable members" well when distracted by "extraneous worldly endeavors."
- **[[Death of a Salesman]]**: identity attached to "success" and external validation; the American Dream as mirage.

## Personal echo
His own short story **[[The One]]** is an indictment of startup/AI hustle culture: ambition warped by social-media success stories into something destructive. The abstract is unusually candid — "I myself have participated in this endeavor." This is the clearest place where a literary theme and [[Traveler Stansberry|his own experience]] merge.

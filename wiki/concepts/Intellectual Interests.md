# Intellectual Interests

[[Traveler Stansberry]]'s private philosophical musings — short notes-to-self that show the same mind as his essays, thinking on its own time. They cluster around four questions:

## 1. Free will vs. determinism
His most striking private note defines his ethics directly: *"strong is using willpower to overcome deterministic or selfish tendencies."* This is the **personal counterpart** to his literary evolution on [[Fate and Free Will]] — and to the systems he builds for himself in [[Self-Discipline and Goals]]. He has, in effect, resolved the Iliad-vs-Narrow-Road tension in his own life: determinism is real, but willpower is the act of resisting it.

## 2. Morality
A standing preoccupation: a note simply reading *"something to do with figuring out morality"* ([[new idea]]); interest in **secular humanism, Kantian rationalism, virtue ethics** ([[secular humanism]]); and the sharp observation that his father "believes in objective morality, yet [is] an atheist" ([[Family and Personal Life]]).

## 3. Religion & self-deception
Continues his [[Faith vs. Knowledge]] skepticism privately: *"if you don't repent, then you go to hell — isn't that a rule… isn't repenting forced?"* And an epistemic note that doubles as a thesis for half his essays: *"nobody sees what they don't want to."*

## 4. Physics & metaphysics
A genuine curiosity about the foundations of reality: the **EPR thought experiment**, **David Bohm**'s books (implicate order / quantum holism), and a mystical aside — *"physical movements are waves that create frequencies which can unlock the universe."*

## In his coursework (the same mind, graded)
- **Doubt & epistemology:** his [[ToK Essay (Doubt)|ToK essay]] argues doubt drives knowledge through math (Cantor's infinities, non-Euclidean geometry, Descartes) and history (Ranke, the Annales School) — the formal version of *"nobody sees what they don't want to."*
- **Math/computation:** [[The Logistic Map]] (chaos, the Feigenbaum constant) and his Alan Turing essay (computation, the Turing test, AI) show the conceptual-math and AI curiosity that also drives [[Homework Hatch (startup)]].

## Adjacent
Heavy interest in **Gandhi / nonviolence / civil disobedience** ([[gandhi quotes (kirk me)]], realized in his [[Civil Disobedience (HL History essay)|HL History essay]]) — connecting his classwork on [[Civil Disobedience]] to real-world moral action.
